## 1. The Offer

**What they sell:** Estate planning legal services (wills, revocable and irrevocable living trusts, special needs trusts, pet trusts, farm succession planning, asset protection via LLCs/limited partnerships) plus probate administration, power of attorney, elder law, and Medicaid planning. Single-attorney firm — clients work directly with Brad Boyum.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:** Peace of mind that their assets will pass cleanly to the people they love — without losing wealth to probate delays, estate taxes, or family conflict. For probate clients: a guided hand through a legally and emotionally complex process at the worst time of their life.

**Price point & commitment:** Not publicly disclosed. Typical Nebraska estate planning legal fees range $1,000–$5,000+ depending on complexity (simple will vs. full trust package). Probate administration is usually billed hourly or as a percentage of estate value. Engagements are **project-based one-off** fees, not subscription. Probate cases can run 6–18 months in duration.

**Vertical:** ☑ Local & service — B2B/professional (legal services)

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:** Omaha-metro Nebraska families, typically 45–70 years old, with accumulated assets (home, retirement savings, farm, or small business) who have not yet formalized an estate plan. Also adult children or surviving spouses thrust into probate after losing a loved one.

**Secondary segments worth separating:**
- **Farmers / agricultural landowners** — specialized succession planning needs
- **Elderly individuals (65+)** — elder law, Medicaid planning, long-term care asset protection
- **Small business owners** — business succession, asset protection structures (LLCs, trusts)
- **Post-loss families** — navigating probate, often time-pressured and emotionally raw

**Pain points (in their own words):**
- "I don't want my kids to lose the house to probate costs"
- "We keep saying we'll do it but then life gets in the way"
- "Dad died and the bank froze his accounts — we can't pay his bills"
- "Is LegalZoom good enough or do we actually need a lawyer?"
- "I don't know if I need a will, a trust, or both"
- "What happens to the farm when we're gone?"

**Where leads come from:** Local SEO (Google searches for "Omaha estate planning attorney"), referrals from CPAs and financial advisors, word-of-mouth from past clients, potentially some social media (Facebook, LinkedIn presence noted).

**How they describe success:** "My family is taken care of no matter what happens to me." "My kids won't have to fight or go to court." "I know exactly what will happen to everything I've worked for."

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying client:** Days to 2–3 weeks. Inquiry → free consultation booked → consultation held → engagement letter signed.

**Free trial / first-session offer:** Yes — **free initial consultation**, no cost and no obligation to retain. This is a high-intent, in-person (or phone) meeting, not a drip-fed digital trial.

**Who/what closes the deal:** The free consultation itself closes most deals. Brad personally walks through the client's situation, identifies gaps, and proposes a plan. The close is relationship-driven, not self-serve. No proposal stage visible — it's consult → retainer.

**#1 objection before buying:** "Do I really need an attorney for this? Can't I just use an online will-maker?" — cost skepticism combined with perceived complexity of legal services vs. DIY alternatives. Secondary objection: procrastination ("we're not old enough to worry about this yet").

**Activation event:** The free consultation. The moment Brad shows a client their specific exposure — e.g., "without a trust, your home goes through probate and here's what that costs your family" — abstract fear becomes a concrete, solvable problem. That's when the client goes from curious to committed.

**How long / self-serve or assisted:** Assisted. Takes one 30–60 minute meeting. Not self-serve — the attorney's personalized analysis is the product preview.

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?** Not by default. Revenue is project-based. However, natural repeat touchpoints exist:
- Estate plan updates triggered by life events (marriage, divorce, birth, death, major asset purchase)
- Clients who did estate planning return when a family member dies and probate is needed
- Aging clients who need elder law / Medicaid planning added to existing plans

**Repeat cycle length:** 3–7 years for estate plan reviews; sooner if a major life event triggers it.

**What expansion looks like:** Upgrade from a simple will to a full revocable living trust package; add elder law / Medicaid planning as clients age into their 70s; farm succession planning for agricultural clients; business clients adding LLCs or limited partnerships for asset protection.

**Churn / drop-off problem:** 
- One-and-done clients who never update their documents — and then they become someone else's probate problem
- Leads who book a consultation but don't show up
- Families who used a different attorney for estate planning and go elsewhere for probate

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Likely primary goal:** More consultation bookings (conversions) — converting website visitors and existing email contacts into booked free consultations, and converting booked consultations into retained clients.

**Secondary goal:** Better retention — keeping past clients engaged so they return for plan updates and referrals.

**Target metric:** Consultation bookings / show-up rate / consultation-to-retained-client conversion rate. (Open rate is a vanity metric here.)

**Brand voice / tone:** Professional yet warm and compassionate. Family-first. Plain English — Brad explicitly avoids legal jargon. Honest about when a lawyer is and isn't needed (trust-building candor). Reassuring during emotionally difficult moments. Not flashy or salesy.

**Compliance constraints:**
- Nebraska State Bar attorney advertising rules: must not make false or misleading claims; must include firm name and address; cannot guarantee outcomes
- CAN-SPAM compliance required for all email
- No attorney-client relationship formed via email — must be disclaimed
- No HIPAA/GDPR concerns apparent for this practice area and geography

**Sending volume:** Unknown — start conservative (1–2x/month) until engagement baseline is established.
