{{first_name}}, has your life changed since we last talked?

It's been a while — and that's completely normal. Life moves fast, and estate planning rarely feels urgent until it suddenly does.

But here's the thing: the plan you put in place reflects your life as it was when you made it. If things have shifted since then, your documents may not reflect your wishes anymore — and your family may not be protected the way you intended.

Common reasons plans need updating

Any of these sound familiar?

✓

A new grandchild or child has joined the family

Beneficiary designations and guardianship provisions may need to be updated.
✓

You bought a home, an investment property, or a business

New assets need to be properly titled or funded into your plan — or they may go through probate anyway.
✓

A marriage, divorce, or separation in the family

These are the most common reasons people unintentionally leave assets to the wrong person.
✓

You've moved to a different state

Estate laws vary by state. A plan drafted in one state may not hold up — or may not reflect how another state handles assets.
✓

Someone named in your plan has passed away

A beneficiary or executor who is no longer living needs to be replaced — or your plan may stall when it's needed most.

A quick review — usually just a short conversation — is all it takes to confirm that everything still lines up with your wishes. You don't need to start from scratch, and there's no obligation.

If your plan still fits your life perfectly, we'll say so. If it needs a small adjustment, we'll show you exactly what that looks like. Either way, you'll leave knowing your family is covered.
