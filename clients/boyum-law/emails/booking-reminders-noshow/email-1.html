You don't need to bring anything formal. Just spend a minute or two thinking about these two things — it helps Brad tailor the review to your specific situation instead of covering ground you don't need:

1

What do you own?

A rough mental list is fine: your home, retirement accounts, a car, maybe a business or farmland. You don't need account numbers or appraisals — just a general picture.

Who would you want making decisions for you?

Think about who you trust most — a spouse, a child, a sibling. This is for your power of attorney and any beneficiary questions that come up. No final answers needed; just who comes to mind first.
