One thing worth knowing before you read it.

Most Nebraska families believe that having a will means their estate will pass quietly and cleanly to their children. It's a reasonable assumption — and it's often wrong.

In Nebraska, a will still goes through probate — the court-supervised process that validates it, pays creditors, and eventually distributes what's left to your heirs. That process is public, takes anywhere from six months to well over a year, and costs money in attorney and court fees that come directly out of the estate.

"We keep saying we'll do it but then life gets in the way — and then Dad died and the bank froze his accounts. We had no idea."

A common conversation we hear far too often.

The alternative — a properly funded revocable living trust — can let your assets pass directly to your family without court involvement. But whether a trust is right for your situation depends on what you own, how it's titled, and what you're trying to protect. That's not a one-size answer, which is why the guide walks through the real differences.

It's not meant to alarm you. Most situations are completely workable once you understand what you're dealing with.
