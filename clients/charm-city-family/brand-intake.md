## 1. The Offer

**What they sell:** A free hyperlocal Baltimore parenting community and blog. Content centers on curated kid-friendly event recommendations, family activity guides for Baltimore City, and community discussion. Secondary creative service: caricature art from photos (offered by page operator Stephanie Malanowski, April 2026 post).

**Core outcome the customer is buying:** "I know about fun, safe, inclusive things to do in Baltimore with my kids this weekend — and I feel part of a community that gets it."

**Price point & commitment:** Free to follow/join. No paid membership or subscription identified. Revenue likely comes from sponsorships, partnerships, or one-off creative services. Zero formal commitment required.

**Vertical:** ☑ Local & service / ☑ Education/nonprofit/membership hybrid (community blog)

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:** Baltimore City parents — predominantly moms with children ages 0–12 — who want vetted, up-to-date info on local family activities and a supportive peer community. Skews toward families who value diversity and inclusion ("celebrate our differences").

**Secondary segments:**
- New/expectant parents new to Baltimore or new to the parenting phase, seeking orientation to the city
- Established Baltimore families hungry for fresh weekend plans beyond their usual rotation
- Diverse families specifically looking for welcoming, affirming spaces

**Pain points:**
1. Hard to find trustworthy, current info on family-friendly Baltimore events in one place
2. Parenting can feel isolating; want a local community that "gets it"
3. Worry about whether venues/neighborhoods are safe and inclusive for their family
4. Screen-time guilt without easy access to enriching offline alternatives

**Lead sources today:** Organic Facebook reach, word-of-mouth, algorithmic group discovery. No SEO-driven or paid-ad presence confirmed.

**How they describe success:** "Found something awesome to do with my kids," "finally feel connected to other Baltimore parents," "discovered a hidden gem."

---

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → community member:** Immediate — clicking "Join Group" or "Follow Page" is the conversion; zero friction.

**Free trial / first-session offer:** The community itself is the free entry point. No paid tier or gated content identified. Caricature service appears to be a low-cost impulse offer ("special this weekend only").

**Close mechanism:** Organic social content → group join / page follow. No self-serve signup funnel, no booking flow, no sales call.

**#1 objection / hesitation:** "Is this still active?" The parked website and modest follower counts (150/361) undermine credibility for new visitors who Google the brand first. A live website and consistent posting cadence would remove this friction.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**Activation event:** A new follower sees a post about a local event or activity they didn't already know about, attends with their family, and comes back to comment or share their experience in the group — converting from passive follower to active community member.

**How long / self-serve?:** Immediate, self-serve — but fully dependent on the algorithm surfacing a relevant post at the right time. There is no triggered onboarding to guide a new follower to their first "aha."

*This becomes the anchor for Campaign 1: Welcome & Onboarding — a sequence that surfaces the best curated content to every new subscriber immediately, without waiting for the algorithm.*

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Recurring revenue:** None formally structured. Community stickiness depends on consistent posting and group activity.

**Renewal / repeat cycle:** Ongoing — there is no renewal event. Retention is driven purely by posting frequency and content quality.

**Expansion opportunities:**
- Sponsored posts / brand partnerships with Baltimore family-friendly businesses
- Paid email newsletter with premium event previews or local deals
- Ticketed family events or meetups
- Caricature / art commissions at family events
- Referral partnerships with local parenting services (camps, tutors, therapists)

**Churn / drop-off risk:** High, given current state. The parked website means any email/SEO traffic is lost. Low posting cadence on Facebook will cause the algorithm to bury the page. Audience has no email relationship — if Facebook reach drops, there is no fallback owned channel.

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal this quarter:** Build owned audience (email list) from existing social following; establish consistent value delivery via email to reduce dependency on Facebook algorithm.

**Target metric & baseline:** Email list size (baseline: 0). Secondary: Facebook group engagement rate (baseline: unknown, follower count 361).

**Brand voice / tone:** Casual, warm, community-first. Conversational and accessible — feels like a knowledgeable neighbor sharing a tip. Light use of emoji. Celebrates Baltimore's diversity and the everyday joys of family life. Not corporate, not aspirational-parenting-influencer. Sample language: "a safe place to celebrate our differences thru support & share joys of living in Baltimore. Gotta know to grow."

**Compliance constraints:** CAN-SPAM / standard email compliance. No HIPAA, financial, or legal constraints identified. Content regularly features children — follow standard child-safe and privacy-respectful practices.

**Sending volume the platform/list can support:** Starting from zero — any volume is appropriate initially. Plan for a small but engaged list (< 1,000) in the first 6 months.

---

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

Given that **everything is missing** and the brand has no email list, the priority is:

1. **Lead-Magnet Nurture (Campaign 2)** — Create a compelling Baltimore family resource (e.g. "The Ultimate Baltimore Family Bucket List" or "50 Free Things to Do in Baltimore with Kids") as a lead magnet to convert Facebook followers and organic search visitors into email subscribers. This is the list-building engine.

2. **Welcome & Onboarding (Campaign 1)** — A 3–5 email sequence triggered by lead-magnet signup that delivers immediate value (top curated content, community intro, what to expect), drives the Facebook group join, and establishes the email relationship.

3. **Value Newsletter (Campaign 5)** — A consistent weekly or bi-weekly email with curated Baltimore family events, activities, and tips. This is the retention engine that keeps the list warm and turns it into an asset that compounds over time.

*Campaign 3 (Trial/Consult Conversion) and Campaign 4 (Booking & Reminders) are deferred until a paid product or bookable service is formalized.*
