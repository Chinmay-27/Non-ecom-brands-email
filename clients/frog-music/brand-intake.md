# Brand Intake — Frog Music Press
**URL:** https://www.frogmusic.com  
**Founder/Creative Director:** Noel Jones, AAGO (Associate, American Guild of Organists)  
**Location:** Augusta, KY  
**Date researched:** 2026-06-04

---

## 1. The Offer

**What does the brand sell?**  
Frog Music Press publishes Catholic liturgical music resources: sheet music books (organ hymns, choirbooks), Gregorian chant guides (beginner through advanced), MIDI organ files, and the PTCO series (*Practical Tools for Church Organists*) for Rodgers and Roland digital organs. Materials are distributed as free PDF downloads, Amazon paperbacks (~$10–$25), and Kindle editions (~$5–$10). A 14-week *Free Chant School* video curriculum is also offered.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:**  
The ability to confidently lead Catholic liturgical music — reading Gregorian chant, registering a digital organ correctly, and building a parish music library — without expensive materials or formal institutional training.

**Price point & commitment:**  
Predominantly free (donation requested). One-time Amazon purchases for print/digital. No subscription, no retainer.

**Vertical:** ☑ Education/nonprofit/membership  
(Sacred music publisher; mission-driven, donation-supported)

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:**  
Amateur or part-time Catholic church organist — often a parish volunteer or low-paid staff musician — who owns or plays a Rodgers or Roland digital organ and needs affordable, liturgically accurate music resources.

**Secondary segments:**
- Catholic choir directors seeking choral repertoire and chant guidance
- Gregorian chant learners / adult beginners (Free Chant School audience)
- Catholic music teachers and parish catechists

**Problem they're in when they start looking:**  
"I need to play for Mass on Sunday and I don't know how to read chant notation" or "I just inherited the parish organ job and have no idea how to use a digital organ's stops." Acute need, low budget, no obvious professional mentor.

**Where leads come from today:**  
Primarily organic search (Google: "Gregorian chant for beginners," "Catholic organ music free"), Amazon book discovery, and word-of-mouth among Catholic musicians (Facebook group: *The Catholic Organist's Page*). The Leupold Foundation and sacred music networks also reference the press.

**How customers describe success:**  
"My choir actually sang the chant at Mass." "I finally understand stop registration." "I didn't have to spend anything — but I donated because it was so helpful."

---

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:**  
☑ Same day — free download is immediate; Amazon purchase is one click. No approval or consultation needed.

**Free trial / first-session offer:**  
Yes — essentially the entire catalog is available for free. The Free Chant School (14-week video series), Free Downloads page, Free Music Shelf, and PTCO sample pages all function as zero-friction trials.

**Who/what closes the deal:**  
Self-serve entirely. The donation page nudges free users to contribute. Amazon product pages handle paid sales. No salesperson or booked call involved.

**#1 objection before buying:**  
"Why donate or buy the print edition when everything is free?" The free model is a double-edged sword — low barrier to entry, but also undercuts the upsell. Secondary: "Is this parish-quality / USCCB-approved?" (authority concern from skeptical music directors or pastors).

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**Activation event:**  
The moment a musician successfully sings through a Gregorian chant piece (or plays an organ hymn) using the free resources and realizes they can lead their choir or congregation. Typically happens after completing the first 1–3 lessons of the Free Chant School, or playing through the opening pages of a PTCO organ book.

**How long / self-serve or assisted:**  
Self-serve. Can happen within hours of discovery if the musician dives into the Free Chant School. Assisted only by the video content itself (Noel Jones teaching on screen).

*This is the anchor for Campaign 1: Welcome & Onboarding — get the new subscriber to their first successful use of one resource within the first 7 days.*

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?**  
No formal subscription. Retention is community- and mission-driven: returning visitors download new publications as they are released, reference the MIDI library regularly, and buy additional volumes in a series.

**Renewal / repeat cycle:**  
No set cycle. New book releases (seasonal Quarterly volumes, new chant titles) are the natural re-engagement hooks. The Catholic Organist's Quarterly has at least 9+ volumes (Fall, Winter, Spring, Summer, Manuals-Only editions) offering a natural series-completion upsell.

**Expansion:**  
Buying additional volumes of *The Catholic Organist's Quarterly*, completing more of the chant beginner series, purchasing the PTCO organ books, or increasing donation amounts over time.

**Churn / drop-off problem:**  
Silent disengagement — free users download once and never return. No subscription means no renewal trigger or cancellation signal. The brand has no automated mechanism to detect or address this drop-off.

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal:** Better retention + more conversions (free → donor/buyer) — the site has strong top-of-funnel traffic from search and Amazon but loses visitors after the first download.

**Target metric & baseline:** Donation conversion rate from free downloaders (baseline unknown); repeat purchase rate on Amazon volumes (baseline unknown).

**Brand voice / tone:**  
Warm, scholarly, devotional. Educational without condescension — writes for beginners but holds an AAGO credential. Think: a trusted colleague in the choir loft, not a marketer. No aggressive sales language; price is almost never the lead. Sacred music as ministry.

**Compliance constraints:**  
CAN-SPAM / CASL standard. Religious/faith-based content — no HIPAA, finance, or legal constraints. Pastoral sensitivity required (volunteers, small parishes, clergy). Avoid pushy commercial tone.

**Sending volume:** Unknown — likely small list, low frequency.

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

Given the gaps in §7 and the brand's free-download / donation model:

1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — Onboard new free-download subscribers into a 5–7 email sequence: welcome → guide them to their first aha moment (first chant lesson or organ piece) → introduce the full catalog → soft donation ask with mission framing.
2. **Lead-Magnet Nurture** — A dedicated sequence for Free Chant School enrollees: week-by-week encouragement emails that mirror the 14-week curriculum, surface related books, and culminate in a series-completion offer (print edition or next chant book).
3. **Value Newsletter** — A regular (monthly or quarterly) email to the full list: new publications, MIDI library additions, liturgical calendar tie-ins (Advent, Lent, Easter), and one teaching tip per issue. Keeps the audience engaged between releases and reinforces Noel Jones's authority.
