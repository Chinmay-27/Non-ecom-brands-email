One thing to do today

Open Lesson 1 of the Free Chant School and sing through the first few lines. That's it. You don't need sheet music, an instrument, or a music degree. You need about ten minutes and a willingness to hum.

Gregorian chant notation looks intimidating at first glance — four lines, square notes, no time signature. Lesson 1 will show you why that actually makes it simpler than modern staff notation, and you'll sing your first phrase before the video ends. I've seen it happen for beginners of every background.

More of an organist than a singer?

If you came here for the organ resources, start with the opening pages of Practical Tools for Church Organists (PTCO) — the free PDF is already in your inbox, or you can grab it from the Downloads page. Sit at your instrument and play through the first two pieces today. You'll hear immediately whether your stop registration is working, and the notes in the margins will tell you why.
