## 1. The Offer

**What does the brand sell?**
Full-service creative and digital marketing agency. Services span:
- Content marketing (copywriting, social media, video/audio production, podcasting)
- Branding strategy and logo design
- Digital advertising (paid social/display)
- WordPress design, development, and consulting
- Digital signage content (menus, waiting areas, animations)
- Career development coaching (résumé writing, LinkedIn optimization, personal branding)
- Training programs (WordPress, social media, career readiness, workforce development)
- Speaking engagements

**Core outcome the customer is buying:**
To become *more visible and heard* — professionally represented online with consistent, quality content — without having to do the marketing themselves. For career clients: a compelling personal brand that opens doors.

**Price point & commitment:**
Custom pricing across all services; no public rates. Contact-to-quote model. Mix of one-off projects and ongoing retainer packages (digital marketing packages are mentioned explicitly). DesignRush lists minimum budget and hourly rate as "Inquire."

**Vertical:** B2B/professional services — creative & marketing agency

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:**
Small to medium-sized business owners and entrepreneurs in the Albany, NY region — typically 1–50 employees in service, retail, or professional industries — who need expert marketing but lack in-house bandwidth or skills.

**Secondary segments:**
- Executives and career changers needing personal branding for job search or promotion
- Nonprofits and organizations contracting workforce development / career readiness training
- Local businesses needing one-off creative work (digital signage, video, branding)

**What problem are they in when they start looking?**
- "I can't keep up with content and social media while running my business."
- "Nobody knows we exist — we're invisible online."
- "Our brand doesn't look or sound professional."
- "I'm job-hunting / pivoting and my LinkedIn and résumé need a complete overhaul."

**Where do leads come from?**
Primarily referrals and organic search; chamber of commerce and professional network memberships (Capital Region Chamber, Ulster County Chamber, Alignable); speaking engagements. No paid acquisition funnel is evident from the site.

**How do they describe success?**
"People are finding us online." "We look credible and professional." "I'm getting calls from the website." "I got the interview / promotion."

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:** Weeks (discovery call → proposal → signed agreement)

**Free trial / consult / first-session offer:**
Yes — Free Consultation offered prominently in the nav and throughout the site. No formal free trial or sample deliverable.

**Who/what closes the deal?**
Michael himself, after a consultation call. The contact form (name, email, message only) feeds a manual follow-up process — fully high-touch, no self-serve option.

**#1 objection before buying:**
Budget/pricing opacity — SMBs are cost-sensitive and the lack of any published pricing creates friction. Second objection: "Will an outside agency really understand my niche business?"

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**Activation event:**
Receiving the first polished deliverable — a finished video, a social media content calendar, a fully redesigned LinkedIn profile, or a brand strategy document — that the client couldn't have produced themselves and that immediately makes them look more professional.

**How long / self-serve or assisted?**
Assisted and relationship-driven. Typically 1–3 weeks from signed agreement to first deliverable, depending on service. No self-serve component exists.

*This is the anchor for Campaign 1: Welcome & Onboarding — set expectations, confirm scope, and drive to that first deliverable milestone.*

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?**
Partially. Digital marketing packages (social media management, content creation) imply monthly retainers. Training programs and one-off creative projects are non-recurring.

**Renewal / repeat cycle:**
Monthly for retainer clients; project-to-project for others. No visible annual contract structure.

**What does expansion look like?**
- Client starts with social media management → adds video production
- Client adds email marketing or digital ads to existing content retainer
- Training client returns for additional programs or referrals for workforce contracts

**Churn / drop-off problem:**
- SMBs cutting discretionary marketing spend in slow seasons
- Single-operator shop creates key-person dependency (if Michael is busy, client feels de-prioritized)
- No community, portal, or self-serve resource to maintain engagement between deliverables
