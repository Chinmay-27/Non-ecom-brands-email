This Month's Visibility Tip

The one thing most Albany business owners get wrong on social media

Hey {{first_name}}, a quick tip this month that takes about five minutes to fix — and genuinely changes what the algorithm does with your posts.

Here's the pattern I see over and over with small businesses across the Capital Region: they post regularly, they put real effort into it — and then the post dies a quiet death with six likes, four of which are from coworkers.

The problem usually isn't the content. It's the timing window.

The Fix

For the first 30–60 minutes after you hit "post," the platform is watching to see whether your audience engages. If you post and immediately close the app, you miss that window. Instead, stay in the comments. Reply to every reaction. Ask a follow-up question. The algorithm reads that activity as a signal that your content is worth showing to more people — and it responds accordingly.

Why this matters for local businesses specifically

In a market like Albany, you're not competing with national brands for eyeballs — you're competing with other local businesses for the attention of the same relatively small pool of potential customers. That's actually an advantage, because local engagement (comments from people in Delmar, shares from someone in Latham) carries extra weight on platforms like Facebook and Instagram. The algorithm treats it as proof that your content is relevant to the community.

You don't need a bigger budget or more posts. You need to be present for that first half-hour.

Try it this week — 5 minutes is enough

1. Schedule your next post for a time when you can actually stick around — not right before a meeting or at the end of the day when you're done.
2. Set a 30-minute timer when you publish. Keep the app open.
3. Reply to every comment — even a quick "thanks, glad this was helpful!" counts. Ask a question back to keep the thread alive.
4. Check your reach numbers 24 hours later. You'll see the difference.
