# Brand Intake — Jeenie Jolie

**URL:** https://jeeniejolie.com
**Date:** 2026-06-04
**Vertical:** E-commerce — organic/artisanal skincare & body care (wellness/beauty)

---

## 1. The Offer

**What they sell:** Physical organic beauty and body care products sold DTC via Shopify. Core SKUs: tallow-based anti-aging face cream, whipped body butters (seasonal/scented), body oils, African black soap, raw shea butter, sugar scrubs, natural loofahs, and product bundles. Lunar ceremony ritual kits are referenced in brand messaging but do not appear as a structured paid service/booking.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:** Glowing, nourished skin through clean, intentional beauty rituals — feeling confident and radiant while honoring a slower, more meaningful approach to self-care.

**Price point & commitment:** $12.99–$44.99 for individual products; ~$80.94 for bundles. One-time purchases only — no subscription or membership tier observed.

**Vertical:** ☑ Other: DTC e-commerce / organic beauty & wellness

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:** Women aged 25–45 who are spiritually inclined and wellness-conscious. They are transitioning away from synthetic mainstream beauty or deepening an existing clean-beauty practice. They resonate with moon rituals, intentional living, and slow self-care. They want their skincare to feel like ceremony, not chore.

**Secondary segments worth separating:**
- *Clean-beauty curious* — not yet fully committed to natural products; needs education on why tallow/organic formulas work
- *Spiritual community* — moon ritual practitioners, manifestation/astrology audience; respond to ritual framing over ingredient-first copy
- *Gift buyers* — purchasing artisanal bundles for wellness-oriented friends or family; seasonal buying behavior

**Problem they're in when they start looking:** Frustrated with ingredient lists on drugstore/prestige products (silicones, parabens, synthetic fragrance) and seeking something that feels intentional, clean, and aligned with their values.

**Where leads come from today:** Instagram, TikTok, Facebook (social commerce and organic content); YouTube; email signup driven by 30% off first-order incentive.

**How they describe success:** "My skin has never felt this soft," "I actually look forward to my skincare routine now," "It feels like a ritual, not just a product."

---

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → purchase:** Same day to a few days. Impulse-driven for lower-ticket items ($12–$25); light consideration cycle for the face cream ($44.99).

**Free trial / intro offer:** No free trial. Primary entry offer is **30% off first order** (code HAPPY30) gated behind email signup. A free guided ritual kit download is referenced as an opt-in incentive on the homepage.

**Who/what closes the deal:** Self-serve add-to-cart on Shopify. The email discount code is the primary close mechanism for first-time buyers.

**#1 objection before buying:** Skepticism about the efficacy and feel of tallow-based or fully natural formulas compared to familiar, heavily marketed drugstore or prestige alternatives. Price relative to a $10 drugstore body butter is also a friction point.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**Activation event:** First use of the product — experiencing the texture, scent, and skin-feel of a tallow face cream or whipped body butter and noticing their skin looks and feels noticeably better.

**How long / self-serve or assisted:** Self-serve; happens at home within days of delivery. No guided onboarding exists today.

**Implication for Campaign 1:** The welcome & onboarding sequence should bridge the gap between purchase and that first-use moment — set expectations for texture/scent, suggest a usage ritual, and invite the customer to notice the difference after 7 days.

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?** No subscription or membership model. Revenue depends entirely on repeat purchases.

**Repeat cycle:** Product-dependent. A body butter or scrub likely lasts 4–8 weeks with regular use; face cream 4–6 weeks. Natural replenishment windows exist but are not being triggered by any evident email flow.

**Expansion looks like:** Adding a second SKU after trying the first (e.g., face cream customer buys body butter); purchasing a bundle; seasonal/holiday gifting; trying new scented releases.

**Churn / drop-off problem:** One-and-done buyers who receive no post-purchase nurture, cross-sell, or replenishment nudge. Without a follow-up sequence, the brand is entirely dependent on the customer remembering to reorder.

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal (inferred):** Convert first-time buyers from the 30% discount offer AND increase repeat purchase rate — the two biggest revenue levers for a product-based brand with no subscription.

**Target metrics (inferred):** Click-to-purchase rate on nurture emails; 60-day repurchase rate; cart-abandonment recovery rate.

**Brand voice / tone:** Mystical, poetic, and aspirational. Blends wellness vocabulary ("vibration," "intention," "ritual," "glow from within") with empowering self-sovereignty language. Warm and introspective — never transactional or clinical. Copy should feel like a letter from a wise, sensual, spiritually grounded friend. Reference: homepage and about-page copy is the clearest voice guide.

**Compliance constraints:** Standard e-commerce (CAN-SPAM / GDPR). No HIPAA, financial, or legal-service constraints. Skincare copy must avoid drug claims per FDA cosmetic rules (do not say "treats," "cures," "heals [a condition]" — use "helps," "soothes," "nourishes" instead).

**Sending volume:** Unknown; to be confirmed against list size and platform plan.

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

Based on §7 gaps and the default priority order:

1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — Bridge the gap between order confirmation and first-use aha moment. 3–4 email sequence: brand story + ritual philosophy → what to expect from the formula → 7-day skin check-in invite → cross-sell introduction.
2. **Lead-Magnet Nurture** — Turn the 30% code into a real conversion sequence. 3 emails: deliver code + ritual context → "still thinking about it?" reminder with ingredient education → final urgency/expiry nudge.
3. **Repeat-Purchase / Expansion (Renewal & Expansion)** — 60-day replenishment + cross-sell flow triggered post-purchase. "Your body butter is almost out" → introduce a complementary SKU → bundle offer.
