Ritual I

Release with African black soap

Full moon energy is completion energy. The body holds what the mind forgets — tension, environmental residue, the quiet accumulation of a long month. Tonight, give your skin permission to release it.

African black soap — the real kind, raw and unrefined — has been used for generations across West Africa as a complete skin-care ceremony in a single bar. Plantain ash gives it its signature charcoal hue and draws impurities gently from the pores. Shea butter and coconut oil follow behind it, coating the skin so it never feels stripped.

Tonight's practice: Draw a warm bath or stand under warm steam. Work a small piece of black soap between wet palms until it lathers — it will be soft, not squeaky. Massage it into your face and body in slow, circular strokes. Breathe. This is not just cleansing. It is clearing space.
○

Ritual II

Feed your skin while it is still warm

There is a small window after cleansing — two to three minutes — when your pores are open and your skin is still holding warmth. This is when it drinks most deeply.

Raw shea butter is slow beauty in its truest form. Unrefined, it retains its full cargo of vitamins A, E, and F — the same fatty acids that strengthen your skin barrier and soften fine lines over time. Unlike most moisturizers that sit on top of the skin, raw shea is a lipid that the skin actually recognizes as its own. It absorbs at a cellular level, not a surface one.

Tonight's practice: Take a small amount of raw shea or your whipped body butter and warm it between your palms until it melts into oil. Apply to damp skin — starting at your feet and moving upward, always toward the heart. As you smooth it in, say (or simply think): I nourish what carries me.

Ingredient Spotlight

Raw Shea Butter

Sourced from the shea tree (Vitellaria paradoxa) and cold-processed to preserve every active compound. Works best layered onto damp skin — the water draws it deeper, and the fatty acids do the rest. You'll know your skin barrier is thriving when your skin feels plump in the morning without having applied anything the night before.

Ritual III

Let the moon work while you sleep

Your skin renews itself most actively between 10 PM and 2 AM. The full moon simply amplifies what is already happening — a heightened state of cellular turnover, increased blood flow, sensitivity to both stress and care.

This is the moment a nourishing face ritual becomes something more than skincare. A small amount of tallow face cream — which mirrors the lipid profile of your own sebum — applied to a clean, slightly damp face tells your skin it is safe to repair rather than defend.

Tonight's practice: After your body ritual, dim the lights. Warm a small amount of face cream between your ring fingers — always the gentlest — and press it softly into your face, working outward from the center. No dragging. Just presence. Set your phone face down. Let the moonlight be the last thing you see.

This Moon's Intention

"I release what no longer nourishes me. I receive what my body already knows."

Repeat it as you anoint your skin. Or write it. Or simply hold it as you drift to sleep under the full moon light.
