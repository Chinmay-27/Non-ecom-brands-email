**Completed:** 2026-06-03  
**Source:** Site crawl (strategiccfoadvisory.com) + Playwright browser session

## 1. The Offer

**What does the brand sell?**  
Two linked service lines sold together under one boutique advisory firm:
1. **CFO Advisory (fractional / part-time / interim)** — ongoing strategic financial leadership: FP&A, cash flow management, financial systems & controls, board reporting, strategic planning & budgeting.
2. **Investment Banking** — transaction advisory: buy/sell-side M&A, capital raising (debt & equity), company valuation & fairness opinions, due diligence / quality of earnings, strategic alternatives analysis.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:**  
"Increase company value by 2–3x" — the promise is not better spreadsheets, it's a materially higher exit multiple, a successful capital raise, or the operational discipline that makes both possible. Customers are buying the outcome: a clean, defensible, high-value business.

**Price point & commitment:**  
- Not published on the site.  
- The ROI calculator defaults suggest ~$4,375/month for a 20 hrs/month fractional CFO engagement at $5M ARR — implying a rough range of ~$3K–$15K+/month for CFO retainers depending on scope.  
- Investment banking likely structured as retainer + success fee (standard IB model).  
- Commitment: **ongoing retainer** (CFO) or **project-based** (IB transactions).

**Vertical:** B2B / Professional Services — Financial Advisory

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:**  
Founder-owned or PE-backed middle market companies with **$10M–$500M in annual revenue** that don't have (or can't justify) a full-time CFO but are navigating a pivotal moment: preparing for an exit, raising capital, managing a cash crisis, or trying to professionalize their financial operations.

**Secondary segments:**
- Growth-stage tech/SaaS companies (Series A/B) — need fundraising narrative + financial model
- Family/founder businesses preparing for a strategic sale — need financial cleanup + M&A advisory
- High-burn companies — need fractional CFO for runway extension / cash triage
- PE-backed portcos — need interim CFO to bridge between transactions

**Industries served:** Technology / SaaS, E-commerce, Business Services, Professional Services, Manufacturing & Distribution, Healthcare, Consumer & Retail, Financial Services & Fintech

**Pain points (in the prospect's words):**
- "We can't afford a $300K/year CFO but we're past the stage of DIY-ing our finances."
- "Investors keep passing — they don't trust our unit economics."
- "We want to sell but our books are a mess and we have no idea what we're worth."
- "We're growing 200% YoY and still running out of cash every quarter."
- "The board wants real reporting and we're sending them Excel files."

**How they describe success:** "Closed the deal." "Got the raise." "Finally know our numbers." "Sold for more than we thought possible."

**Where leads come from:** SEO / content (lead magnets), direct outreach, referrals (past client case studies suggest strong word-of-mouth potential), possibly LinkedIn given founder-audience fit.

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:** Weeks to months (typically 2–8 weeks for CFO retainer; longer for IB mandates which require more scoping and trust)

**Free trial / consult / demo:**  
Yes — **free 30-minute strategic consultation** offered as the primary CTA across the entire site. Positioned as delivering immediate value: "We'll review your situation and provide immediate insights on how to increase your company's value." No obligation; operates under NDA.

**Who / what closes the deal:**  
1. Prospect fills out the contact form (name, email, phone, company, industry, revenue range, primary need, situation description).  
2. Team responds within 24 hours.  
3. 30-minute free strategy call.  
4. Custom proposal with clear ROI projection.  
→ **A booked call is the gating action.** Deal is closed by a human (advisory relationship sale).

**#1 objection before buying:**  
*"Can I justify the monthly cost?"* — directly addressed on the homepage with an interactive ROI calculator showing projected annual value vs. monthly investment. At default settings ($5M ARR, 20 hrs/month), the calculator outputs 1,932% ROI. The entire page architecture is built around pre-empting this objection.

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**Activation event:** Delivery of the **60-day strategic roadmap** — the site explicitly promises "actionable strategic recommendations within 60 days to accelerate your path to value maximization." This is the first hard proof of value after engagement begins.

**Pre-engagement "aha":** The free consultation is engineered as a mini-aha — prospects receive on-the-spot valuation insights before paying anything. This is the conversion bridge between lead and client.

**Self-serve or assisted?** Fully assisted — relationship-driven, no self-serve component.

*(Campaign 1 anchor: the Welcome & Onboarding sequence should guide new clients toward a smooth kickoff and set expectations for the 60-day roadmap delivery.)*

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?**  
Partially — CFO advisory retainers are recurring monthly. Investment banking is project/transaction-based (one-time mandate + success fee).

**Renewal / repeat cycle:**  
CFO retainer renews monthly; natural review points at 90 days, 6 months, 12 months. IB mandates typically 3–12 months per transaction.

**What does expansion look like?**  
- CFO retainer client → add investment banking mandate (sell-side M&A or capital raise)  
- Increase advisory hours / scope as company scales  
- Referral to new portfolio companies (PE-backed clients)  
- Repeat engagement post-transaction (e.g., integration CFO support)

**Churn / drop-off problem:**  
The biggest churn risk is **natural project completion** — once the transaction closes, the capital raise lands, or the financial crisis is stabilized, clients may feel they no longer need ongoing advisory. Must maintain perceived strategic value between transactions. A secondary risk: if the 60-day roadmap doesn't feel differentiated, clients may question the retainer.

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal:**  
Most likely **more conversions / bookings** (free consultations → paying clients) + **more leads** (lead-magnet capture and nurture). Full lifecycle build is the right framing given nothing appears to exist.

**Brand voice / tone:**  
Authoritative and credibility-led. Leads with Wall Street pedigree (Deloitte Big 4, Wells Fargo IB, Ivy League MBA, CPA/CFA/Series 7-63-79) and hard outcome numbers (2-3x value, 40% burn reduction, 8.2x EBITDA exit). Professional but outcome-focused — more "here's what we achieved for clients like you" than "here's our process." Sits **formal-to-mid** on the formality scale. Emails should feel like they come from a trusted senior advisor, not a marketing department.

**Compliance constraints:**  
- Holds Series 7, 63, 79 securities licenses — investment banking advisory is FINRA-regulated.  
- Must **avoid unqualified performance guarantees and speculative ROI claims** in email (e.g., "we will increase your value 2-3x" as a promise vs. a range/expectation).  
- All client engagements run under NDA — email copy must reflect this discretion.  
- Standard CAN-SPAM (US) and applicable GDPR/CASL opt-out compliance required.

**Brand colors (from site):**
- Primary / CTA gold: `#C9921F`
- Dark navy (buttons, headings): `#0D1B2A`
- Background light gray: `#F5F7FA`
- Body text: `#1A2438`

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

Based on the full-lifecycle gap and the default priority from the playbook:

1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — greet new inquiries / consultation bookers, set expectations, prime for the 60-day roadmap promise, and reduce no-shows at the consultation call.
2. **Lead-Magnet Nurture** — 3 lead magnets are live with zero follow-up. Education sequence for each download type (segmented by magnet: cash flow vs. growth vs. fundraising) that warms leads toward booking the free consultation.
3. **Trial/Consult Conversion** — follow up with prospects who booked a consultation but haven't yet signed, or who attended but didn't convert. Address the cost/ROI objection with case studies and the ROI calculator story.
