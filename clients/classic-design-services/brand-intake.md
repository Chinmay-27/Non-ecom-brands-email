# Brand Intake — Classic Design Services

**URL:** https://www.classicdesignservices.com
**Date:** 2026-06-04
**Vertical:** Local & service / B2B professional

---

## 1. The Offer

**What they sell:** White-glove moving, receiving, warehouse storage, delivery, installation, crating, and fine art handling services. Three divisions: Classic Design Services (transit/logistics/storage for the design trade), CV Fine Art Services (art handling for museums/collectors/dealers), and FabriGuard (fabric, upholstery, and rug cleaning/protection).

**Core outcome the customer is buying:** Peace of mind that valuable, irreplaceable possessions — fine furnishings, antiques, fine art — arrive undamaged, on time, and perfectly installed. For interior designers specifically: the freedom to focus on creative work while a trusted partner handles all logistics.

**Price point & commitment:** Custom quotes only; no published pricing. White-glove positioning suggests a mid-to-high price point versus standard movers. Engagements are per-project but evolve into ongoing relationships with trade clients. No subscription or membership model.

**Vertical:** ☑ Local & service  ☑ B2B/professional (dual — serves both trade professionals and residential homeowners)

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary customer / ICP:** Interior designers and design trade professionals in the Atlanta, GA metro area (and surrounding region for storage/delivery) who need a single, accountable logistics partner for the end-to-end handling of client furnishings and fine art.

**Secondary segments worth separating:**
1. Residential homeowners — premium household moves, disaster recovery, furniture repair
2. Builders & remodelers — storage and protection during construction/renovation
3. Manufacturers & showrooms — nationwide delivery, overflow warehousing
4. Museums & galleries — fine art installation, specialized crating, valuation
5. Film & entertainment — on-set item management, prop storage, post-production restoration

**Problems they're in when they start looking:**
- A past move or installation went wrong (damaged piece, late delivery)
- Facing a high-stakes client project with no reliable logistics partner
- Outgrowing current storage or vendor arrangements
- Relocating a household or office and needing specialist care

**Where leads come from:** Referrals from interior designers (explicitly mentioned in testimonials), word-of-mouth in the design trade community, SEO/search (ranked well for Atlanta moving/logistics), and industry associations (NARI Atlanta member).

**How customers describe success:** "Everything arrived perfectly." "My designer recommended them — they were amazing." Reliability and flawless execution are the success language.

---

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:** Days for residential moves; weeks for first-time trade engagements; relationship deepens over months of repeat project work.

**Free trial / consult / first offer:** No trial. Free estimates delivered via email or phone after the prospect fills out the quote request form. This is the primary conversion event.

**Who/what closes the deal:** A customer service representative builds and delivers the estimate; conversion happens through direct phone/email follow-up. Self-serve is not available — all jobs require a quote.

**#1 objection before buying:** Cost uncertainty and skepticism that a premium specialist is worth more than a standard moving company — especially from residential prospects or first-time trade clients unfamiliar with the brand.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**Activation event:** The first flawless project completion — when the client (designer or homeowner) sees their high-value items received, stored, delivered, and installed without a single damage incident or scheduling failure. For designers, this also means their client had a great experience they can attribute to the designer's choice of vendor.

**How long does it take:** Same day to several weeks depending on project scope (a single delivery is immediate; a full design-installation project may span weeks).

**Self-serve or assisted:** Fully assisted — a dedicated CDS representative manages the entire project.

*This flawless first job is the anchor for Campaign 1: Welcome & Onboarding.*

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?** Not contractually, but operationally yes — interior designers and trade clients use CDS repeatedly across multiple client projects. Manufacturers and showrooms maintain ongoing warehousing arrangements.

**Renewal / repeat cycle:** Project-by-project, which for active designers may mean monthly or quarterly engagements. No formal renewal date.

**Expansion path:** Receiving → storage → delivery/installation → fine art handling → in-house furniture repair → FabriGuard fabric protection/cleaning. Each service is an upsell opportunity.

**Churn / drop-off risk:** High sensitivity to a single bad experience — a damaged piece or missed deadline can permanently end a designer relationship. Word-of-mouth in the design community amplifies both good and bad experiences. Inactivity (no job in 6–12 months) likely signals the client found an alternative vendor.

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

Based on the gaps in §7 and the full-lifecycle picture above:

1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — triggered by first estimate request or first completed job; sets expectations, builds trust, introduces all three divisions, and makes the case for an ongoing partnership relationship with design-trade clients.
2. **Trial/Consult Conversion** — structured 3–5 email follow-up sequence after an estimate is sent; addresses the cost-vs-value objection head-on, surfaces social proof (designer testimonials), and creates urgency around booking before the move date.
3. **Referral / Advocacy** — sent 1–2 weeks after a successful job completion; capitalizes on the designer referral channel that is already their #1 lead source; provides a simple, frictionless way for happy clients to refer colleagues in the design trade.

*(If a nurture sequence is needed before booking, Campaign 2 can be replaced with a Lead-Magnet Nurture once a lead magnet — e.g., a "10-point checklist for protecting fine furnishings during a client installation" — is created.)*
