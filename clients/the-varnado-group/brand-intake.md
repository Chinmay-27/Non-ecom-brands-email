## 1. The Offer

**What they sell:** Employee benefits consulting and brokerage for small businesses. Services include voluntary benefits such as short- and long-term disability, cancer coverage, hospital indemnity, group health insurance, dental, and vision — plus "much more" per the site.

**Core outcome:** Small businesses get affordable, comprehensive benefits packages that let them compete with large employers for talent — "leveling the playing field."

**Price point & commitment:** No pricing is published. The model is almost certainly commission-based (carrier pays broker), meaning the consulting is cost-neutral to the employer client. Commitment is typically annual — tied to open enrollment and plan renewal cycles.

**Vertical:** B2B / Professional Services (insurance & benefits brokerage)

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:** Small business owners (roughly 1–50 employees) across industries in the greater Baton Rouge, LA area who currently offer few or no employee benefits and are feeling the competitive pressure around talent.

**Secondary segments:**
- Small businesses with basic benefits (e.g., health only) looking to layer in voluntary/supplemental products
- HR managers or office managers at growing SMBs managing a benefits refresh or first-time enrollment

**Problem when they start looking:**
- Losing employees to larger companies that offer better benefits
- Struggling to attract candidates who ask about benefits during hiring
- Overwhelmed by the complexity, compliance, and cost perception of group benefits

**Lead sources today:** Primarily referrals and direct outreach (personal network of John Varnado); LinkedIn presence; no visible paid ad or SEO content strategy.

**How they describe success:** "My team finally has real benefits." / "I can compete with the big companies now." / "My employees feel taken care of."

---

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time to close:** Weeks to months — B2B relationship sale requiring a discovery/needs assessment, proposal, and enrollment window alignment.

**Free trial / entry offer:** Unknown from public site; very likely a free consultation or needs assessment call is the de facto entry point.

**Close mechanism:** Inbound inquiry via contact form or phone → personal consultation with John Varnado → custom proposal → enrollment.

**#1 objection:** "We're too small / can't afford it." Small business owners assume comprehensive benefits are out of reach financially or administratively.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**Activation event:** First enrollment completed — employees receive their benefits cards/welcome kits and actually use their coverage. The business owner sees the impact on morale and hiring conversations.

**Self-serve or assisted:** Fully assisted — John Varnado and his team guide the entire process.

**Implication for Campaign 1:** Onboarding emails should bridge the gap from "signed agreement" to "enrollment complete," reinforcing the decision, setting expectations for the process, and celebrating the milestone when benefits go live.

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Recurring?** Yes — the annual open enrollment/renewal cycle is a built-in recurring engagement point. Benefits plans are re-evaluated and renewed each year.

**Renewal cycle:** Annual (tied to plan year and open enrollment windows, typically Oct–Jan for calendar-year plans).

**Expansion looks like:**
- Adding voluntary benefit lines to an existing health-only plan (dental, vision, disability, cancer)
- Increasing covered headcount as the client business grows
- Upgrading plan tiers as the client can afford more

**Churn / drop-off risk:** Clients may switch brokers at renewal if they feel underserved or receive a competitive bid. Relationship quality and proactive annual check-ins are the primary retention levers.

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Likely primary goal:** More conversions/bookings (turning inquiries into enrolled clients) and better retention (keeping existing clients through renewal).

**Target metrics:** Consultation-to-client conversion rate; client renewal rate; referral count (estimated baselines unknown).

**Brand voice / tone:** Professional but warm and accessible. Positions as a trusted advisor who "gets it" for the small business owner. Not insurance-jargon-heavy — educational and straightforward. The mission-driven framing ("leveling the playing field") suggests values-led communication.

**Compliance constraints:** Louisiana Department of Insurance licensing disclosures required in marketing. CAN-SPAM compliance. ACA/ERISA disclosure care in benefit-related communications. Handles benefit plan data — avoid PHI-adjacent language; this is not a HIPAA covered entity but caution is warranted.

---

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

Based on the complete gap in all backbone campaigns and the B2B professional services cycle:

1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — immediately sets expectations post-inquiry/post-enrollment, delivers value, reduces drop-off
2. **Trial/Consult Conversion** — nurtures leads from initial contact form submission through to a booked consultation (the critical bridge since no lead magnet exists)
3. **Renewal, Anniversary & Expansion** — captures the annual enrollment window that is currently completely unaddressed and represents the highest lifetime value lever
