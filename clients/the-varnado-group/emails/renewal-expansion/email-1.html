Why Starting Now Matters

Waiting until the last minute costs you options

Most employers start thinking about open enrollment when the deadline is already close. That's when choices get made under pressure — limited plan options, no time to compare, and employees who feel rushed through the process.

Starting 90 days out means we can take a real look at what's working, compare any new plan offerings your team might benefit from, and give employees the lead time they need to make informed decisions.

As Your Business Grows

Coverage lines worth adding to your renewal

Renewal season is the natural time to ask: what do your employees still need that you're not offering? These voluntary benefit lines are popular additions for growing teams — and since they're carrier-funded, the cost to add them is often lower than most owners expect.

Dental

One of the most-requested benefits by employees. Covers preventive care and major work — and it shows up in every job comparison.

Vision

Low premium, high perceived value. Glasses and eye exams are a benefit employees actually use and notice on the benefits summary sheet.

Disability (Short & Long-Term)

Protects employee income when illness or injury takes them out of work. Reduces the pressure on you as an employer when the unexpected happens.

Cancer Coverage

Cash benefits directly to employees facing a cancer diagnosis — for treatment, travel, or lost income. Often the most meaningful payout employees ever receive.

We'll walk through which of these fit your team's size and situation during the renewal review — no pressure to add everything, just a look at what's available.
