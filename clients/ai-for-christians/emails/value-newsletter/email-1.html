The Weekly Prompt

Your devotion time, supercharged — in 60 seconds

Most believers open their Bible in the morning, read a few verses, feel something — and then life hits and it's gone by noon.

AI won't replace that sacred moment. But it can help you go ten layers deeper in the same fifteen minutes — pulling original language context, cross-references, and commentary the way a seminary professor would, on demand, for free.

Here's the exact prompt I use with Logos Bible Study AI (free on Poe). Copy it, drop in your passage, and watch your study open up:

Copy-Paste Prompt

I'm studying [PASTE YOUR VERSE OR PASSAGE HERE]. Please help me understand: 1. The original Greek or Hebrew meaning of any key words 2. The cultural and historical context a first-century reader would have known 3. Three cross-references that illuminate this passage 4. One practical application I can carry into today Keep the tone devotional, not academic.

Spirit-Check

The mistake believers make when they let AI "pray" for them

This week's preheader promised I'd address it — so here it is, plainly:

Letting AI generate your prayers and then reading them back as if they're yours is spiritual ventriloquism. You're not praying — you're performing prayer. There's a difference.

Now, here's what AI can do that's genuinely helpful in prayer life: it can surface Scripture you didn't know existed, remind you of God's character when you're too worn down to think straight, and help you structure intercession for situations you don't have words for yet — as a starting point your own spirit then takes over.

The rule: AI is the research assistant. You are still the one who prays.

"The Spirit helps us in our weakness. We do not know what we ought to pray for, but the Spirit himself intercedes for us through wordless groans."

— ROMANS 8:26 (NIV)
 

Build the Habit

Your 5-minute Digital Discipleship routine

Every week this section gives you one small habit to stack onto what you're already doing. This week's is simple:

1

Open your passage first. Read it. Sit with it. Don't touch AI yet.
2

Write one question. Something honest — something you genuinely don't know or understand about what you just read.
3

Now use this week's prompt. Let Logos answer your question — then take the best of what it gives you back to prayer.
4

Write down one sentence. The one thing that landed. Tuck it somewhere you'll see it today.
