## 1. The Offer

**What does the brand sell?**  
Mortgage brokerage services. Two primary loan programs:

1. **No-Equity / Debt Consolidation / Home Improvement** — $15K–$500K. No appraisal required in most cases, easy qualifying, no upfront costs, no equity required, interest may be tax-deductible.
2. **Purchase or Refinance** — $50K–$4M. Covers primary, rental, and second homes. E-Z Qualifier and no-income/no-asset-verification options available. Impaired credit and bankruptcy OK. Self-employed and fixed-income borrowers welcome.

Additional uses: cash-out for any reason (car, vacation, college), new construction, equity lines of credit.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:**  
Lower monthly payments, debt relief, and access to home equity — *saving money rather than overpaying their lender.* The emotional outcome is financial breathing room and the feeling that someone finally said "yes."

**Price point & commitment:**  
Commission/origination-based. No upfront application fees, no out-of-pocket cost to explore options. One-time transaction per loan. Repeat business comes via future refinances (rate-cycle dependent) or referrals.

**Vertical:** ☑ Local & service (mortgage brokerage, B2C)

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:**  
Homeowners aged roughly 30–60 who are cash-strapped, over-leveraged, or carrying high-interest consumer debt. Especially those who have been turned down by traditional banks due to low credit scores, no equity, or non-standard income.

**Secondary segments worth separating:**
- Debt-burdened homeowners seeking consolidation + cash-out relief
- Homeowners looking to lower their rate or monthly payment via refi
- Home improvement seekers (no equity required)
- First-time homebuyers needing flexible qualification
- Self-employed / irregular-income borrowers
- Borrowers recovering from bad credit or bankruptcy

**Problem they're in when they start looking:**  
- "I'm drowning in credit card payments and my bank turned me down."
- "My house needs work but I have no savings and no equity."
- "I'm paying too much every month and I don't know if there's a better option."

**Where do leads come from?**  
Primarily inbound phone calls and direct web traffic. Older site suggests organic/direct search and word-of-mouth referrals. Newsletter signup on homepage. A "Tell a Friend" referral page exists.

**How do they describe success?**  
*"You lowered our interest rate, paid off our credit card debts, gave us some cash, and left us with spending money. We ended up saving $475 a month. It's like getting a raise without getting a raise."* — Real testimonial from John & Lisa, Irvine CA.

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:** Days to a few weeks (consultation → application → underwriting → closing).

**Free trial / consult / first offer:**  
YES — free, no-obligation phone consultation AND a free Benefit Analysis that shows the borrower exactly how much they will save before any commitment. *"There is NO COST to find out how much you'll save and never any obligation to continue."*

**Who/what closes the deal?**  
An inbound phone call or online loan application, followed by personal handling by Michael J. Dorff or a loan counselor. No self-serve close — the call IS the close mechanism.

**#1 objection before buying:**  
*"My credit is bad / I have no equity / I've already been turned down by my bank."* The brand pre-handles this directly in copy: "Impaired or Bad Credit O.K. (including Bankruptcy)" and "No Equity Required."

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**Activation event:**  
The **free Benefit Analysis** — the moment the borrower sees the exact dollar amount they will save per month. This converts a skeptical, burned lead into a believer and committed applicant.

**How long does it take?**  
Assisted (via phone or online form). Can happen in the initial consultation call.

*This is the anchor of Campaign 1: Welcome & Onboarding — every email should drive toward booking the free consultation and getting the Benefit Analysis done.*

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?**  
No formal subscription or retainer. Revenue is transaction-based.

**Repeat cycle:**  
Refinances are rate-cycle driven (typically every 3–7 years when rates drop meaningfully). Michael Dorff signs every communication personally — relationship-based retention.

**Expansion looks like:**  
- Refinancing when market rates drop
- Adding a home equity line after initial purchase loan
- "Tell a Friend" referral bringing in household contacts
- Future construction or improvement loans as equity builds

**Churn / drop-off risk:**  
- Lead shops multiple brokers simultaneously; competitor poaches between inquiry and closing
- Long processing time increases mid-funnel drop-off
- No visible post-close nurture to maintain relationship for future refinances

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal:**  
Most likely **more conversions/bookings** (consultation-to-application rate) and **reactivation** of cold leads/past clients. The funnel has the top (newsletter, phone calls) and the bottom (loan app) but the middle is a gap.

**Target metrics:**  
- Consultation booking rate from email
- Application completion rate after consultation
- Referral volume

**Brand voice / tone:**  
Warm, direct, benefit-first, personal. Signed by Michael J. Dorff — one-person voice of authority and trust. Uses plain language; emphasizes FREE, NO COST, GUARANTEED in bold/caps. Speaks to people who feel judged or turned down by institutions. Reassuring, not salesy.

**Compliance constraints:**  
- RESPA, TILA, Reg Z disclosure requirements on mortgage communications
- Equal Housing Opportunity lender (logo on site)
- California DRE licensed broker
- NMLS disclosure required in emails (NMLS# unknown — verify before sending)
- CAN-SPAM compliance
- Email must not guarantee specific rates; must include required APR/terms disclosures if rates are mentioned

**Sending volume:** Unknown — start conservatively given list likely built over many years with variable engagement.

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

Based on the gaps in §7 and the default priority order:

1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — Drive every new subscriber/inquiry to the free consultation and Benefit Analysis. The aha moment is showing them their exact savings number; email must get them on the phone or to the application.
2. **Trial/Consult Conversion** — Follow-up sequence for leads who completed the free consult but haven't submitted an application. Overcome the "my credit is too bad" / "I have no equity" objection with proof (testimonials, program specifics).
3. **Lead-Magnet Nurture** — Drip sequence for newsletter subscribers who haven't yet booked a consultation. Educational content (how refinancing works, signs you're overpaying) that builds trust and creates urgency to get the free Benefit Analysis.
