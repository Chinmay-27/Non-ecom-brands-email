## 1. The Offer

**What they sell:** Curated gift and home decor boutique — candles, jewelry, home accessories, fine barware, gourmet food, baby gifts, kitchenware, personal care, fashion accessories, and collegiate items. Deliberately stocks American artisan brands, with 8+ Georgia-based brands and 13+ Southern-based brands on the floor.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:** A genuinely thoughtful, unique gift or home accent — something that feels personal and intentional rather than grabbed from a big-box aisle. The store's curatorial work takes the decision-making burden off the buyer.

**Price point & commitment:** One-off retail purchases. No subscription, membership, or retainer discovered. Estimated basket range: $20–$200. In-store and online.

**Vertical:** ☑ Local & service

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:** Atlanta-area adults, primarily women aged 30–65, who shop for gifts on life occasions (housewarmings, Mother's Day, Christmas, hostess, showers, graduations) and who value supporting local/American artisan makers over mass retail.

**Secondary segments worth separating:**
- **Gift-givers:** Occasion-triggered shoppers buying for others — high urgency, high AOV potential, repeat seasonally
- **Home nesters:** Shoppers refreshing or styling their own space — more deliberate, higher-ticket items, interested in home decor storytelling
- **Local/conscious buyers:** Customers who lead with values — actively seek Georgia-made, Southern-made, American artisan; ideal brand ambassadors

**Problem they're in when they start looking:** "I need a gift for [occasion] that isn't generic and that shows I actually put thought into it — and I don't want to spend an hour searching Amazon."

**Where leads come from today:** Walk-in traffic (Peachtree Battle Shopping Center foot traffic), Instagram (@randolphs_finegifts, ~1,900 followers), Facebook, word-of-mouth/referral, email newsletter list.

**How customers describe success:** "I found the perfect thing," "The staff knew exactly what to suggest," "I always find something I didn't know I needed," "I love that they support local makers."

---

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → purchase:** Same day — this is impulse and occasion-driven retail. The decision is made in-store or within a single browse session online.

**Free trial / consult / first-session offer:** None discovered. The store itself is the free browse — walk in and staff assists. Gift wrapping is done in-store while the customer keeps shopping (a notable service touch).

**Who/what closes the deal:** Knowledgeable in-store staff + the physical product experience. Online: the curated catalog and product storytelling. No sales call or proposal process.

**#1 objection before buying:** Price ("can I get something similar cheaper elsewhere?") and occasion fit ("do they have the right thing for this specific person/event?").

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**The activation event:** Walking into the store (or opening a newsletter) and immediately spotting something that solves the gift occasion they've been dreading — a product they wouldn't have found on their own, presented with context about its maker.

**How long / self-serve vs. assisted:** Instant in-store with staff. Online it depends on content quality and how well the product photography and copy convey the artisan story.

*This is the anchor for Campaign 1: Welcome & Onboarding — emails should reproduce the "walking in and discovering" feeling: here's what's new, here's the story behind it, here's the occasion it's perfect for.*

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?** No formal subscription or membership. Repeat purchasing is seasonal and occasion-driven.

**Repeat cycle:** Major seasonal peaks: Christmas/holiday (Nov–Dec), Mother's Day (Apr–May), Valentine's Day (Jan–Feb), graduation season (Apr–Jun), back-to-school (Jul–Aug). Secondary: housewarmings, hostess gifts year-round.

**What "expansion" looks like:** Moving from single-item gifts to curated gift baskets; buying for multiple occasions per season; graduating to higher-ticket home decor items; becoming a reliable "go-to" for all gift occasions throughout the year.

**Churn / drop-off problem:** One-occasion buyers who never receive a reason to return. Lapsed newsletter subscribers who were attracted by a single sale and never engaged with the brand story. Out-of-Atlanta online customers with no physical proximity.

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal (inferred):** More conversions/repeat bookings — turning one-occasion buyers into year-round gift-occasion customers; growing email list into a retention channel, not just a broadcast list.

**Target metric:** Email-driven repeat purchase rate; click-through on seasonal campaign emails; new subscriber → first purchase conversion.

**Brand voice / tone:** Warm, sophisticated, and locally proud. Conversational but tasteful. Celebrates the makers and the stories behind products. Speaks to the occasion and the recipient, not just the product specs. Never discounty or high-pressure. Reads like a knowledgeable friend who loves great shops.

**Compliance:** Standard CAN-SPAM retail. No HIPAA, finance, legal, or GDPR-specific constraints identified.

**Sending volume:** Unknown — confirm with ESP on kickoff.

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

Based on the gaps in §7 and the retail lifecycle (no trial/booking motion; newsletter exists but lacks structure):

1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — greet new subscribers, tell the brand story (artisan focus, local roots), introduce product categories by occasion, and drive first purchase with editorial content (not a discount)
2. **Value Newsletter (structured)** — rebuild the newsletter with a repeatable format: maker story + occasion spotlight + new arrivals + seasonal CTA; this becomes the retention engine
3. **Seasonal Campaign Series** — pre-built email pushes timed to Mother's Day, holiday, and Valentine's Day buying windows with curated gift guides by recipient type

*(Win-Back / Re-Engagement is the logical 4th once the list has been active long enough to generate lapsed segments.)*
