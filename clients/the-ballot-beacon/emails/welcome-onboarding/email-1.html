The Social Security Fairness Act

What passed:

Congress repealed two long-standing rules — the Windfall Elimination Provision (WEP) and the Government Pension Offset (GPO) — that had reduced Social Security benefits for roughly 3.2 million public-sector workers, including teachers, firefighters, and police officers.

Why it matters:

These offsets were created in the 1980s to prevent "double-dipping" — but critics argued they unfairly penalized workers who split careers between government jobs and private-sector work. The debate was about fairness, not party lines.

How it hits your wallet:

Affected retirees are now receiving retroactive back pay averaging $6,700, plus higher ongoing monthly benefits — some seeing increases of $360–$1,190 per month. If you or a family member worked in a public-sector job and have a government pension, check your Social Security statement.
