# Brand Intake — The Ballot Beacon

**URL:** https://www.theballotbeacon.com
**Date:** 2026-06-04
**Vertical:** Education / Nonprofit / Membership — civic news newsletter

---

## 1. The Offer

**What they sell:** A subscriber-supported political newsletter that translates new state and federal laws into plain English. Published weekly to bi-weekly; each issue is a 3-9 minute read.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:** Know how laws that just passed will affect your wallet, rights, and daily life — without needing a law degree or sitting through partisan commentary.

**Price point & commitment:**
- Free tier: "Unlock free access" — no paywall at entry, no credit card required.
- Paid tier: Subscriber-supported upgrade (specific pricing not publicly listed; gated behind signup flow).
- Commitment: Recurring subscription; self-serve cancel.

**Vertical:** ☑ Education/nonprofit/membership (civic journalism / independent newsletter)

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:** Civically engaged adults (approx. 30-60) who feel shut out by both legalese and partisan media — they want to stay informed but distrust cable news and don't have time to read 40-page bills. Not attorneys. Often in states with active legislative calendars (TX, GA, CA, NY confirmed in content).

**Secondary segments:**
- Political moderates and independents who distrust both left and right outlets
- Local-news-desert readers with no credible civic journalism nearby
- Working professionals who want a 5-minute brief, not a 30-minute deep dive
- State-specific readers who want laws filtered to their state

**Problem they're in when they start looking:** "A new law just passed and I have no idea how it affects me — every outlet is spinning it and I can't find a straight answer."

**Lead sources:** Organic search, social (Instagram @ballotbeacon_), word-of-mouth, archive/SEO traffic.

**How they describe success:** "I finally understand what's actually in that law" / "I can explain this to my family without getting into politics."

---

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:** Same day — low-friction subscription with free entry.

**Free trial / first offer:** Yes — free newsletter access is the entry point. No paywall, no credit card. Conversion to paid happens after the reader experiences value inside the newsletter.

**What closes the deal:** Self-serve upgrade prompt (inside newsletter or on upgrade page). No sales call or proposal required.

**#1 objection before buying:** "This is just another biased political newsletter dressed up as neutral." Trust and credibility around the nonpartisan claim is the core barrier — especially in today's polarized climate.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**Activation event:** Opening the first issue and reading a clear, jargon-free explanation of a recently passed law that is directly relevant to the subscriber's state or life situation.

**How long it takes:** Immediate upon first email delivery (3-9 minute read).

**Self-serve or assisted:** Fully self-serve.

**Campaign implication:** The Welcome & Onboarding sequence must pre-sell the "aha" before the first issue lands — set the expectation ("your first issue arrives [day], here's what to look for"), then follow up after issue #1 to confirm they got value and prompt the upgrade.

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Recurring revenue:** Yes — newsletter subscription, recurring billing.

**Renewal / repeat cycle:** Implicit renewal (subscription auto-renews); no fixed annual event unless they introduce a yearly plan.

**Expansion:** Upgrade from free reader → paid subscriber. Potential future expansion: state-specific paid tiers, premium deep-dives, or community access.

**Churn / drop-off problem:**
- Disengagement during slow legislative periods (no new laws → no reason to open)
- "Didn't feel like it covered my state" — relevance gap for non-featured states
- Subscribers who signed up during a viral moment but never built a reading habit
- Perceived slant by either side of the political spectrum eroding the nonpartisan brand promise

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal:** ☑ More conversions (free → paid) and better retention; full lifecycle build is the opportunity.

**Target metric:** Paid subscriber conversion rate from free list; paid subscriber retention rate. Baseline unknown.

**Brand voice / tone:** Plain-spoken, direct, credible, nonpartisan. Conversational but not casual — like a trusted friend who happens to have read the bill for you. Core mantra: *"No Slant. No Spin. No BS."* Copy must never imply partisan lean in either direction.

**Compliance constraints:**
- CAN-SPAM: Yes — standard newsletter compliance required.
- Political content: Must never imply partisan endorsement or political affiliation. Nonpartisan brand identity is core to trust — any copy that reads as left or right slanted is a brand-killing risk.
- No HIPAA, financial advice, or legal-professional-services constraints.

**Sending volume:** Unknown — determined by beehiiv plan tier and list size.

---

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

Based on gaps above and the default priority order:

1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — Set the expectation, build the reading habit before the first issue, confirm value after issue #1, and prime for upgrade.
2. **Free-to-Paid Conversion** (Trial/Consult Conversion analog) — Nurture free subscribers toward paid with social proof, law-impact stories, and a clear upgrade CTA.
3. **Win-Back / Re-Engagement** — Reactivate lapsed free subscribers who haven't opened in 60-90 days before they churn off the list entirely.
