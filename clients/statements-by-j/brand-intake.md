# Brand Intake — Statements by J

**URL:** https://www.statementsbyj.com
**Date researched:** 2026-06-04
**Vertical:** Home furnishings / e-commerce retail (with physical showroom)

---

## 1. The Offer

**What they sell:** Modern, designer-aesthetic furniture sold primarily online with nationwide shipping from their Commerce, CA warehouse. Product lines span kitchen & dining sets, living room sofas and accent chairs, bedroom pieces, office furniture, entryway consoles, and small-space solutions. A physical showroom in Commerce (Los Angeles) is available by appointment.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:** A stylish, designer-quality home — without the markups of traditional luxury furniture stores or the gatekeeping of "stuffy showrooms."

**Price point & commitment:** One-off product purchases (no subscription or retainer). Mid-tier luxury positioning — higher than mass-market (IKEA, Wayfair) but significantly below boutique designer retail. Specific SKU pricing not publicly listed; customers browse and checkout per item.

**Vertical:** ☑ Other: Home furnishings e-commerce with local showroom (Los Angeles)

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary customer / ICP:** Style-conscious homeowners aged roughly 25–55 in major US metros (Los Angeles, New York, Miami, Chicago, Dallas, etc.) who want modern, designer-look furniture at an accessible price. They follow interior design trends, care about aesthetics, and are frustrated that beautiful furniture is either out of budget or hard to find.

**Secondary segments worth separating:**
1. **Interior designers & stylists** — sourcing bold, unique pieces for client projects; likely repeat high-volume buyers
2. **New movers / first-time homeowners** — furnishing an entire home at once; high basket size potential
3. **Small-space / apartment dwellers** — drawn to the dedicated "Small Space Living Rooms" category
4. **Local LA shoppers** — can book a showroom appointment; higher-intent, closes faster

**Problem they're in when they start looking:** They've outgrown cheap flat-pack furniture but can't justify (or afford) boutique designer prices. They want their home to look like a magazine spread without the $10K sofa.

**Where leads come from:** Social media (Instagram, Pinterest, Facebook — all active), organic search ("luxury furniture Los Angeles," "modern furniture affordable"), word of mouth and designer referrals. Likely some Google Shopping ads.

**How they describe success:** "It looks exactly like the photo." "Finally found unique pieces that feel high-end." "My friends always ask where I got my furniture."

---

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:** Days to 2–3 weeks. Online shoppers may browse across multiple sessions before committing (furniture is a considered, high-involvement purchase). Local shoppers can close same-day via showroom visit.

**Free trial / consult / first-session offer:** No free trial. In-person showroom appointment available (free) in Commerce, CA for LA-area customers. This is the closest analog to a "demo."

**What/who closes the deal:** Self-serve online checkout for the majority. In-showroom consultation for local buyers. No sales team or proposal process detected.

**#1 objection before buying:** "I can't see or touch it before I buy." Buying furniture online carries real risk — color accuracy, scale in the room, material quality. Their 7-day return policy and 5,000+ five-star reviews are the main trust signals addressing this.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**Activation event:** The moment the first piece is delivered, placed in the room, and the customer sees their home transformed — the designer look they envisioned at a price that actually worked. Fast shipping (1–4 business days from LA warehouse) shortens the wait.

**How long / self-serve or assisted:** Self-serve. Delivery is handled by their warehouse; white-glove delivery available for LA-area orders. No assisted onboarding beyond customer service.

**Campaign implication:** Welcome & Onboarding should bridge the gap between purchase and delivery — building anticipation, setting expectations on delivery timeline, and priming them to share/review when it arrives.

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?** No subscription or membership. Pure repeat-purchase model — customers return when furnishing another room, moving again, or replacing pieces.

**Repeat cycle length:** Unpredictable; potentially 6–24 months between purchases unless the buyer is a designer with active client projects.

**Expansion looks like:** Purchasing for additional rooms (bought a dining set → return for living room sofa), moving up to higher-ticket pieces, buying from new monthly arrivals.

**Churn / drop-off problem:** High single-purchase abandonment. No detected loyalty program or structured re-engagement sequence. Once a customer is satisfied with their purchase, there's no obvious mechanism to pull them back before they forget about the brand.

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal (inferred):** More conversions from existing list + better post-purchase retention. The 15% first-order discount is generating sign-ups; the gap is converting those subscribers and keeping buyers engaged long-term.

**Target metrics:** Email CTR → site visit → purchase; repeat purchase rate; showroom appointment conversion.

**Brand voice / tone:** Aspirational but anti-snobbery. Bold and confident without pretension. Inclusive luxury — the brand explicitly positions against "crazy markups" and "stuffy showrooms." Copy should feel like a stylish friend giving you the inside track, not a formal retail brand. Use phrases that celebrate the discovery of beautiful design at real-world prices.

**Compliance constraints:** Standard CAN-SPAM / e-commerce. No healthcare, finance, or legal vertical. GDPR consideration for any EU-based traffic. Return policy (7-day) should be cited accurately in any transactional or post-purchase copy.

**Sending volume:** Unknown; sized to list (unknown list size, but 19 years in business and 5,000+ reviews suggests a meaningful base).

---

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

Based on the gaps above and the default priority order:

1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — upgrade from pure discount-code delivery to a 3–5 email sequence that tells the brand story, addresses the "can't touch it" objection with social proof, and bridges the gap to first delivery
2. **Post-Purchase / Room Completion** — a cross-sell sequence triggered after first order ships, designed to complete the room and request a review while excitement is high
3. **Win-Back / Re-Engagement** — target buyers who haven't purchased in 6–12 months; "new arrivals" and seasonal hooks give natural reactivation angles
