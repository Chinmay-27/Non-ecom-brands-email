# Brand Intake — Zippy Entertainment (zippyisfunny.com)

---

## 1. The Offer

**What they sell:** Mobile event entertainment services delivered to the client's location across the Phoenix metro area. Core menu:
- Foam, bubble, and gender-reveal parties ($395–$475 / session)
- Balloon artistry — arches, columns, themed designs, special-effect balloons, balloon drops (starting at $450–$525)
- Face painting and airbrush tattoos (hourly)
- Character appearances — Santa, Easter Bunny, superheroes, princesses, aliens (hourly)
- Magic shows (hourly)
- Balloon creation classes (hourly)

**Core outcome the customer is buying:** A professionally produced "wow moment" that makes the host the hero of the party — high-energy, memorable entertainment the kids and guests will talk about, delivered stress-free with zero cleanup.

**Price point & commitment:** One-off per-event. Typical ticket: $400–$600+ for a single service; multi-service events run higher. 50% non-refundable retainer to book; balloon décor and character experiences 100% upfront. Discounts for extended or recurring bookings. Preferred payment: Venmo/Zelle.

**Vertical:** ☑ Local & service

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:** Parents aged 25–45 in Phoenix metro suburbs (Scottsdale, Mesa, Gilbert, Chandler, Tempe, Queen Creek) planning a child's birthday party or family celebration. Middle-to-upper-middle income; willing to spend $400–$600+ on entertainment because the event matters and they want it done right.

**Secondary segments worth separating:**
1. Corporate event planners (team celebrations, holiday parties, company picnics)
2. Schools and daycares (field days, end-of-year parties)
3. Community organizations and nonprofits (fundraisers, block parties, charity events)

**Problem they're in when they start looking:**
- "My kid's birthday is 4 weeks away and I need something that will blow everyone away"
- Overwhelmed by party planning; want a single vendor who handles the entertainment completely
- Burned by unreliable vendors before; looking for someone trustworthy and professional

**Where leads come from today:** Google search (local SEO for "foam party Phoenix," "balloon artist Scottsdale," etc.), word-of-mouth / referrals, and possibly social media. Contact form and direct phone are primary capture points.

**How they describe success in their own words:** "The kids had a blast," "It was the best party ever," "You made it so easy — I didn't have to do anything," "Worth every penny."

---

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:** ☑ Days (most) to weeks. Site recommends booking 4–6 weeks to 1 year in advance; last-minute is possible.

**Free trial / free consult / first-session offer:** No free trial. Free availability check and informal phone/email consultation is the de facto entry point. Strong no-risk guarantee: if Zippy misses a confirmed booking, the event is free (up to 15 guests).

**Who/what closes the deal:** Availability confirmation → quote → client initiates 50% retainer payment (self-serve via Venmo/Zelle). No formal proposal or salesperson step; it's operationally lightweight.

**#1 objection or hesitation before buying:** Price — "Is $400–$500 for one hour of entertainment worth it vs. renting equipment cheaper?" Secondary: "Are you available on my date?" (fear of missing out on a good vendor).

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**Activation event:** The live event itself — specifically the first few minutes when the foam engulfs the yard or the balloon artist produces a stunning character creation. This is the proof of value.

**Pre-event aha:** Receiving a clear, professional booking confirmation and pre-event prep checklist gives parents early confidence they chose well — this is where the email lifecycle can manufacture an "aha" before the physical event.

**How long / self-serve or assisted:** Assisted. Zippy shows up, handles all setup and operation, then cleans up. Zero host effort post-booking.

*This becomes the anchor for Campaign 1: Welcome & Onboarding — build anticipation, deliver prep logistics, and reinforce that the decision was the right call.*

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?** Not by subscription — but naturally repeating. Birthday parties recur annually; corporate and community clients book multiple times per year. Retainer can roll over within 12 months on reschedule.

**Repeat/renewal cycle:** ~12 months for birthday families; variable for corporate/event clients.

**What "expansion" looks like:**
- Add a second service type to the same event (foam + face painting + balloon arch)
- Upgrade to premium packages (Glow-in-the-Dark foam, gender reveal add-ons)
- Longer duration add-ons ($200–$250/hr each additional hour)
- Character appearances bundled with balloon artistry

**Churn / drop-off problem:**
- Families whose kids age out of kids' entertainment (the natural lifecycle)
- One-and-done clients who don't know they can book again (no post-event nurture)
- Price-sensitive clients who try a cheaper competitor after year 1
- No-shows are mitigated by retainer policy, but last-minute cancellations still happen

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal this quarter:** ☑ More conversions/bookings (turn inquiry form submissions into paid bookings) + ☑ better retention (activate post-event referral and anniversary flows).

**Target metric & current baseline:** Booking conversion rate from inquiry form submissions (baseline unknown — to confirm on kickoff). Secondary: repeat booking rate.

**Brand voice / tone:** Enthusiastic, warm, and playful — the voice of a fun, reliable professional. Casual and approachable; uses exclamation points freely. Emphasizes joy ("mind-blowing," "happy memories") and trust ("licensed, insured, bonded, FBI-cleared, 100% guaranteed"). Military-veteran reliability underpins the friendliness. *Not* corporate-stiff; *not* so casual it feels amateur.

**Compliance constraints:** Standard CAN-SPAM / B2C US. No HIPAA, GDPR, or financial/legal industry constraints. Cancellation and retainer policy language must be accurate in any booking-related emails.

**Sending volume:** Unknown — to confirm once platform is identified.

---

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — Post-booking confirmation sequence: confirm the booking, build anticipation, deliver host prep checklist (water hookup, space requirements, what to expect), and prime for the review ask post-event.
2. **Lead-Magnet Nurture / Inquiry Conversion** — For availability-check form submissions that don't immediately book: address the price objection, showcase social proof (video testimonials, photos), and create urgency around calendar availability.
3. **Booking Reminders & No-Show Prevention** — T-7 day, T-48hr, and day-of reminder sequence with logistics checklist and excitement-building content, reducing last-minute cancellations and no-shows.

*(After these three: Anniversary/Renewal and Referral/Advocacy are the highest-ROI next campaigns given the naturally recurring event calendar.)*
