# Brand Intake — Beyond The Ordinary Show

---

## 1. The Offer

**What does the brand actually sell?**
Two distinct layers:
- **Free show** — Beyond The Ordinary Show: live webinar broadcasts and replays featuring 30+ spiritual thought leaders per season. Entry is free via email opt-in. This is the primary lead engine.
- **Paid membership** — *Living in the Extraordinary* at **$39/month, no contract**: weekly live transmissions with John Burgos, direct Q&A access, on-demand recordings, and a tight-knit community container.
- **Marketplace** — third-party speaker programs (e.g., A'sha Love's shadow work series) and John's own pocket guides (audio); pricing not publicly listed, estimated $97–$500+ range.
- **Mentorship** — 1:1 work with John; pricing not disclosed publicly.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:**
Movement from confusion, anxiety, and fear → clarity, ease, and love. Specifically: spiritual awakening, breaking old patterns, and feeling seen/held by a community of kindred spirits. For the membership, the positioning is less about knowledge and more about *pressure to act* — being in an environment that catalyzes decisive change.

**Price point & commitment:**
- Free tier: no commitment
- Membership: $39/month, month-to-month (explicitly "no contracts, no catch")
- Marketplace / mentorship: variable, not publicly listed

**Vertical:** Education / membership / spiritual media

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:**
Spiritually curious adults, skewing female, ages ~35–65. They feel stuck, anxious, or unfulfilled by mainstream culture and are actively seeking consciousness expansion, energy healing, and personal transformation. They're often already familiar with spiritual concepts (chakras, Akashic Records, shadow work) but struggle to translate seeking into sustained action.

**Secondary segments:**
1. **Casual listeners/free attendees** — podcast and replay opt-ins who haven't converted to paid; largest pool
2. **Marketplace buyers** — one-off purchasers of speaker programs; may not identify as "community" members
3. **Mentorship clients** — high-trust, high-ticket buyers in direct relationship with John

**Problem they're in when they start looking:**
Feeling unseen, misunderstood, or spiritually lost. A sense that mainstream media/life doesn't address the deeper questions they're sitting with. Often accompanied by anxiety, stagnation, or a longing for meaningful community.

**Where leads come from today:**
Primarily organic — podcast discovery (Apple Podcasts, Spotify, Libsyn), YouTube, social media (Instagram @JOHNBURGOS_SPIRITUALACTIVIST, Facebook group), and word-of-mouth within spiritual communities. Guest speaker cross-promotion from webinar series drives significant opt-ins each season.

**How they describe success:**
"I finally feel like I belong somewhere." / "I went from knowing what I wanted to actually doing it." / "I feel clear and grounded for the first time." / "The community holds me accountable in a way I've never experienced."

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:** Days to weeks

The typical path: discover show via podcast/social → opt-in for replay access → attend live calls → develop trust with John/community over multiple sessions → convert to $39/month membership or purchase a marketplace offer.

**Free trial / entry offer:**
Yes — the entire free show functions as an extended trial. No time-limited free trial for the membership itself; the free content is permanently accessible via opt-in.

**Who/what closes the deal:**
Email nurture + repeated live call attendance. John's presence and energy on the live calls is the primary closer. No formal sales call identified. Self-serve sign-up for the membership.

**#1 objection before buying:**
"Is a $39/month membership worth it when so much is already free?" Secondary: skepticism about whether a spiritual community will create *tangible* change vs. just being more consumption.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**Activation event:**
Attending a first **live** webinar transmission and experiencing a felt sense of resonance — connecting with a speaker's message, feeling the energy of the community, or having a personal insight shift during the call. This is the moment the platform stops feeling like content and starts feeling like transformation.

**How long does it take?**
One live call (typically 60–90 minutes). Self-serve: they opt in, get the link, show up.

**Implication for onboarding:**
The welcome sequence must do one job above all else: get the new subscriber to show up live. Everything else is secondary.

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?**
Yes — $39/month membership is the primary recurring revenue stream.

**Renewal / repeat cycle:**
Monthly. No annual plan identified (no annual discount or lock-in mentioned).

**What does expansion look like?**
- Purchasing marketplace programs from featured speakers
- Upgrading to personal mentorship with John
- Buying pocket guides / additional audio resources
- Attending live in-person events (if/when offered)

**Churn / drop-off risk:**
Members who stop attending live sessions lose the felt sense of community that justifies the subscription. Without accountability structures or milestone moments, passive members will quietly cancel. Month-to-month with no contract lowers the barrier to exit.

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal:** Full lifecycle — the gaps span every stage from opt-in nurture through retention and win-back.

**Highest-leverage starting point:**
The free show generates a large opt-in list that is almost certainly under-monetized. The biggest revenue unlock is a proper nurture-to-conversion sequence for free subscribers → $39/month membership.

**Target metrics:**
- Free → paid membership conversion rate (unknown baseline; verify on call)
- Live call show-up rate from new opt-ins
- Membership churn rate (monthly cancellations)

**Brand voice / tone:**
Inspirational, warm, mystical, and community-first. Uses spiritual language naturally (transmissions, activations, awakening, consciousness). Heart-centered and emotional rather than logical or feature-driven. Never pushy or overtly salesy — trust is earned through generosity. Copy should feel like a message from a wise friend, not a marketer.

**Compliance constraints:**
Standard CAN-SPAM / GDPR. No HIPAA, financial-sector, or legal-sector restrictions. Advisable to include standard personal-results disclaimers on transformation claims, but no regulated-industry compliance required.

**Sending volume:** Unknown — verify list size and ESP plan on call.
