Top executives read an average of 60 books a year. Not because they have more hours — because they've stopped letting the wrong books win the race for their attention.

Every quarter, our team pulls the titles that are actually shaping how leaders think, decide, and lead right now. Here are the three that earned a spot this quarter — plus one most leaders overlook entirely.

 

Pick No. 01

The Voltage Effect — John A. List

Why it matters: Most leaders can start something. Few can scale it without watching the results collapse. List — a University of Chicago economist who has run experiments at Uber, Lyft, and in classrooms across America — reveals the hidden science behind why great ideas fail at scale, and exactly what separates the ones that don't.

Pick No. 02

Slow Productivity — Cal Newport

Why it matters: Busyness has become the default proof of leadership — and it's burning out the best people on your team, possibly including you. Newport argues that sustainable high performance demands fewer things done at a deeper level, and he builds a practical framework for rethinking how you and your organization define meaningful output.

Pick No. 03 — The One Most Leaders Skip

Leading from the Inside Out — Kevin Cashman

Why it matters: Leaders chase strategy, systems, and process — and overlook the variable that drives all of it: themselves. Cashman's timeless framework on purpose-driven leadership has shaped CEOs across Fortune 500 companies, yet it quietly disappears from most reading lists. It belongs on yours. Read this one slowly.
