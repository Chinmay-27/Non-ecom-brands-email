## 1. The Offer

**What they sell:** Small-batch roasted specialty coffee shipped same day as roasting. 50+ varieties spanning single-origin, blends, flavored, functional coffee, and K-cup pods. Premium specialty teas (8+ varieties). Curated sample packs for exploration.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:** A cup you can actually taste the freshness in — coffee that arrived days after roasting, not months. The promise is "you'll notice the difference immediately."

**Price point & commitment:**
- Single bags: $19.99–$23.99 (one-off or subscribe-and-save)
- Sample packs: $21.99 (6-variety 2oz tasting kits — low-risk entry)
- K-cups: $19.99 (12-pack) / $59.99 (60-pack)
- Teas: $22.99 each
- Subscription: flexible frequency, cancel anytime, no fees, 48-hour notice window before next ship
- Free US shipping on all orders

**Vertical:** ☑ Other: DTC / specialty food & beverage (Shopify-based e-commerce)

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:** Home coffee enthusiast, roughly 25–45, who has graduated past drip machines and cares about freshness, sourcing, and flavor. Owns specialty brewing gear (pour-over, AeroPress, espresso). Frustrated by stale supermarket coffee. Willing to pay $20–$24/bag for quality they can feel.

**Secondary segments worth separating:**
1. **Convenience/office K-cup buyer** — wants pod format, less interested in terroir
2. **Single-origin aficionado** — wants traceability, origin stories, rotating seasonal lots
3. **Gift buyer** — looking for a premium coffee gift for a coffee-loving friend or colleague
4. **Flavored/casual drinker** — wants enjoyable daily cup, not deeply nerdy about process

**Problem they're in when they start looking:** "I'm tired of bad coffee and I want something that actually tastes like it was cared about." Or: "I want to find a subscription I can trust that won't send me stale bags."

**Where leads come from today:** Unknown from research — likely organic search (freshness/small-batch keywords), social (Instagram coffee community), and word-of-mouth. Newsletter signup on homepage. Sample packs as acquisition vehicle.

**How they describe success:** "This is the best coffee I've ever had at home." / "I can actually taste the difference." / "I never have to wonder if my coffee is stale anymore."

---

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:** Same day to 2–3 days. Low-consideration DTC purchase — primary friction is discovery, not deliberation.

**Free trial / first offer:** Sample packs ($21.99 for 6 × 2oz) function as a low-risk trial. No free tier. Accessible price point removes most hesitation.

**Who/what closes the deal:** Self-serve Shopify checkout. Free shipping removes final friction. Strong product photography and freshness messaging do the selling.

**#1 objection before buying:** "Is this actually fresher/better than what I can get locally, and is it worth paying $20–$24 when I can grab something at Whole Foods?" — the quality-vs-price trust gap is the main barrier for first-time buyers.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**Activation event:** First brew from a bag received within days of roasting. The sensory proof — aroma out of the bag, flavor in the cup — is where the brand promise becomes real.

**How long:** 2–5 days from order (same-day roast + ship → delivery). Entirely self-serve, no assisted onboarding.

**Implication for Campaign 1:** The welcome sequence must pre-sell the unboxing/brew moment *before* the bag arrives — prime the customer's senses and give them a brew guide so the first cup lands right. Then follow up after estimated delivery to capture the reaction and push toward subscription.

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?** Yes — subscription model available. Flexible frequency (customer can modify), skip deliveries, pause up to 3 months, swap products, cancel with no fees (48-hour notice).

**Renewal/repeat cycle:** Driven by chosen subscription frequency (weekly, bi-weekly, monthly — specific options not confirmed on site). One-time buyers can be re-engaged when their bag is likely running low (~2–3 weeks post-purchase for a 12oz bag).

**What "expansion" looks like:**
- Move from one-time to subscription
- Upgrade from sample pack to full bag
- Add teas or functional coffee to existing order
- Gift a subscription to someone else
- Trade up to premium single-origins after starting with blends

**Churn / drop-off problem:**
- Subscription: missing the 48-hour cancel window causes frustration and damages trust
- One-time buyers: never prompted to reorder or subscribe after first purchase (likely biggest leak)
- Lapsed subscribers: no visible win-back program
- Gift buyers: transactional, rarely become recurring

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal (inferred):** Convert one-time buyers → subscribers, and reactivate lapsed customers. Likely also: grow list through sample pack acquisition and newsletter.

**Target metric:** Subscription conversion rate from first purchase; repeat purchase rate within 60 days.

**Brand voice / tone:** Confident and aspirational, not elitist. Educational but accessible — respects the craft without lecturing. Founder-warm (Dave is reachable via email). Clean and direct copy style. "Only the best reach Top Tier" — sets standards without pretension.

**Compliance constraints:** CAN-SPAM / GDPR standard. Subscription emails must include clear cancel instructions (FTC Negative Option Rule). No special vertical compliance (no healthcare, finance, legal). Food/beverage — avoid unsubstantiated health claims.

**Sending volume:** Unknown — scale to list size.
