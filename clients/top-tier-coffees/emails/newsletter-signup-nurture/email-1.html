The Problem

That "best by" date doesn't tell you when it was roasted.

Coffee hits its peak flavor in the first two to four weeks after roasting. After that, the aromatic oils — the ones responsible for everything you actually taste — begin to oxidize. The CO₂ that keeps it fresh off-gases. What's left is flat, bitter, and predictable in all the wrong ways.

Most grocery store coffee was roasted months before it reached the shelf. Then it sat in a warehouse. Then on the shelf. By the time you open the valve on that bag and take a sniff, you're already weeks past the window.

You're not imagining it. That coffee has always tasted a little like nothing.
