# Brand Intake — Port City 251
**URL:** https://www.portcity251.com  
**Date researched:** 2026-06-03  
**Vertical:** Local media / community newsletter — Mobile, Alabama

---

## 1. The Offer

**What they sell:**  
Port City 251 runs a free weekly email newsletter covering hyperlocal Mobile, AL news — events, restaurant openings, business announcements, civic decisions, real estate, community stories. On the business side, they sell advertising packages (banner ads, business spotlights, event listings, Facebook posts) to local small businesses who want to reach that subscriber base.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:**  
- *Subscribers:* Stay effortlessly informed about everything happening in their city without searching — a curated, 5-minute morning read that feels like a neighbor filling you in.  
- *Advertisers:* Genuine hyper-local reach and community credibility at prices a small local business can actually afford.

**Price point & commitment:**  
- Newsletter: **free**, no commitment, unsubscribe anytime  
- Ad packages: **Bronze $95/mo** · **Silver $175/mo** · **Gold $325/mo** (month-to-month, per 4 issues)  
- One-off add-ons: Business Spotlight $95 · Event Listing $25 · Facebook Post $50  
- Top Banner ad $180/4 issues · Mid $120 · Footer $80

**Vertical:** Local & service (hyperlocal media + SMB advertising)

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:**  
- *Consumer side:* Mobile, AL residents aged roughly 25–65 who care about their community, follow local news casually, and want one convenient place to catch up — not a news junkie, just a connected neighbor.  
- *Business side:* Local small business owners (restaurants, retail, services, events) who want affordable, community-trusted visibility and feel overlooked by expensive or unfocused digital ad options.

**Secondary segments worth separating:**  
1. Free directory listers — businesses that added themselves to the directory but have not bought advertising (highest-value warm lead pool)  
2. Event organizers — community orgs, nonprofits, or promoters who need event reach (entry-point buyers via the $25 event listing)  
3. Potential referral sources — subscribers who are themselves business owners or know business owners

**What problem are they in when they start looking?**  
Subscribers: They feel disconnected or catch local news too late — after an event has passed, after a restaurant has closed. Advertisers: They need local customers but find Facebook Ads too complex or broad, and traditional local media is either gone or too expensive.

**Where do leads come from today?**  
Organic: word of mouth ("500+ neighbors already subscribed"), Facebook community group (10,000+ members), and SEO for Mobile AL local news searches. Advertising leads likely come from inbound form fills by business owners who subscribe and then decide to advertise.

**How do they describe success?**  
Subscribers: "I always know what's happening in Mobile." Advertisers: "People actually come in and mention they saw us in the newsletter."

---

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:**  
- Subscriber: **same day** (one-click form, instant confirmation)  
- Advertiser: **days to 1–2 weeks** (form → 24-hr response → email/phone close)

**Free trial / free consult / first-session offer:**  
No formal trial for advertising. The free newsletter itself lets prospective advertisers evaluate quality and audience fit before spending. The free directory listing is a natural low-friction entry point — a business gets listed at zero cost, making the upsell to a paid package a natural next conversation.

**Who/what closes the deal?**  
Subscribers: 100% self-serve web form. Advertisers: inbound contact form → team member responds and closes by email or phone within 24 hours.

**#1 objection or hesitation before buying:**  
*Advertisers:* "Your list is only 500 people — is that worth $95–$325/month?" The 10K Facebook community is the main counterweight, but the team needs a strong ROI narrative and social proof. *Subscribers:* "I already get too many emails" — countered by the no-spam, one-click-unsubscribe promise.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**For subscribers:**  
Opening the first newsletter and spotting something they would have completely missed — a weekend event, a new restaurant, a business opening near them. The thought: *"I almost didn't know about this."* That moment of pleasant surprise converts a passive subscriber into a habitual opener.

**For advertisers:**  
Receiving a customer who walks in and says "I saw you in the Port City 251 newsletter." Or seeing a spike in calls/web traffic the week their Business Spotlight runs. Self-serve; happens naturally within the first send cycle if the audience is engaged.

**How long does it take?**  
Subscribers: first newsletter (within days of signup). Advertisers: first issue their ad runs (within the next weekly send).

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?**  
Yes, on the business side — Bronze/Silver/Gold are monthly recurring ad packages. Subscriber side is free but engagement (opens/clicks) is the metric of health.

**Renewal / repeat cycle:**  
Monthly for advertisers; weekly for subscriber sends.

**What does "expansion" look like?**  
Bronze → Silver → Gold package upgrades. Add-on Business Spotlights, Event Listings, and Facebook posts between billing cycles. A Gold advertiser could also become a content partner or sponsor of a newsletter section.

**Churn / drop-off problem:**  
- *Subscribers:* Unsubscribe if content becomes too ad-heavy, feels templated, or stops covering their specific interests. Retention = quality and relevance of editorial content.  
- *Advertisers:* Churn if they can't attribute ROI — the small list size is an ongoing objection. Need proactive "here's what your ad did" reporting (Gold includes monthly analytics; Bronze/Silver don't).

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal:**  
Grow both sides of the marketplace: more newsletter subscribers (audience growth = advertising leverage) and more paying advertisers (revenue). Most pressing gap is converting free directory listers and warm inquiries into paid ad packages.

**Target metric:**  
Advertiser conversion rate from inquiry → paid package; subscriber list growth rate; open rate maintenance (currently ~25%).

**Brand voice / tone:**  
Warm, neighborly, and community-proud. Casual and conversational — like a well-informed neighbor sharing the week's highlights over coffee. Celebratory of local businesses and residents. No corporate speak, no jargon, no national-media detachment. Uses "neighbors" naturally. First-person plural ("we cover", "our community") feels authentic here.

**Compliance constraints:**  
Standard CAN-SPAM (unsubscribe link, physical address in footer). No healthcare, finance, or legal constraints. Local Alabama operation — no GDPR exposure expected. Gmail contact address suggests lean, early-stage team.

**Sending volume:**  
Weekly newsletter to 500+ is the baseline. Lifecycle automation sequences would be layered on top — small list means deliverability is not a concern at current scale.

---

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

Based on gaps above and the dual-audience model (subscribers + advertisers):

1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — New subscriber sequence (3–4 emails) that delivers the first-value moment, sets expectations, and surfaces the business directory / advertising opportunity to anyone who lists a business
2. **Lead-Magnet Nurture** — Convert free directory listers into paying advertisers: explain the audience, lead with the Business Spotlight offer ($95 one-time, low commitment), include social proof
3. **Trial/Consult Conversion** — Follow-up sequence for advertising inquiries that don't close immediately: objection handling (ROI, list size), Bronze package offer with easy next step, deadline or limited-availability nudge
