The real reason mornings feel so heavy

I want to ask you something, and I want you to be honest with yourself when you answer it.

When your alarm goes off in the morning — not the second alarm, the real one — what's the first feeling that hits you?

For a lot of women I talk to, it's not excitement. It's not even neutral. It's this low, heavy feeling — like the day is already asking too much of you before you've even sat up.

I've been there. I know what it feels like to live a life that's hard to get up to in the morning. And for a long time, I thought it meant something was wrong with me — that I was lazy, or broken, or just not built for the life I wanted.

Here's what I know now: it's not a personality flaw. It's a pattern. And patterns can be changed.

The real culprit? A confidence gap — not a character flaw.

That heavy feeling in the morning usually isn't about being tired. It's about disconnection — the gap between who you are right now and who you sense you're supposed to be. When that gap feels huge, getting out of bed feels pointless.

Confidence isn't a trait you either have or you don't. It's something that grows — one small, deliberate act of self-trust at a time. Which means the heaviness you feel? It's a signal, not a sentence.

Today's Free Practice

The 3-Line Morning Reframe

Before you check your phone tomorrow morning, grab a pen and finish these three lines. It takes under two minutes:

1 "One thing I believe about myself today is…"
2 "The woman I'm becoming would…"
3 "Today, I choose to show up by…"

This is the same kind of reframe work built into your 30-day workbook. Your brain is looking for evidence of who you are — give it something true to hold onto every single morning.

I wrote a full breakdown on the blog that goes deeper into why this works — including the specific mindset shift that makes the difference between doing this once and actually building it into a habit.

It's free. It's practical. And it's exactly the kind of tool I wish someone had handed me when I was still hitting snooze on my own life.
