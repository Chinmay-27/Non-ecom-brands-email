## 1. The Offer

**What does the brand sell?**  
Women's personal-development content: a blog (100+ free articles), free self-improvement eBooks and workbooks, and implied coaching/courses by certified life coach Brittany Cherrell. Currently the only visible product is a free 30-day quotes eBook + workbook hosted on Teachable. Paid courses and 1:1 coaching are implied by her credentials and directory listings but are not publicly listed with pricing at this time.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:**  
Unlocking full potential, building confidence, and attracting a life worth waking up for — via practical inner-work tools that don't require a large financial investment.

**Price point & commitment:**  
- Free tier: eBook, blog articles (primary acquisition channel)  
- Paid courses: Budget-conscious / low-ticket (no current price listed)  
- 1:1 coaching: Unknown; likely mid-ticket, by inquiry only  
- No subscription or membership confirmed  

**Vertical:** ☒ Education / nonprofit / membership (self-improvement school for women)

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:**  
Women of any age or life stage who feel stuck, under-confident, or disconnected from their potential. Particularly budget-conscious women who want personal development without expensive coaching fees.

**Secondary segments worth separating:**  
1. Women entrepreneurs / ambitious women (listed in Women Helping Women Entrepreneurs directory)  
2. Women in major life transitions (career change, relationship shift, new chapter)  
3. Early-stage personal development beginners (very new to inner work)  

**Problem they're in when they start looking:**  
"I'm living a life that's hard to get up to in the morning." Brittany uses this exact language — the pain state is low motivation, stagnation, and a gap between who they are and who they want to be.

**Where leads come from:**  
- Pinterest (active profile: @leveluplady2020)  
- Facebook page (@leveluplady2020)  
- Blog SEO (100+ articles, strong content base)  
- Women's entrepreneur communities / directories  
- Teachable school (organic / search)  

**How they describe success in their own words:**  
Feeling confident, living fearlessly, going after a life they love, achieving their full potential, attracting success.

---

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:** Days to weeks (for low-ticket digital products); potentially months for 1:1 coaching.

**Free trial / lead magnet:** Yes — the 30-day quotes eBook + workbook is free, functioning as both a lead magnet and a top-of-funnel product. The blog itself serves as ongoing free value.

**Who/what closes the deal:** Self-serve Teachable signup for free and paid courses. Direct email (brittany@leveluplady.co) for coaching inquiries — no booking page or CTA visible.

**#1 objection before buying:** Price / affordability. The brand explicitly states "without paying a fortune" and positions all offerings as "free and budget conscious" — cost is the primary stated barrier they overcome in their messaging.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**The activation event:**  
Reading a resonant blog article OR completing a day's prompt from the free 30-day eBook/workbook and experiencing a mindset reframe. First value is entirely content-led.

**How long / self-serve?**  
Self-serve and immediate — the eBook downloads instantly from Teachable at no cost. Blog articles are ungated. The moment is cognitive/emotional, not a product feature.

*→ Welcome & Onboarding campaign anchor: deliver the free eBook, walk her through Day 1, and prime her to keep coming back.*

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?**  
Not confirmed. No membership, subscription, or retainer is publicly visible. Retention is currently passive (repeat blog readership, future course releases).

**Renewal / repeat cycle length:** Unknown / not applicable with current visible product set.

**Expansion path:**  
Implied but not architected: free blog/eBook → low-cost course → 1:1 coaching. No visible upgrade CTA or pricing ladder.

Very high passive churn risk. Free-content consumers have zero financial commitment and can disengage silently. There is no visible re-engagement or nurture mechanism. The brand's email list (if one exists) is the primary retention lever — and it appears underused.

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal (inferred):** More conversions/bookings and better retention — the brand has strong top-of-funnel content but no mechanism to convert free readers/downloaders into paying clients.

**Target metric & baseline:** Unknown — no public data on CTR, conversion rate, or list size.

**Brand voice / tone:**  
Warm, sisterly, conversational. Brittany opens with "Hey Lady!" and speaks from personal experience ("I know what it feels like to live a life that's hard to get up to in the morning"). Empathetic, humble, and encouraging. Budget-conscious positioning keeps the tone inclusive rather than aspirational-premium. Not corporate, not preachy — a trusted older sister who's figured some things out.

**Compliance constraints:** None identified. Standard CAN-SPAM / GDPR apply. No healthcare, finance, or legal content noted.

**Sending volume:** Unknown; likely small list size given early-stage brand footprint.

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

Given that all campaigns are missing and the brand's primary motion is content → free download → (no follow-up), the priority is:

1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — greet new eBook downloaders, deliver immediate value, set expectations for what Level up Lady is, and warm them up toward a next step (blog, future course, or coaching inquiry)
2. **Lead-Magnet Nurture** — 4–6 email sequence that follows up on the 30-day eBook, shares complementary blog content, and introduces the idea of working more closely with Brittany
3. **Trial/Consult Conversion** — since there's no booking CTA visible, this campaign introduces a soft invite to a discovery call or a low-ticket next offer; turns nurtured leads into paying clients for the first time
