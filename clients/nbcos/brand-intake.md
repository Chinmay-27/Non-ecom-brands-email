# Brand Intake — NBCOS
**National Board for Certification in the Orthopedic Specialties**
nbcos.org · St. Petersburg, FL · Founded 2019 · Not-for-profit credentialing agency

---

## 1. The Offer

**What they sell:**
Board-recognized specialty certification exams and continuing education for orthopedic allied health professionals, bundled inside an annual NBCOS Full Membership. Core products:

| Product | Details |
|---|---|
| OPE-C® (Orthopedic Physician Extender Cert) | Flagship exam — 125 MCQ, 4-hour limit, 70% pass required |
| OPE-SC (Surgical Certification) | Perioperative orthopedic specialty exam |
| OPE-Radiography | Radiographic imaging specialty exam |
| OPE-CS (Clinical Specialist) | Combined credential earned after all three exams |
| ROT (Registered Orthopedic Technologist) | Free for ASOP members; casting/bracing focus |
| CEU Library | 150+ Category A CEUs included in membership |
| Program Director Membership | Educator tier with curriculum resources |

**Core outcome the customer is buying:**
A nationally recognized credential that validates orthopedic clinical competency — the thing that gets an AT hired at a 9K orthopedic office, 6K hospital, or 10K+ urgent care center instead of a generalist without the designation.

**Price point & commitment:**
- $179/year — Full Membership (exam access + 150+ CEUs + member portal + career portal)
- $37 — 50+ Category A CEU bundle (non-member)
- $79 — 100+ Category A CEU bundle (non-member)
- $197/year — Program Director membership ($127 renewal)
- $50 — Exam retake fee
- Recurring annual membership; no monthly option apparent

**Vertical:** Education / Nonprofit / Membership (professional credentialing)

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:**
Certified Athletic Trainers (ATC) who want to move into or advance within orthopedic clinical settings. They already hold BOC certification but need a specialty designation to compete for orthopedic-specific roles.

**Secondary segments:**
1. Early-career ATs — recent grads wanting a competitive edge before entering the job market
2. Mid-career ATs — plateaued in general settings, eyeing orthopedic clinics or hospital systems
3. Athletic training program directors/faculty — building OPE curriculum to send students into the credential pipeline
4. Orthopedic allied professionals — OTs, surgical techs, and casting techs pursuing the ROT credential

**Pain points:**
- General AT certification doesn't prove orthopedic specialty competency to employers
- No widely adopted orthopedic specialty credential existed before NBCOS — the organization was founded to fill this gap
- Annual CEU requirements feel like a chore without a focused specialty outcome
- Orthopedic employers increasingly prefer candidates with documented specialty skills; ATs without OPE-C feel passed over
- Uncertainty: "Is this credential actually recognized by employers?"

**Where leads come from today:**
Primarily ASOP (American Society of Orthopedic Professionals) membership overlap, university athletic training programs enrolled in the OPE curriculum, BOC-certified AT community, and orthopedic industry events/conferences.

**How they describe success:**
"I passed the boards." / "I'm now OPE-C certified." / "I got the job at [orthopedic group]." / "My employer covered it."

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying member:** Days to a couple of weeks. This is a professional, research-driven decision but not a long enterprise sale. Candidates typically research the credential, review the Candidate Guide, check if employers recognize it, then join.

**Free entry point / lead magnet:**
- Free OPE Candidate Guide (PDF)
- Free sample exam questions
- Free ROT exam for ASOP members
No paid trial; membership is the conversion event.

**What closes the deal:**
Self-serve — the "JOIN NOW" button on the website triggers online signup and immediate portal access. No sales call required.

**#1 objection before buying:**
"Is the OPE-C® actually recognized and valued by the orthopedic employers I want to work for?" — fear of paying $179 for a credential that interviewers won't know. Secondary objection: "Can I pass the exam?" (addressed by sample questions and Candidate Guide).

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**Activation event:** Passing the OPE-C® (or ROT) exam and receiving the board credential — the moment the designation can go on a résumé, LinkedIn, and ID badge.

**How long does it take:** Variable — members can sit for the exam shortly after joining. Study time is self-directed using the included CEU library and home-study modules.

**Assisted or self-serve:** Self-serve exam via online testing portal; study resources are in the member portal.

*This moment (credential earned) is the anchor for Campaign 1: Welcome & Onboarding — everything should drive toward exam completion.*

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?** Yes — annual membership. CEU reporting cycle also drives ongoing engagement.

**Renewal cycle:**
- Annual membership renewal
- 2-year CEU reporting cycle: 24 hours in year 1 (prior to/including 2026), 12 hours in year 2 (2027); members audited by April 1

**Expansion looks like:**
- Stacking all three exams to earn the OPE-CS (Clinical Specialist) combined credential
- Upgrading from a CEU bundle to Full Membership
- Program director upgrading to institutional partnership
- Conference and workshop attendance (member discounts apply)

**Churn / drop-off risk:**
- Members who don't log required CEUs before December 31 deadline risk credential lapse
- Members whose orthopedic employers don't actively recognize or reward OPE-C see diminishing ROI on renewal
- Members who passed the exam but never got an orthopedic job feel the credential didn't deliver

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal:** Likely a mix of more conversions (free resource → paid membership) and better retention (CEU completion → renewal). The credential-stacking upsell to OPE-CS is a natural expansion play.

**Target metrics:** Membership conversion rate from lead magnet downloads; exam completion rate post-signup; annual renewal rate; CEU completion rate before December 31 deadline.

**Brand voice / tone:**
Authoritative and credentialing-precise — speaks peer-to-peer with healthcare professionals. Never dumbed down, never cold. Career-outcome focused: "your credential," "your next role," "your patients." Formal enough to signal legitimacy; warm and encouraging enough to move action. Think: professional association meets career coach.

**Compliance constraints:**
- CAN-SPAM required (physical address + unsubscribe in all emails)
- No HIPAA exposure (no patient data handled)
- Non-profit — avoid specific salary or employment guarantees in copy
- Credential claims must be accurate and not overstate employer recognition

**Sending volume:** Unknown; assume conservative list with room to grow.

## First 3 Campaigns to Build

Based on the gaps above and the default priority order:

1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — Drive new members from signup → exam completion (the activation event). Segment: ATC candidates vs. program directors.
2. **Lead-Magnet Nurture** — Convert Candidate Guide / sample question downloaders into paid Full Membership. Address the #1 objection (employer recognition) with social proof.
3. **Renewal & CEU Deadline Reminder** — Automated sequence tied to December 31 CEU deadline and annual membership renewal date. Include OPE-CS upsell for members who've passed one or two exams.
