## 1. The Offer

**What they sell:** Single-ingredient, human-grade dog treats and chews. Every product is one ingredient — Just Chicken, Just Beef, Just Turkey, Just Pork, Just Fish Skins, Just Organic Sweet Potatoes, and Summit Dog Chews. Made in small batches in a Boston commercial kitchen. Also offers a Build-a-Box customization, gift boxes, and eGift cards.

**Core outcome:** Peace of mind. Owners know *exactly* what their dog is eating — no fillers, no preservatives, no steroids, no mystery. Dogs with allergies, sensitivities, or picky palates finally have a treat they love and can tolerate.

**Price point & commitment:**
- Individual bags: $13.95–$20.95
- Meat Lover's Box (bundle): from $83.80
- Gift Box: $43.95
- Free shipping on orders over $66
- Subscription option available (repeat purchase; exact intervals/discount not published)
- Satisfaction guarantee: full refund if dog doesn't enjoy the treats — no-risk first purchase

**Vertical:** ☑ Other: DTC pet food / dog treats (e-commerce, physical product)

## 2. The Audience

**Primary customer / ICP:** Health-conscious dog owner, roughly 30–55, who treats their dog like a family member. Prioritizes clean, transparent ingredients. Often has a dog with food sensitivities, allergies, a picky palate, or is a senior/puppy. Mid-to-upper income; willing to pay a premium for trustworthy sourcing.

**Secondary segments worth separating:**
- Dog owners managing specific protein allergies (single-ingredient = elimination-diet safe)
- Senior dog owners who need softer textures (Just Chicken Rounds)
- Gift-givers (holiday, birthdays, new puppy)
- New puppy owners establishing clean feeding habits
- Multi-dog households / high-volume repeat buyers (subscription targets)

**Problem they're in when they start looking:** Their dog reacted badly to a mainstream treat (vomiting, itching, diarrhea), or they read an ingredient label and were alarmed. Or a vet recommended single-ingredient treats. They're searching for "best treats for dogs with allergies" or "single ingredient dog treats USA."

**Where leads come from today:** Organic SEO (blog content targets sensitive-stomach, grain-free, ingredient queries), social media, word-of-mouth/referrals (97% positive reviews, 9-year customer stories), and likely some paid social.

**How they describe success:** "I finally know what my dog is eating." / "Her coat is beautiful, vet noticed." / "Same enthusiasm 9 years later." / "Perfect for my senior dog."

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:** Same day. Low-consideration purchase once the value prop lands — see ingredients, read reviews, buy.

**Free trial / low-risk entry:** No free trial, but a **full satisfaction guarantee** ("if your dog doesn't love it, we'll refund you") effectively removes purchase risk. This is the brand's trial substitute.

**What closes the deal:** Self-serve e-commerce. Social proof (1,162 reviews, 97% positive), ingredient transparency, USA-sourcing badge, free-shipping threshold, and the satisfaction guarantee.

**#1 objection:** Price premium. $20 for a bag of chicken strips vs. $6 at the grocery store. Overcome by: single ingredient (you can verify it), human-grade, no imported mystery meat, satisfaction guarantee, and the fact that customers' vets have noticed improvements.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**Activation event:** The dog goes wild for the treat AND the owner flips the bag and sees just one ingredient. Both happen together on first delivery. Validates palatability *and* purity in one moment.

**How long / self-serve:** Entirely self-serve. Happens on receipt of the first order — no onboarding, no call, no setup. The product does the work.

*→ This is the anchor for Campaign 1: Welcome & Onboarding. The email job is to pre-frame this moment ("when your order arrives, watch your pup's reaction — and flip the bag"), set expectations about what makes these treats different, and prime for the second order.*

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?** Yes — subscription model is available. Likely a standard "subscribe & save" model; exact discount and frequency not published on the site.

**Repeat cycle:** Dogs eat treats regularly; a health-conscious owner re-orders every 4–8 weeks depending on dog size and treat frequency.

**Expansion looks like:**
- Trying a new protein (Just Beef → also try Just Turkey)
- Adding Summit chews as a complement to treats
- Build-a-Box for multi-protein households
- Gift boxes / eGift cards at holidays, birthdays, new-puppy gifts

**Churn / drop-off risk:**
- One-time buyers who don't convert to subscription lapse silently
- Flavor fatigue if owner only ever buys one product
- Price sensitivity if household budget tightens
- Dog passes away (natural end of lifecycle)

**Target metric baseline:** Unknown — need to establish. Likely tracking repeat purchase rate and subscription attach rate.

Avoid disease-cure claims per FTC/AAFCO pet marketing guidelines (can say "supports healthy coat," cannot say "treats allergies").
