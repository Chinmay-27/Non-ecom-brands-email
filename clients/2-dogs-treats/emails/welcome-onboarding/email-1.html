Hey, I'm Mike — and this whole thing started with Riley.

Riley was my dog. She had a sensitive stomach that made every treat a gamble — I'd buy something that looked healthy, she'd eat it, and within an hour she'd be miserable. I'd flip the bag over to figure out why, and I'd find a paragraph of ingredients I couldn't pronounce.

So I started making her treats at home. Just chicken. That's it. She went absolutely wild for them, her stomach settled, and I could see exactly what I was feeding her. A few neighbors asked if they could buy some. That turned into a Boston commercial kitchen. That turned into 2 Dogs Treats.

Today we make a handful of proteins — chicken, beef, turkey, pork, fish skins, organic sweet potato, Summit chews — and the rule hasn't changed: every bag is one ingredient.

When your order arrives, do this.

Two things happen the first time your dog gets a 2 Dogs treat. Customers tell us about this so often it feels like a rite of passage:

1.

Watch your pup. We're talking nose-twitching, tail-going-full-helicopter, pawing-at-the-bag level of excited. Real food smells like real food — they can tell immediately.

2.

Flip the bag over. Read the ingredient list. It takes two seconds. There's only one thing on it. That's the whole point.
