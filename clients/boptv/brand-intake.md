# Brand Intake — BOP TV (Black on Purpose Television Network)
**URL:** https://www.boptv.com  
**Vertical:** Streaming media / Entertainment / Membership  
**Tagline:** "Black TV The Way It Should Be!"  
**Founded:** 2013 by Michael Campbell (Las Vegas)  
**Positioning:** The largest Black-owned streaming television network in the world

---

## 1. The Offer

**What they sell:** A subscription streaming TV service with 30+ channels of independently produced, Black-owned and Black-created content — films, TV shows, cartoons, documentaries, BOP TV originals, and more. Secondary offerings include production services (studio, audio post, video editing, live streaming, music production), advertising packages for Black businesses, content distribution for creators, a media arts training center, and an affiliate referral program.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:** A single destination for positive, conscious Black entertainment — content that reflects Black culture and excellence without the negative stereotypes recycled on mainstream TV. "There's Nothing Blacker."

**Price point & commitment:**
- Monthly: **$12.99/month**, no contract, cancel anytime
- Annual: **$99.99/year** (~$8.33/mo) — promoted as "Best Value"
- Both plans: full access, 30+ channels, new content added weekly

**Vertical:** ☑ Streaming media / Entertainment / Membership (subscription)

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:** Black Americans and global African diaspora, roughly 25–55, who feel culturally unserved or misrepresented by mainstream streaming platforms (Netflix, Hulu, Prime). They want positive, conscious Black content for themselves and their families.

**Secondary segments worth separating:**
1. **Black families with children** — dedicated kids & teen channel; onboarding should highlight family content
2. **Black business owners** — want to advertise their brand to a Black audience; separate B2B nurture track needed
3. **Independent Black content creators** — want distribution for their shows/films; content submission funnel
4. **African market** — BOP TV is actively expanding into Nigeria and Ghana; culturally distinct segment

**Problem they're in when they start looking:** Scrolling through Netflix/Hulu/Prime and finding no authentic Black-created content, or finding only content with negative stereotypes. They want "Black TV the way it should be" — not the mainstream version of Blackness.

**Where leads come from today:** Social media (Facebook, Twitter/X, Instagram), YouTube channel (small — 1.09K subscribers), word of mouth, affiliate referrals, organic search, streaming device app stores (Roku, Amazon Fire, Apple TV). Limited paid acquisition signals on site.

**How they describe success:** "Finally a place where I can watch content that looks like me." "Excellent presentations of African-American history and culture." "Thank you for countering the negative characterization of African-Americans." (verbatim five-star reviews shown on site)

---

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:** Same day. Fully self-serve — visitor lands, sees CTA, clicks "Sign Me Up," and subscribes directly.

**Free trial / intro offer:** None listed. The annual plan ($99.99/yr) is positioned as the value offer vs. monthly. No free trial, no demo, no sample content gate noted. This is a notable gap — there's no low-risk entry point for skeptical prospects.

**Who/what closes the deal:** Self-serve web or app signup. Pricing page has two plan cards with "Sign Me Up" buttons. No salesperson, no consultation.

**#1 objection before buying:** "Why would I pay for this when Netflix has more content?" — content library depth vs. larger platforms, plus unfamiliarity with the brand outside the core community. Secondary concern: streaming tech quality / device compatibility.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**The activation event:** Streaming their first show or movie they couldn't find on any mainstream platform — the moment they discover a channel (History & Culture, Inspirational, Kids, etc.) that speaks directly to their identity and interests.

**How long / self-serve or assisted:** Immediate and self-serve once subscribed. But with 30+ channels and no guided onboarding, many new subscribers likely stall at the content catalog and churn before the aha moment.

**Email implication:** Welcome & Onboarding is the most urgent campaign to build. It must do the job of a guided tour — segment by content interest (Family / Culture & History / Business / Inspirational), then deliver 2–3 curated picks within the first 48–72 hours to get the subscriber streaming before they forget they signed up.

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Revenue recurring?** Yes — monthly or annual subscription.

**Renewal/repeat cycle:** Monthly auto-renews; annual renews once per year.

**Expansion looks like:**
- Upgrade from monthly ($12.99) → annual ($99.99) plan
- Black business owners purchasing advertising packages
- Content creators purchasing production services
- Event sponsorships and vendor opportunities

**Churn / drop-off problem:** Passive churn is the biggest risk. New subscribers who don't find their preferred content quickly will simply stop using the app and let the subscription lapse. Low brand awareness (small social following) means the subscriber base likely skews heavily toward true believers — which is great for NPS but creates a referral growth ceiling. An engagement/value newsletter is the main lever to keep subscribers active between login sessions.

**Email platform / ESP / CRM:** Unknown — not surfaced on site. Likely a basic ESP (Mailchimp, Constant Contact, or similar); sophistication unknown.

**Existing lead magnets:** Affiliate program, content creator submission program, Center for Media Arts & Technology classes. None appear to have a documented email nurture sequence.

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal:** More conversions from site visitors (subscriber acquisition) + better retention of existing subscribers — the two levers that matter most for a self-serve streaming subscription.

**Target metrics:** Subscriber conversion rate from pricing page visits; monthly subscriber churn rate; monthly→annual upgrade rate. (Baseline: unknown without ESP/analytics access.)

**Brand voice / tone:** Bold, proud, unapologetically Black. Community-first and empowerment-forward. Direct ("Sign Me Up"), declarative ("The Largest Black Owned Streaming Television Network in the World"), and rallying ("Join The BOP TV Family" / "Where we love being Black!"). Righteous edge when addressing industry racism. Casual warmth in community-facing copy.

**Compliance:** Standard CAN-SPAM / GDPR. No HIPAA, finance, or legal-specific constraints. Privacy policy published on site.

**Sending volume:** Unknown. Start conservatively (1–2 emails/week) until engagement data is available.

---

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

Based on the gaps above and the default priority order:

1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — Orient new subscribers to 30+ channels, deliver 3 curated picks matched to their interests within 48–72 hours, and drive the first active streaming session before passive churn sets in.
2. **Value Newsletter** — Weekly "New on BOP TV" content spotlight email to keep active subscribers engaged between sessions and reduce quiet churn. This also seeds Renewal/Expansion messaging.
3. **Renewal & Expansion (Monthly → Annual Upgrade)** — At the 30-day mark (before a second monthly charge), pitch the annual plan as the smarter move. Pairs with a win-back sequence for subscribers who cancel at that point.
