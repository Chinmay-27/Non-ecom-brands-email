A message from the UAF team

{{first_name}}, you are not alone in this.

Whatever brought you here — a loss too enormous to carry alone, a fear you can't stop thinking about, or the need to do something that matters — we are glad you found us. And we want you to know: thousands of families across this country have stood exactly where you are standing right now.

Fentanyl has stolen too many people we love. It has left too many empty chairs, too many nights of silence, too many families asking why. No family should have to walk through that alone — and no other family should have to go through what yours has.

That is why United Against Fentanyl exists. And now that you've joined us, here is exactly what you have access to — at no cost, with no barriers.

What you're now part of

1

Weekly peer-led grief support sessions

Every week, people who have lived through exactly what you are living through gather to share, listen, and hold space for one another. No clinical jargon. No pressure to speak. Just families who understand — because they are inside it too.
2

Monthly crisis and policy updates

We track the legislation, the supply chains, and the congressional battles so you don't have to. Each month, a curated update lands in your inbox — what's moving, what's stalled, and what you can do about it.
3

Walk for Lives — a community you can see

National awareness walks bring thousands of families together in cities across the country. The next walks are July 11 (multi-city) and September 26 in Washington, DC. You can walk, volunteer, or simply show up.

Your one step for this week

Reserve a seat at this week's trauma-informed grief support session. It's free. You don't have to speak. You don't have to have the right words. You only have to show up — and for many families, that single session is the moment everything begins to feel less impossible.

Led by families with lived experience. Not clinicians. Not strangers to this pain.

Fentanyl does not care about party lines. It does not care about zip codes or family backgrounds. It takes who it takes. And the families who are left behind deserve better than silence, better than isolation, and better than a system that hasn't caught up to the scale of this crisis.

We believe your grief can become purpose. Not because grief needs to be productive, but because the community waiting for you in that session has found something in the collective that none of us could find alone.

We are glad you're here.

— The United Against Fentanyl team
