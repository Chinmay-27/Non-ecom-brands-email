You don't have to say a word.

A lot of people register and then wonder if they're ready. You don't need to be. You don't need to share your story, speak up, or have the right words. Showing up is the whole thing.

Every person in that session has lived something close to what you're carrying. They know what it feels like to have a loss that the people around you can't fully understand — and they're not there to fix it. They're there because they understand.

What to expect

✓

Peer-led, not clinical. Sessions are facilitated by families who have experienced fentanyl loss — not therapists or government officials. It is a safe space, not a group therapy appointment.

✓

Listening is participating. Many attendees spend their first session with their camera off and their mic muted. That is completely welcome. You are still part of the circle.

✓

No agenda, no pressure. The session runs about 60 minutes. There is no required reading, no homework, no next steps. Just people who understand.

✓

Confidential. What is shared in the session stays in the session. Names and stories are not shared publicly without express, ongoing consent.
