# Brand Intake — United Against Fentanyl
**URL:** https://www.unitedagainstfentanyl.org
**Date:** 2026-06-04

---

## 1. The Offer

**What does the brand actually sell?**
United Against Fentanyl is a nonpartisan nonprofit. It does not sell a product — it operates on donations and volunteer engagement. Its "offer" to the audience is free community membership, peer grief support, policy advocacy resources, and national awareness events.

Core programs:
- **Fentanyl Families Network** — A nationwide peer support community with monthly curated email updates and weekly trauma-informed grief support sessions led by people with lived experience. Open to anyone affected by the crisis.
- **Walk for Lives** — Coordinated national awareness walks across vulnerable cities (multi-city July 11; Washington DC September 26). Participants can start a walk, volunteer, or sponsor.
- **Thought Leadership Series** — Podcast featuring public health and national security experts on fentanyl supply chains, policy, and usage.
- **State-by-State Resource Directory** — Links to help resources in all 50 states + DC, including the SAMHSA national helpline (800-662-4357).
- **Advocacy Campaigns** — Congressional action campaigns demanding bipartisan fentanyl legislation.

**Core outcome the supporter is buying:**
Turning personal grief into collective purpose. The organization offers bereaved families a community that understands their loss, a structure to channel that grief into advocacy, and the belief that their story can change policy.

**Price point & commitment:**
- All core resources: free
- Revenue model: donations (no publicly disclosed giving tiers)
- Donation types: one-time and recurring options available
- Commitment: zero barriers to join the network or attend events

**Vertical:** Education / Nonprofit / Advocacy (membership/community model)

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:**
Parents and family members who have lost a loved one to fentanyl poisoning — particularly those who lost a child or young adult (18–45 age range, per the DEA's own statistics that fentanyl is the leading killer of that cohort). They are grieving, seeking community, and motivated to prevent the same loss from happening to others. They have a high emotional stake in policy change.

**Secondary segments worth separating:**
1. **Concerned parents / community leaders** — Have not lost someone yet but are frightened by the epidemic and want to take preventative action; receptive to awareness and education content
2. **Young adults and teens** — Awareness and harm-reduction audience; need different messaging (peer-appropriate, non-preachy)
3. **Policy advocates, lawmakers, and media** — Amplification and partnership targets; respond to data-driven, nonpartisan framing
4. **Organizational partners and corporate sponsors** — Want to align with a credible nonpartisan cause; need partnership and impact-reporting content

**Problem they're in when they start looking:**
Acute grief and isolation after a fentanyl-related loss, or acute fear that a loved one is at risk. Often triggered by a news event, a personal loss, or a family member's overdose. They are searching for answers, community, and something to do with their pain.

**Where leads come from today:**
Organic search, social media shares, word-of-mouth within bereaved family networks, media coverage (DEA seizure news, congressional hearings), Walk for Lives event attendance, and podcast listeners.

**How they describe success in their own words:**
"No other family should have to go through what we went through." "I want my child's death to mean something." "Congress needs to act before more kids die."

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → engaged supporter:**
Immediate to short (days). The free, low-friction entry point (newsletter signup, network join) means there is no barrier to initial engagement. Donation conversion is a slower, trust-based arc — weeks to months of relationship-building before a first gift.

**Free trial / first-session offer:**
Yes — everything is free. The Fentanyl Families Network offers:
- Free membership with no conditions
- Weekly grief support sessions (free, trauma-informed, peer-led)
- Free event participation (Walk for Lives)
- Free resource directory access

**Who/what closes the deal:**
- For network membership: self-serve signup on the website
- For donations: emotional resonance from storytelling (spokesperson families, event participation) + direct email asks
- For advocacy actions (contacting Congress): petition campaigns and email calls to action

**#1 objection or hesitation:**
Cynicism that advocacy will produce real change — many bereaved families have been failed by the system and doubt that another nonprofit will make a difference. Secondary objection: emotional overwhelm makes sustained engagement difficult during active grief.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**The activation event:**
Attending a weekly trauma-informed grief support session OR attending/watching a Walk for Lives event and experiencing the realization that they are part of a nationwide community — thousands of families who share their loss and are channeling it into something meaningful.

**How long does it take?**
The first grief support session is the fastest path — available weekly, self-serve registration, immediate peer connection. Walk for Lives events are seasonal but deeply activating when attended.

**Is it self-serve or assisted?**
Primarily self-serve (sign up on the website); grief sessions are facilitated by peer leaders with lived experience (not professional therapists, making it accessible and non-clinical).

*This becomes the anchor of Campaign 1: Welcome & Onboarding — the email job is to get new subscribers to their first grief session or local walk.*

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?**
Engagement is recurring (weekly sessions, monthly emails, annual events). Donation revenue can be recurring if supporters opt into a recurring gift, but the organization does not appear to have a structured recurring giving program with named tiers or explicit benefits.

**Renewal / repeat cycle length:**
- Weekly: grief support sessions
- Monthly: curated email updates on crisis news and policy
- Annual: Walk for Lives events (multi-city July; DC September)

**What does "expansion" look like?**
The supporter journey escalates from passive to active:
1. Newsletter subscriber (passive)
2. Fentanyl Families Network member (community engaged)
3. Walk volunteer or organizer (active locally)
4. Recurring donor (financial commitment)
5. National advocacy spokesperson (shares story with lawmakers, media)
6. Walk city captain or board advisor (organizational leadership)

**Churn / drop-off problem:**
Emotional burnout is the primary churn risk — grief is exhausting, and sustained advocacy engagement requires ongoing motivation. Secondary risk: loss of belief that policy change is possible (especially after legislative setbacks). Supporters who joined after a spike in media attention may disengage when the news cycle moves on.

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal:**
Full lifecycle build — the organization has the community infrastructure (network, events, podcast) but appears to lack the email automation to move supporters through the journey from subscriber → engaged member → donor → advocate.

**Priority campaigns to build:**
1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — Greet new subscribers, establish credibility and mission, guide to first grief session or local walk within 7 days
2. **Lead-Magnet Nurture** — Convert network sign-ups into first-time donors and walk participants; educate on the crisis scope and legislative agenda
3. **Win-Back / Re-Engagement** — Reactivate lapsed subscribers and disengaged network members with a "the fight continues" emotional hook

*(Post-event follow-up and donor upgrade sequences are the next priority after these three.)*

**Target metric:**
Unknown baseline. Likely targeting: network membership conversion rate from newsletter, Walk for Lives registration growth, and recurring donor count.

**Brand voice / tone:**
Earnest, urgent, and deeply human. Nonpartisan ("fentanyl is not a left vs. right issue — it is a human issue"). Centers lived experience ("grief becomes purpose"). Direct calls to action paired with empathy and hope. Avoids clinical or distant language; uses real family stories. Does not exploit grief — instead honors it as fuel for change.

**Compliance constraints:**
- Nonprofit (501c3 assumed) — standard donor disclosure requirements
- Sensitive audience: grief, loss, addiction stigma require careful, trauma-informed messaging
- CAN-SPAM compliance required
- No HIPAA obligation (not a healthcare provider), but grief support content should follow mental health messaging best practices
- All advocacy content must maintain nonpartisan framing — never endorse candidates or parties
- Personal family stories used in email must be sourced from the four national family spokespersons or consented network members

**Sending volume:**
Unknown. Standard nonprofit list hygiene and frequency (2–4 sends/month for the main list; triggered sequences as needed) is a safe starting assumption.
