# Brand Intake — AlphaGraphics

**Date completed:** 2026-06-04
**Source:** AI-assisted pre-research (alphagraphics.com, franchise data, industry sources)

## 1. The Offer

**What does the brand sell?**
Full-service B2B print and marketing communications, delivered through 270+ independently owned franchise centers across 6+ countries. Core categories: graphic design, digital/offset printing, large-format graphics and signage, vehicle wraps, direct mail, promotional products, packaging, and branding/identity services. Corporate clients can also access the agEnterprise centralized online ordering portal for multi-location brand management.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:**
Professionally printed and designed marketing materials that make their business look credible, attract customers, and maintain consistent brand presentation — without needing an in-house design team or managing multiple vendors. Speed and reliability are secondary outcomes customers explicitly value.

**Price point & commitment:**
Project-based custom quotes. No public pricing. Small orders (business cards, flyers) typically $50–$500; large-format, signage, or multi-piece campaigns run $1,000–$10,000+. No subscription or retainer — revenue is repeat-project-based. Corporate and franchisor accounts may have standing purchase agreements or portal-based ordering.

**Vertical:** B2B/professional (primary) + Local & service (secondary, via locally owned franchise locations)

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:**
SMB owner or marketing manager at a local/regional business (retail, professional services, real estate, restaurant, healthcare) who needs reliable, high-quality print and marketing materials on a recurring basis. They care about quality, color accuracy, and turnaround time more than rock-bottom price.

**Secondary segments:**
- Corporate / enterprise marketing and procurement teams managing brand rollouts across multiple locations
- Franchisors needing centralized, brand-consistent materials for their franchisee network (served via the National Programs division)
- Real estate agents and brokerages (high-volume, highly predictable repeat print buyers)
- Non-profits and event organizers (periodic high-need bursts)

**Problem they're in when they start looking:**
They have a deadline — a trade show, grand opening, campaign launch, or seasonal push — and need quality materials fast. Or they've been burned by an online-only printer (wrong colors, poor quality, no support) and want a reliable local partner they can trust.

**Where leads come from today:**
Walk-ins and local awareness, referrals from other businesses, Google search ("printing near me," "sign shop," "banner printing"), direct outreach by individual franchise owners, and national/regional marketing from the corporate entity.

**How customers describe success:**
"It looked exactly like our brand." / "They turned it around in 24 hours." / "I don't have to worry about it anymore — I just call them."

---

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:** Days (same-day for simple jobs; 1–2 weeks for complex projects)

**Free trial / free consult / lead offer:**
Yes — free quote/estimate is the universal entry point at every location. Many locations also offer free design consultations. The agEnterprise portal gives corporate accounts a self-serve online ordering experience.

**Who/what closes the deal:**
Local franchise owner or sales associate. Customer submits a quote request (online form, in-person, or phone), receives a proof and price, approves it, pays, and the job enters production. Repeat customers often bypass the formal quote stage and call directly.

**#1 objection before buying:**
Price relative to commodity online printers (Vistaprint, Canva Print, Moo). Customers who don't know AlphaGraphics assume they're paying a premium without understanding the quality, turnaround, color accuracy, and local service differential.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**Activation event:**
Receiving the first finished product — business cards, banner, signage, or marketing kit — and seeing that quality and brand accuracy exceed what they could get from an online-only printer, delivered on time and without hassle.

**Time to aha:** 1–5 business days from order placement (varies by product complexity). Same-day available for select products.

**Self-serve or assisted:** Assisted — local franchise owner and design team manage the process end-to-end.

*This is the anchor of Campaign 1: Welcome & Onboarding. The email sequence should set expectations for the production process, build anticipation, then follow up post-delivery to capture the satisfaction moment and introduce the next product category.*

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?**
Not by subscription, but highly repeat-purchase. SMBs reorder on predictable cycles. Corporate accounts may have standing orders or portal-based recurring purchasing. The franchise model means the local owner is the primary relationship holder.

**Renewal / repeat cycle length:**
- Business cards / stationery: every 3–6 months
- Seasonal marketing materials (holiday, back-to-school, trade show): annually
- Direct mail campaigns: monthly for active marketers
- Event signage: ad hoc / project-by-project

**Expansion:**
Customers who start with one product category (e.g., business cards) are typically unaware of the full service range. Cross-selling to adjacent categories — signage, vehicle wraps, direct mail, promotional products — is the primary expansion lever. Corporate accounts expand through more locations and more product types via agEnterprise.

**Churn / drop-off problem:**
One-and-done customers who place a single order and disappear — either because they don't think of AlphaGraphics for the next need, or they try a cheaper online option. Also at risk: accounts whose primary contact (the local franchise owner) changes, breaking the established relationship.

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal this quarter:** Full lifecycle build — every backbone campaign is a gap; priority should follow the default order with a twist toward retention given the repeat-purchase model.

**Target metric & baseline:**
- Repeat purchase rate (reorder within 6 months of first order)
- Quote-to-close conversion rate from the online form
- Reactivation rate of dormant accounts (6+ months no order)
- Baseline: unknown — to confirm on kickoff call.

**Brand voice / tone:**
Professional, confident, and locally warm. AlphaGraphics speaks like a trusted neighborhood expert with big-brand capabilities. Copy should be solution-focused, jargon-light, and emphasize speed, quality, and partnership. Not corporate-stiff; not discount-driven. Peer-to-peer B2B register — "we get it, here's how we help" energy.

**Compliance constraints:**
Standard CAN-SPAM / CASL. Franchise structure means list ownership may be split between corporate and location-level — list segmentation and unsubscribe handling must be managed carefully per source. No healthcare, financial, or legal content restrictions.

**Sending volume:**
Unknown at national level; franchise network scale suggests significant aggregate list potential. To confirm per campaign scope (corporate-only vs. location-level deployment).

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — for new quote-requesters and first-time order customers; set production-process expectations, build anticipation, deliver the aha, then introduce the full service range before the first reorder window opens.
2. **Renewal & Reorder Reminder** — triggered at 60–90 days post-order (product-type dependent); recapture the repeat purchase before the customer defaults to an online option.
3. **Win-Back / Re-Engagement** — for accounts with no order in 6+ months; remind them of past quality and give them a compelling reason to return (new product category, seasonal hook, or local-owner touch).
