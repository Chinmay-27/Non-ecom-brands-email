## 1. The Offer

**What they sell:**  
A suite of done-for-you (DFY) and DIY lead generation programs for local small businesses:
- **Yelp Help™** — Yelp profile and review management
- **Review Tap™** — Active review generation system
- **Birthday Marketing Formula™** — Birthday-triggered customer engagement campaigns
- **Coupon Club™** — Coupon-based promotions to drive repeat visits
- **Email & SMS marketing** — Managed or self-serve list communication
- **Digital display advertising** — Local digital ad placements

**Core outcome the customer is buying:**  
An owned buyer list and a repeatable system for getting customers to come back — without depending on walk-ins, word-of-mouth, or expensive agencies.

**Price point & commitment:**  
Exact pricing not published. Positioned as affordable for "any budget." No long-term contracts; month-to-month. Free trial offered on most/all programs — clients commit financially only after seeing results.

**Vertical:** Local & service (with SaaS/software-delivered program elements)

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:**  
Local small business owner — restaurants, retail boutiques, personal service businesses (salons, spas, fitness studios), and home service/trades — who is frustrated with expensive marketing agencies and has no reliable system for building a customer list.

**Secondary segments worth separating:**
- Food & beverage / restaurants (strong birthday marketing fit)
- Personal services (salon/spa — review and loyalty programs fit well)
- Home services / trades (review generation is highest-value lever)

**Problem they're in when they start looking:**  
They've wasted money on an agency, have no repeat customer system, and feel like marketing is complicated and out of reach for a small operation.

**Where leads come from:**  
Primarily organic search and word-of-mouth referrals; founder outreach. Website CTAs direct to phone and email.

**How customers describe success:**  
"More customers coming back," "people actually finding us online," "I finally have a list I own."

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:** Days to 1–2 weeks (phone/email consultation, free trial start)

**Free trial / first offer:** Yes — free trial on most programs, no upfront cost, explicit "try before you buy" positioning across all services.

**Who/what closes the deal:** Dr. Jay Herrera directly via phone (956-203-9533) or email (Jay@LeadStoke.com). Founder-led, high-touch, consultative close.

**#1 objection before buying:** "Marketing is too complicated and I've already been burned by agencies." Secondary: "I don't have time to manage another tool."

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**Activation event:** First tangible result from the program — a new online review comes in, a birthday coupon gets redeemed, a new customer contacts the business through the list. This is what converts a free-trial user to a paying client.

**How long / self-serve or assisted:** Typically within the first week or two of the trial. DFY programs are largely assisted by LeadStoke; DIY is self-serve with guidance.

*This is the anchor for Campaign 1: Welcome & Onboarding — bridge the gap between trial start and first result.*

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Recurring?** Yes — ongoing monthly programs (review management, birthday marketing, coupon club, SMS/email).

**Renewal/repeat cycle:** Monthly. No annual contracts mentioned.

**Expansion:** Cross-sell additional programs. A client who starts with Review Tap™ can add Birthday Marketing Formula™, then Coupon Club™. Each program is additive, not replacing.

**Churn / drop-off risk:** Clients who don't see a quick early win during the trial phase; business owners who don't allocate minimal time for DFY follow-up; seasonal businesses going quiet.

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal:** More conversions — specifically converting free-trial users to paying monthly clients, and expanding single-program clients to multi-program.

**Target metric:** Trial-to-paid conversion rate; number of active monthly programs per client.

**Brand voice / tone:** Warm, plain-spoken, founder-led. Empathetic to the small business owner's frustration. Anti-jargon, anti-corporate. Reads like advice from a fellow business owner who figured it out — not a pitch from a marketing agency. Conversational, direct, reassuring.

**Compliance constraints:** CAN-SPAM and TCPA apply (email + SMS services). No healthcare, finance, or legal vertical constraints evident. Standard unsubscribe/opt-out required.

**Sending volume:** Unknown — to be confirmed based on list size.

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

Based on the gaps above and the free-trial-first sales model:

1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — Activate new trial users; guide them to their first result before the trial window closes
2. **Trial-to-Paid Conversion** — Convert engaged trial users to paying monthly clients with social proof, objection-handling, and a clear next step
3. **Lead-Magnet / Free-Trial Nurture** — Warm nurture for leads who signed up for a trial but haven't fully engaged yet (the hesitant segment)

*(If a referral program exists or is easy to add, Campaign 8: Referral/Advocacy is a natural 4th given the founder-trust dynamic.)*
