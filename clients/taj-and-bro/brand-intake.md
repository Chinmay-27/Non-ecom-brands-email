## 1. The Offer

**What they sell:** Labor-only moving services — loading, unloading, and specialized piano moving. This is a service business, not a product. The customer supplies the truck (rented from U-Haul, Penske, etc.); Taj & Bro supplies the skilled, experienced crew.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:** A damage-free, physically effortless move at a fraction of full-service mover pricing. For piano customers specifically: peace of mind that a 500-lb instrument won't be destroyed or dropped.

**Tagline / positioning line:** *"You Rent the Truck. We Lift."* (derived from their homepage copy: "WE PROVIDE ELITE HEAVY-LIFTING AND SPECIALIZED PIANO MOVING SERVICES SINCE 2011. YOU RENT THE TRUCK, WE LIFT.")

**Price point & commitment:** Quote-based hourly labor. No public pricing. NJ market benchmark is ~$100–150/hr for a 2-person crew. One-off transaction per job; no subscription or retainer. Commercial and storage clients may book repeatedly.

**Vertical:** ☑ Local & service

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:** NJ/NY metro area homeowners and renters who have already rented a moving truck and need professional labor to load/unload it — and piano owners (private, schools, churches) needing specialty moves.

**Secondary segments:**
- Commercial clients: offices, storage facilities, retail — recurring needs
- Realtors or property managers who stage/move furniture regularly
- Real estate investors (property turnover)

**Problem they're in when they start looking:**
- Move date is days away; they've rented a truck but realize they can't do the heavy lifting safely
- They own a piano and every generic mover they've called doesn't know how to handle it
- A previous DIY move resulted in injury or damage and they won't make that mistake again

**Where leads come from:** U-Haul MovingHelp marketplace (confirmed listing), Nextdoor (confirmed listing), Google search, word of mouth, direct referrals.

**How customers describe success:** *"Everything showed up in one piece," "They were fast and professional," "Way easier than doing it ourselves," "The piano didn't have a scratch."*

---

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:** Same day to a few days. Move dates are usually imminent when the customer starts searching — this is an urgency-driven purchase.

**Free trial / low-risk entry:** Free quote via phone call `(908) 457-3846` or online quote form. Low friction, no commitment.

**Who/what closes the deal:** Phone call. The quote conversation is the sales conversation — price + available date confirmed verbally.

**#1 objection before buying:**
> *"Can I actually trust strangers to handle my piano / expensive furniture without breaking something? And will they actually show up?"*

Secondary objection: *"Maybe I can just ask friends to help for free."*

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**The activation event:** The crew shows up on time on moving day, works efficiently without being directed, and every item — especially the piano — arrives undamaged. The customer exhales for the first time all day.

**Time to aha:** Immediate — happens during the job itself (hours, not days).

**Self-serve or assisted:** Fully assisted. The customer's only job is to have the truck ready.

*This anchors Campaign 1: Welcome & Onboarding — pre-move prep emails that reduce anxiety, set expectations, and reduce no-shows.*

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?** Mostly no for residential (people move infrequently). Yes for commercial clients and storage facilities.

**Repeat cycle:** Residential: 1–5 years between moves. Commercial: monthly or quarterly depending on client type.

**Expansion looks like:**
- Booking an extra mover or extra hours day-of
- Returning for a second move (new apartment, office relocation)
- Referring friends, family, or colleagues who are moving
- Piano owners recommending to other musicians, schools, churches

**Churn / drop-off problem:**
- One-time residential nature means most customers don't return organically
- Platform-dependent bookings (U-Haul MovingHelp) bypass the brand's own list entirely
- No structured referral or review-ask process = lost word-of-mouth velocity
- Quote requests that don't convert same-day are likely abandoned with no follow-up

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal:** More conversions/bookings (quote → confirmed job) and better referral velocity.

**Target metrics:** Quote-to-booking conversion rate; repeat/referral bookings as % of total.

**Brand voice / tone:** Bold, direct, confident. Premium positioning ("elite," "precision," "artisanal care") but grounded in blue-collar pride. Short sentences. No corporate filler. Occasional swagger is on-brand.

**Compliance constraints:** Standard CAN-SPAM. No HIPAA, financial, or legal verticals. Straightforward service business.

**Sending volume:** Unknown list size — likely low volume to start. Standard ESP deliverability practices sufficient.

---

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

Per the default priority (everything missing), and adjusted for the urgency-driven, quote-close sales cycle:

1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — post-booking confirmation + pre-move prep sequence (reduces no-shows, sets expectations, builds trust before the job)
2. **Trial/Consult Conversion (Quote Nurture)** — 2–3 email sequence for quote requests that don't book same-day; address trust objections, show social proof, create urgency around date availability
3. **Booking, Reminders & No-Show** — day-before and day-of reminders, logistics checklist, what-to-expect; also triggers the post-move review ask and referral nudge
