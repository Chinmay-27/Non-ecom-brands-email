# Brand Intake — HouseBuildr

**URL:** https://www.housebuildr.com  
**Location:** 7800 Wilshire Blvd, Beverly Hills, CA | (818) 961-3285 | info@housebuildr.com  
**License:** CSLB# 1025901  
**Founded:** 2014 (expanded to roofing/home improvement 2016)  
**Scale:** 50 employees, 1000+ installations, 10+ years experience

---

## 1. The Offer

**What they sell:** Residential and commercial solar installation (primary), plus solar panel maintenance, HVAC, roofing, and windows services. Full home energy and improvement ecosystem.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:** Dramatically lower (or eliminated) monthly electricity bills + energy independence, while capturing the 30% federal tax credit before it expires. The pitch is financial ROI + environmental pride.

**Price point & commitment:**
- High-ticket, custom-quoted — residential solar projects typically run $15,000–$30,000+ before incentives
- One-time installation fee; recurring maintenance contracts available
- No apparent subscription tier; expansion happens through additional service purchases (HVAC, roofing, etc.)

**Vertical:** Local & service (solar/home improvement contractor, California)

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:** California homeowners, ages 35–65, upper-middle to affluent income, in the Beverly Hills / greater Los Angeles market. Environmentally conscious and financially motivated — they want lower bills and feel good about clean energy.

**Secondary segments:**
- Commercial property owners and small businesses seeking cost reduction and ESG credibility
- Whole-home improvement buyers who bundle solar + roofing + HVAC + windows into one project

**Problem they're in when they start looking:**
- Frustrated by some of the highest utility rates in the US (CA electricity costs)
- Overwhelmed by the complexity of going solar — permits, financing, equipment, installer trust
- Deadline-motivated by expiring federal tax credit incentives

**Lead sources (inferred):** Paid search (Google Ads for "solar installer Los Angeles"), referrals from past customers, contractor networks, possibly home improvement events or community outreach. SEO likely plays a role given their blog/content infrastructure.

**How they describe success:** "My bill is almost nothing now," "I'm finally making money from my roof," "I wished I'd done it sooner."

---

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:** Weeks to months. Solar is a considered purchase — requires site visit, energy audit, custom proposal, permit approval, financing decisions, and then installation scheduling.

**Free offer / trial:** Free site assessment and custom quote (standard in the solar industry; strongly implied by their CTAs and sales model). No formal trial product — service begins at contract signing.

**Who/what closes the deal:** Solar sales representative + site assessment + custom proposal + financing options presentation. High-touch, human-led close.

**#1 objection or hesitation:** Upfront cost and ROI uncertainty — "That's a lot of money — how long until I break even?" Secondary objection: distrust of the installer's quality and post-install support.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**Activation event:** First utility bill received after the solar system goes live — typically showing near-zero or dramatically reduced charges (or even a credit from net metering). This lands 30–90 days post-installation.

**Self-serve or assisted:** Fully assisted — customer has no DIY involvement in installation. The aha moment is passive (they receive the bill) rather than an action they take.

**Implication for onboarding emails:** The welcome/onboarding sequence must bridge the gap between contract signing and that first bill — setting expectations, explaining what's happening during permitting and installation, building anticipation, and delivering a "congratulations, your system is live" moment to prime the emotional payoff.

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?** Partially. Core installation is one-time; maintenance contracts and service visits create recurring revenue. Expansion (HVAC, roofing, windows) adds one-time upsell events.

**Renewal / repeat cycle:** Annual maintenance check-ups; system performance monitoring year-round. No formal membership model observed.

**Expansion looks like:** Upselling existing solar customers to roofing replacement, HVAC upgrades, or window replacements — framed as a whole-home efficiency play. Battery storage (e.g., Tesla Powerwall) would be a natural adjacency.

**Churn / drop-off problem:**
- Post-install disengagement — once installed, customers rarely hear from the company until something breaks
- No apparent re-engagement of warm leads who received a quote but didn't convert
- Risk of losing maintenance business to cheaper independent solar service providers

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal (inferred):** More conversions/bookings (quote → signed contract) and better retention/expansion of existing customer base. The tax credit deadline has passed, so urgency messaging needs a new hook.

**Target metrics:** Quote-to-contract conversion rate; site assessment show-up rate; maintenance contract attachment rate.

**Brand voice / tone:** Professional yet warm and approachable. Forward-thinking, sustainability-driven, community-oriented ("Build Better, Together"). Avoids hard-sell tactics; positions as a trusted partner. Copy should be confident and educational — explaining complexity clearly without talking down to the reader.

**Compliance constraints:**
- California contractor license (CSLB# 1025901) — must not make unlicensed claims
- Federal and state incentive claims must be accurate and current (the 30% ITC claim needs to be updated for 2026 IRA schedules)
- Standard CAN-SPAM / email marketing compliance
- No HIPAA or financial services regulation

**Sending volume:** Unknown; recommend starting conservatively (1–2 emails/week per active sequence) given unknown list quality.

---

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

Based on the gaps above and the default priority for a service business with a free-consult motion:

1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — nurture new quote/inquiry leads through the site assessment → proposal → contract journey; bridge the installation waiting period for signed customers
2. **Trial/Consult Conversion** — convert free-quote recipients who haven't booked or haven't signed; address cost objection and ROI uncertainty directly
3. **Booking, Reminders & No-Show** — automate site assessment appointment confirmations, day-before reminders, and post-no-show recovery to protect pipeline
