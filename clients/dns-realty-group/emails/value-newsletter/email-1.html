What this actually means for you

If you're selling

With only 1.4 months of supply, well-priced homes are moving fast — often with multiple offers. Pricing right on day one is more important than ever; overprice and you'll sit while correctly-priced neighbors close in under three weeks.

If you're buying

Tight inventory means you'll need to move decisively. Get pre-approved before you start touring so you can write a clean offer the moment you find the right home. Waiting a week to "think about it" can mean losing it.

 

This Month in Charles County

Waldorf & La Plata: a closer look

Waldorf continues to be the engine of Charles County activity. Move-up buyers from Prince George's County are still crossing the line looking for more space at a better price point — and finding it, though the gap is narrowing. La Plata's historic district homes are seeing renewed interest from remote workers who prioritize walkability and a smaller-town feel over commute time.

One thing I'm watching: the new Route 301 corridor improvements. When infrastructure improves, values tend to follow within 12–18 months. Homeowners in that path who've been on the fence about selling might want to think about their timing sooner rather than later.

 

One Thing Worth Knowing This Month

Maryland's HomeCredit program is still active. If you know a first-time buyer — a family member, coworker, neighbor — this federal tax credit can be worth up to $2,000 per year for the life of their mortgage. Most buyers don't know it exists. Pass this along.
