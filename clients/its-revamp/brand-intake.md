# Brand Intake — Its Revamp (Revamp | Digital Revenue Engines)

**Vertical:** B2B / Professional Services — Digital Marketing Agency  

---

## 1. The Offer

**What does the brand actually sell?**  
An integrated, outcomes-based digital marketing retainer branded as a "Digital Revenue Engine." Rather than selling SEO, web design, or PPC as standalone services, Revamp packages them into a unified system designed to generate consistent, measurable leads and revenue for local/regional businesses. Core components: SEO-optimized website design, local and national SEO, lead generation, PPC advertising, social media marketing, and content marketing.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:**  
Predictable, consistent leads and a robust sales pipeline — market leadership expressed as revenue, not rankings.

**Price point & commitment:**  
- Custom pricing (not publicly disclosed)  
- Web design entry project: estimated $5,000–$25,000+ (one-off)  
- Ongoing retainer: estimated $1,500–$5,000+/month (recurring)  
- Vertical: ☑ **B2B/professional**

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:**  
Local and regional business owners in competitive service verticals — law firms, HVAC, roofing, plumbing, tree services, and professional services — primarily in San Antonio / Texas but also nationwide. Owner-operated or small-team businesses that need leads and can't afford to manage digital marketing in-house.

**Secondary segments worth separating:**
1. Home-service contractors (HVAC, roofers, plumbers) — high search volume, seasonal urgency, price-sensitive but high lifetime value
2. Legal / professional services (law firms, accountants, consultants) — high ticket, reputation-sensitive, longer trust cycle
3. Healthcare / med-spa / wellness — local competition, trust-driven, may have compliance sensitivities
4. Restaurants and retail — Google Maps and reviews-dependent, often lower budget

**Problem they're in when they start looking:**  
Invisible on Google while competitors capture leads they should be getting. Often burned by previous agencies that took money with no measurable results.

**Where leads come from:**  
Organic search, Google Maps, referrals, and press/PR (JCE SEO has active PR/press releases). Free SEO audit serves as a primary lead-capture mechanism.

**How they describe success:**  
"My phone is ringing." "I'm getting consistent leads from Google." "I'm ranking #1 for [service] in [city]."

---

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time to close:** Days to weeks  
- Free audit/assessment → discovery call → proposal → contract

**Free entry offer:**  
- Free Google Maps ranking assessment (primary lead magnet)  
- Free SEO audit / initial consultation call

**Who/what closes:**  
Booked strategy call — founder or account executive presents audit findings and custom proposal.

**#1 objection before buying:**  
"I've hired SEO agencies before and got nothing." Trust and proof of ROI is the primary barrier — commoditization skepticism is high in this space.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**Activation event:**  
Client sees their business appear in the Google Maps 3-pack or on page 1 for a high-intent local keyword — and receives their first organic lead as a direct result.

**How long it takes:**  
60–90 days from onboarding (SEO has an inherent ramp period — managing this expectation is critical). Assisted — account manager communicates progress via reporting and check-in calls.

*This becomes the anchor of Campaign 1: Welcome & Onboarding — set the 90-day expectation, celebrate first wins as they happen.*

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Recurring?** Yes — monthly retainer for ongoing SEO, content, ads management, and performance reporting.

**Renewal/repeat cycle:** Monthly rolling or annual contract; key milestone reviews at 6 months and 12 months.

**Expansion looks like:**  
- Upsell from SEO-only to full "Revenue Engine" stack (add PPC, social, content, reputation management)  
- Add new geographic service-area campaigns  
- Scale ad spend as ROI is proven  

**Churn / drop-off risk:**  
- Slow results in first 90 days with no proactive communication  
- Mis-set expectations on SEO timeline ("I thought I'd see results in a month")  
- Lack of visible progress reporting  
- Competitor poaching with lower-price offer

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal:** Likely conversion of free audit leads → paying retainer clients, plus retention of existing clients.

**Target metric:** Booked calls from lead-magnet nurture; new retainer close rate; churn rate — baselines to be confirmed on kickoff.

**Brand voice / tone:**  
Professional, authoritative, and outcome-obsessed. Speaks in revenue and ROI language — "leads," "rank #1," "grow revenue" — not marketing fluff. Confident without being bro-marketing. Consultative and strategic.

**Compliance:**  
CAN-SPAM (US B2B) — standard unsubscribe link and physical address required. No HIPAA, financial, or legal compliance triggers.

**Sending volume:** To be confirmed; list size unknown.
