Hi {{first_name}}, you received your offer a few days ago — and we want to make sure you have the full picture before you decide.

The most common reason note holders pause — and we hear this every week — is this feeling:

"If my note is worth $180,000 in payments, why would I take $130,000 now? I'd be leaving $50,000 on the table."

That feeling makes complete sense. The math looks simple — and it seems to say wait. But that comparison leaves out three things that are costing you money every single month you hold the note.

01 — Time Value of Money

A dollar today is worth more than a dollar in 2043.

$130,000 in hand right now can be invested, put to work, or used to improve your life immediately. That same $130,000 paid out in small pieces over the next 20 years loses purchasing power every single year — to inflation, to opportunity cost, and to the time you spend waiting.

When you factor in a modest 3–4% annual inflation rate, the real value of those future payments shrinks considerably — often right in line with the offer you have in hand today.

02 — Default Risk

The $180,000 assumes every payment arrives. Yours may not.

Right now, you are the lender. If your buyer loses their job, faces a medical crisis, or simply decides to stop paying, the burden of collection — and the cost of a potential foreclosure — falls on you. That's not a small risk over a 20-year window.

When you accept our offer, that risk transfers entirely to Swift. You receive a guaranteed amount and walk away. No more tracking payments, no more worrying about what happens if they miss one.

03 — What the Discount Actually Buys

You're not giving up money. You're buying certainty.

The discount Swift applies reflects our cost of capital, the risk we take on, and the time value of the money we pay you up front. It's the same calculation any lender or investor makes — and it's what allows us to hand you a lump sum today instead of asking you to wait 20 years and hope for the best.

In plain terms, here's the trade:

Holding your note

  • Payments spread over 15–25 years
  • You absorb all default risk
  • Inflation erodes real value
  • You manage collection, late notices, and disputes
  • Money arrives in small pieces, when it arrives
  

Accepting your offer

  • Full lump sum in hand at closing
  • Default risk moves to Swift — completely
  • You can put money to work immediately
  • Swift handles all paperwork and closing costs
  • Peace of mind, no more tracking payments
