# Brand Intake — Old Landmark Publishing

**Brand:** Old Landmark Publishing
**URL:** https://www.oldlandmarkpublishing.com
**Date completed:** 2026-06-04
**Research method:** Web crawl (homepage, shop, about, contact, product pages) + web search

---

## 1. The Offer

**What they sell:**
Two distinct revenue streams operating from one site:
1. **Ebook retail store** — genre-specific ebooks (religious/Christian, AI/business, cozy fantasy) priced from free to $17 (one title originally $47). Two titles are permanently free and serve as lead magnets.
2. **Author publishing services** — ebook creation & design, multi-platform distribution (Amazon Kindle, Barnes & Noble, etc.), and marketing/promotion for self-publishing authors. Custom-quoted; no public pricing.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:**
- *Readers:* An affordable, niche digital book they can't easily find in mainstream stores.
- *Authors:* Their manuscript turned into a professionally published, distributed, and marketed ebook — without having to figure out the process themselves.

**Price point & commitment:**
- Ebooks: $2.50–$17 (one-off purchase); two titles free.
- Publishing services: unknown ticket size, project-based engagement (multi-week to ongoing).

**Vertical:** Education / independent publishing — straddles B2C (reader retail) and B2B/creator (author services).

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:**
Independent or first-time authors — especially in religious/Christian, AI/business, and niche fiction genres — who want professional end-to-end publishing support without a traditional publisher.

**Secondary segments worth separating:**
| Segment | What they buy | Entry point |
|---|---|---|
| Christian / religious nonfiction readers | Low-ticket religious ebooks | Organic search, referral |
| AI / productivity seekers | AI Side Hustles, AI-Proof Your Life | Ads, social, search |
| Cozy fantasy readers | The Opal and the Rose | Organic, excerpt sample |
| Self-publishing authors | Publishing + marketing services | Contact form, referral |

**Problem they're in when they start looking:**
- Authors: "I've written a book (or want to) but have no idea how to get it published, on Amazon, and in front of readers."
- Readers: "I want an affordable ebook on [religious living / AI income / fantasy] that's not $25 from a big publisher."

**Where leads come from today:** Organic search (SEO), word-of-mouth (4.8/5 from 150 reviews indicates some referral loop), possibly social. No paid ad evidence found. 15,000+ site visitors claimed.

**How they describe success (author clients):** "My book is out there, it looks professional, and people are actually finding and buying it."

---

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:**
- Ebook buyers: same day (self-serve checkout).
- Publishing service clients: days to weeks (contact → consultation → custom plan → agreement).

**Free trial / entry offer:** Two permanently-free ebooks ("AI-Proof Your Life," "The Opal and the Rose: Excerpt") act as lead magnets. No free consult or sample publishing service explicitly advertised.

**Who/what closes the deal:**
- Ebooks: self-serve cart + checkout (Apple Pay, Google Pay, Visa, MC).
- Publishing services: contact form or live chat → team responds and "crafts a captivating plan."

**#1 objection before buying:**
- Authors: *"Will a small indie publisher actually get my book seen by readers?"* (credibility + reach concern)
- Ebook buyers: price sensitivity at the sub-$5 and $17 price points; no reviews visible on newer titles.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**For ebook readers:** Completing the free download and finding the content genuinely useful or enjoyable — ideally within the first sitting.

**For publishing service authors:** Receiving a concrete publishing plan that makes their vision feel real and the process feel managed. This is the activation event that converts an inquiry into a paying engagement.

**Self-serve or assisted?** Ebook downloads are fully self-serve. Publishing service onboarding is assisted (team-driven conversation → plan delivery).

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?** No subscription or membership model is visible. Revenue is transactional (ebook one-offs) or project-based (publishing engagements).

**Renewal / repeat cycle:** No fixed cycle. Repeat ebook purchases are opportunistic (new title releases). Publishing clients may return for marketing retainers or new projects.

**What "expansion" looks like:**
- Reader → publishing services client (cross-sell)
- Publishing client → ongoing marketing retainer
- "Business in a Box" package (mentioned on product page — a bundled digital brand-launch kit)
- "AI Literacy Series" (email upsell mentioned to buyers of the AI Side Hustles ebook)

**Churn / drop-off problem:**
- One-time ebook buyers with no email nurture vanish after purchase.
- Authors who don't see post-launch sales results have no retention path or support mechanism.
- No visible community, newsletter, or loyalty program to re-engage either segment.

## 6. Data & Tooling

**Email platform / ESP / CRM:** Unknown — site appears to run on WooCommerce (WordPress). Likely Mailchimp or a WooCommerce-native ESP; unconfirmed.

**Behavioral triggers available:** Likely basic WooCommerce triggers (purchase confirmation, abandoned cart). Unknown whether advanced behavioral triggers (page visit, inactivity, free download event) are wired up.

**Signup/form data captured:** Minimal visible capture. No newsletter pop-up or opt-in form found on homepage or shop. Free ebook downloads likely capture name + email at checkout; publishing inquiries capture via contact form.

**List size & engagement:** Unknown. No public data; 150 reviews and 15,000 visitor claims suggest a modest but real audience.

**Existing lead magnets:**
1. *AI-Proof Your Life* — free ebook (originally $17)
2. *The Opal and the Rose: A Cozy Fantasy Excerpt* — free ebook (originally $17)

---

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal (inferred):** More conversions — both ebook sales and publishing service inquiries. The free ebooks suggest lead generation intent but no nurture infrastructure exists to capitalize on it.

**Target metric (inferred):** Conversion rate from free-download subscriber to paid ebook buyer; inquiry-to-client conversion for publishing services.

**Brand voice / tone:** Professional yet warm and encouraging. Collaborative framing ("partner," "let's have a conversation"). Inspirational for authors; practical and results-focused for AI/business content; reverent for religious titles. Avoids hard-sell language.

**Compliance constraints:** Standard CAN-SPAM / commercial email. No HIPAA, financial, or legal-services restrictions. Standard digital publishing norms.

**Sending volume:** Unknown. List size not confirmed; start conservatively until engagement data is available.

---

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

Given all backbone campaigns are missing and the brand has two active lead magnets with no follow-up:

1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — triggered on free ebook download or first purchase; introduce the brand, deliver value from the lead magnet, set expectations for both reader and author audiences.
2. **Lead-Magnet Nurture** — 3–5 email sequence post-download; build trust, surface the paid ebook catalog, and soft-pitch publishing services to the author segment.
3. **Trial/Consult Conversion** — for contact-form submitters interested in publishing services; move them from curious inquiry to booked conversation and signed engagement.
