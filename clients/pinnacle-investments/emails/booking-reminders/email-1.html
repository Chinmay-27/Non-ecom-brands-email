Have these 3 things on hand before the call

Your advisor can give you a real, accurate offer in a single conversation when they can see the key facts about your note. Here's what to pull together — most of it is already in the paperwork you have at home:

1

Your original note or contract document

The promissory note, deed of trust, land contract, or seller-finance agreement. Your advisor will want to confirm the original terms — interest rate, original loan amount, and remaining term. A photo on your phone works fine if the original is in a filing cabinet.
2

Your payment history and current balance

How long has your buyer been paying, and have payments come in on time? Consistent, on-time payment history is one of the biggest factors in your offer. A bank statement, a personal ledger, or even a rough count of months and any missed payments is enough to start.
3

The property address and approximate value

The street address of the property securing the note and a rough sense of its current market value — a recent appraisal, a Zillow estimate, or simply the sale price from a few years ago. This lets your advisor factor the underlying collateral into the offer.
 

What to expect on the call

This is a straightforward conversation, not a sales pitch. Your advisor's goal is to understand your note and your situation, answer any questions you have, and outline what a written cash offer would look like. The call typically runs 20–30 minutes.

You are under no obligation at any point. If the number works for you, great — if not, there's no pressure and nothing owed. You'll continue receiving your note payments as usual until — and unless — you choose to sell.
