The 5 factors that decide what your note is worth

When a note buyer prices your note, these are the five things they examine. Understanding them now means that when you eventually receive an offer, every dollar of it will make sense.

Factor 1

Interest Rate on the Note

A higher rate means more income for the buyer — which translates directly into a higher price for you. Notes carrying above-market rates are especially valuable.

Factor 2

Original Down Payment

A larger down payment signals that the buyer had real equity at stake from day one — a strong predictor that payments will continue. Even 10–20% down meaningfully improves an offer.

Factor 3

Remaining Term

More payments remaining generally means more total value to purchase. A shorter term can also work in your favor — it reduces the buyer’s risk window and often commands a tighter discount.

Factor 4

Payer’s Credit Profile

A payer with a strong credit history and a clean payment record is the most reassuring signal a note buyer can see. It lowers the risk premium built into the offer — and raises your number.

Factor 5

The Property

The note is secured by real property, making it the ultimate backstop. Type, condition, location, and marketability all factor in. A solid, well-located property is favorable to the offer — even if other factors are average.

The Handbook goes deeper on each of these and shows how a note with average scores on four factors can still command a strong offer if one factor is especially favorable. It’s the kind of context that turns an offer from a surprising number into a fully understood one.
