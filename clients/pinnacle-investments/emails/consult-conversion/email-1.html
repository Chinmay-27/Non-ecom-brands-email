How Your Offer Was Calculated

Six factors. Each one explained.
1

Interest Rate on the Note

Notes with rates below today's market yields are less valuable to a buyer than one charging a higher rate. The gap between your note's rate and the current market yield accounts for part of the discount you see in the offer.
2

Original Down Payment

A larger down payment means the buyer had more skin in the game from the start — which makes the note safer and more valuable. Smaller down payments carry more risk, and that risk is reflected in the price.
3

Remaining Term

A note with many years left on it ties up capital for longer. The longer the wait, the more a buyer's required yield affects today's present value. Shorter remaining terms typically command better offers.
4

Payer's Credit Profile

The creditworthiness of the person making payments to you is one of the biggest drivers of risk — and therefore of value. A strong credit profile and clean payment history means more confidence in future payments.
5

Payment History

Every on-time payment is a data point that builds confidence. A clean, uninterrupted payment record increases the value of your note — sometimes significantly — because it demonstrates the payer's reliability over time.
6

Underlying Property Condition

The property is the collateral behind your note. Its condition, current market value, and loan-to-value ratio are the safety net that protects both you and the buyer if payments stop. Stronger collateral supports a stronger offer.

Your offer of {{offer_amount}} reflects all six of these factors, priced against the data on your specific note. It is a fair-market offer — not an arbitrary discount.

An Alternative Worth Knowing About

Not ready to sell the whole note? You don't have to.

A partial note sale lets you sell just a portion of your remaining payments — say, the next five years' worth — and receive a lump sum for that slice now. When those payments end, the remaining stream reverts back to you. You get cash today and still hold some of the note. Many of our clients prefer this as a first step. It's worth a conversation.
