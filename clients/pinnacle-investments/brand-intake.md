# Brand Intake — Pinnacle Investments
**URL:** https://www.pinnacle-investments.com  
**Date:** 2026-06-04  
**Vertical:** B2B/Professional — Financial Services (Mortgage Note Buying)

---

## 1. The Offer

**What they sell:** Pinnacle Investments buys seller-financed mortgage notes, trust deeds, land contracts, and promissory notes from private note holders nationwide — both performing and non-performing. They also offer partial note purchases, allowing holders to sell only a portion of their remaining payments.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:** Immediate lump-sum liquidity and complete relief from the ongoing burden of collecting monthly payments, managing paperwork, and worrying about payer default.

**Price point & commitment:** One-time transaction. No published pricing — each offer is customized based on the note's interest rate, down payment, remaining term, payer credit profile, payment history, and underlying property condition. Typical note balances range from tens to hundreds of thousands of dollars; Pinnacle buys at a discount to face value (standard in the industry). No out-of-pocket cost to the seller.

**Vertical:** ☒ B2B/Professional services (financial/real estate note transactions)

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:** Private individuals — often homeowners or real estate investors — who owner-financed a property sale and are now holding the resulting mortgage note. They're receiving monthly installment payments but want or need a lump sum instead. Skews toward retirees and older property owners; based nationwide with Arizona as likely core market.

**Secondary segments:**
- Retirees converting note income to retirement capital
- Note holders with urgent financial events: medical bills, college tuition, estate planning, tax obligations
- Experienced real estate investors wanting to redeploy capital from multiple held notes
- Accidental note holders (inherited a note or received one in a legal settlement) who want to simplify

**Problems they're in when they start looking:**
- "I need cash now and my money is stuck in this note"
- "Collecting payments every month is a hassle and I'm worried my payer will stop"
- "I don't know what my note is worth or whether I can even sell it"
- "I have a big expense coming and I can't wait 10+ years for payments"

**Where leads come from:** Likely paid search (intent-based — people searching "sell my mortgage note"), SEO on note-buying keywords, and referrals from real estate attorneys, title companies, and financial planners.

**How they describe success:** "I got a fair price, the process was easy, and I had the money in my account in two weeks."

---

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact to close:** Days to weeks. Closing typically takes 10–15 working days once an offer is accepted.

**Free trial / consult / demo:** Yes — free no-obligation note analysis and written quote are the primary entry points. The Note Holder's Handbook is a secondary educational lead magnet.

**What closes the deal:** Phone or email conversation with an advisor → customized written offer → acceptance → title company facilitated closing → funds via cashier's check or wire transfer.

**#1 objection before buying:** *"Will the discount be too steep — will I actually get a fair price?"* Secondary: *"Is this company trustworthy / legitimate?"* (note buying is a relatively obscure niche prone to skepticism).

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**Activation event:** Receiving a concrete written offer with a real dollar figure — the moment the note holder sees that liquidity is real, attainable within two weeks, and the number is acceptable.

**Timeline:** Self-serve quote request form → advisor contact within 1 business day → written offer typically within a few days of receiving note documents.

**Assisted or self-serve:** Assisted — an advisor is involved in structuring the offer.

*This is the anchor for Campaign 1 (Welcome & Onboarding): nurture the lead from quote request to offer acceptance.*

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?** Primarily one-time transactions per note. Low inherent repeat rate per individual client.

**Renewal/repeat cycle:** No standard cycle. A note holder may return if they hold multiple notes or wish to sell additional payment streams from a partially sold note.

**Expansion looks like:**
- Partial note sales — selling additional tranches of the payment stream over time
- Clients who hold multiple notes returning for subsequent transactions
- Referrals to other note holders (the highest-leverage expansion channel given 22,000+ past transactions)

**Churn / drop-off problem:** Quote prospects going cold after requesting a free analysis — receiving the offer but not accepting because they're comparison-shopping or simply not ready. This is the primary lifecycle gap.

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal:** ☒ More conversions/bookings (converting quote requesters to accepted offers) + ☒ Reactivation (re-engaging cold quote prospects)

**Target metric:** Quote-to-close conversion rate (unknown baseline); secondary: referral volume from past clients.

**Brand voice / tone:** Professional, authoritative, and reassuring. Credibility-forward — leans on 26 years in business, 22,000+ completed transactions, 8 certified planners. Plain-spoken and educational; the audience needs trust built before acting. Warm but never casual or salesy. Think: knowledgeable financial advisor, not aggressive sales rep.

**Compliance constraints:** CAN-SPAM / CASL required. Financial services context — avoid language constituting investment advice. State-specific disclosure requirements may apply to financial transaction language. Email footers should disclaim non-broker status where relevant. No HIPAA.

**Sending volume:** Unknown — to be determined based on list size.
