What you're actually paying for.

Pick up a $10 serum at CVS and read the label. You'll find "Aqua" (water) listed first — that's fine, most formulas need it. But look further: synthetic emulsifiers, preservative cocktails, petroleum-derived fillers, fragrance compounds that don't have to disclose what they're actually made of.

Those fillers aren't dangerous, exactly. But they're doing nothing for your skin. They're padding the formula — thickening it, making it look lush, extending shelf life — while the actual active ingredients get diluted toward the bottom of the list.

When you buy our $26 serum, every dollar goes toward a named botanical ingredient you can look up, trace, and pronounce. Not toward a thickener. Not toward a preservative system designed for a five-year shelf life in a warehouse. Toward the thing that actually touches your skin.
