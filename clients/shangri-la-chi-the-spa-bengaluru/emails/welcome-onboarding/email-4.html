The names on our treatment menu carry the weight of ancient traditions — and we know they can feel a little distant when you're simply looking for the right hour of relief.

So consider this a quiet conversation about what each ritual actually does, and — more usefully — what it might do for you.
 

The rituals

Signature

CHI Signature Massage

A seamless weave of three traditions — Swedish long strokes ease the surface tension, Shiatsu pressure points release what lies deeper, and Reflexology maps its way through the feet to restore balance throughout the body. The result is difficult to describe but easy to feel: a thorough unknotting that reaches places a single technique simply cannot.

Good for: Held tension across the shoulders and back  ·  End-of-week exhaustion  ·  Your first visit to CHI

Ayurvedic

Abhyanga

The word means "loving touch." In Ayurveda, warm oil applied in long, rhythmic strokes along the body's natural channels is understood to move stagnant energy, nourish the tissues, and draw the nervous system down from its high-alert state. It is slow, intentional, and deeply warming — nothing about it is in a hurry.

Good for: A body that has been running too fast for too long  ·  Dry skin and a restless mind  ·  Those new to Ayurveda

Ayurvedic

Shirodhara

A narrow, continuous stream of warm herbal oil pours gently across the forehead — steady, unhurried, meditative. It asks nothing of you except stillness. Many guests describe the moments afterward as the closest thing they have found to truly switching off: a clarity that arrives quietly, without warning. It is our most contemplative ritual.

Good for: A mind that won't slow down  ·  Disrupted sleep  ·  Those who want something they have never tried

Japanese

Shiatsu

Shiatsu means "finger pressure." Rooted in Japanese bodywork, the technique works along the body's meridian lines — sustained, firm compressions applied by thumb, palm, and elbow that coax the body to release tension it has been holding for weeks, sometimes months. It is not always gentle, but guests consistently leave feeling taller.

Good for: Stubborn muscle tightness  ·  Physical fatigue that rest alone won't fix  ·  Those who prefer firm pressure

Ayurvedic

Pinda Sweda

Boluses of rice cooked in warm herbal milk are pressed and rolled against the body in steady, circular movements. The warmth penetrates quickly, the rhythm is deeply lulling, and the herbal properties nourish as they work. It is a treatment that makes itself felt for days afterward — a sustained ease rather than an immediate release.

Good for: Tiredness in the muscles  ·  Anyone seeking warmth and deep nourishment  ·  The colder months

Before you arrive

Come a little early.

Fifteen to thirty minutes before your treatment, the hydrothermal wing on the third floor is yours — steam room, sauna, Jacuzzi. These are not afterthoughts. They are the preparation. Warmth opens the body, quiets the mind, and allows whatever comes next to go deeper.

Your therapist will conduct a short health consultation when you arrive. It takes only a few minutes and ensures that everything — the oils selected, the pressure applied, the pace of the session — is suited to you that day.

When you feel ready to choose, the full menu is waiting for you — with everything from a sixty-minute introduction to a longer Shirodhara session that asks only that you bring yourself and leave everything else at the door.
