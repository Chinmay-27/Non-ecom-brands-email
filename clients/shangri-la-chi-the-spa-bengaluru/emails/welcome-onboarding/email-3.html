The question we hear most often
from people considering their first visit.

{{first_name}}, we know what you may be weighing.

A hotel spa sounds beautiful — perhaps too beautiful. But is it real? Are the therapists trained with the same depth as a specialist? Or is the setting simply doing most of the work?

It is a fair question. And the honest answer is what makes CHI different from almost everything else in the city.

 

A standard that travels

CHI, The Spa is not a Bengaluru-specific idea. It is a globally calibrated wellness philosophy — present in Shangri-La properties across Asia, the Middle East, Europe and the Americas — held to the same training standard in each. The techniques practised here are not an approximation of ancient Asian healing disciplines. They are those disciplines: Shiatsu pressure-point work, Swedish therapeutic flow, Reflexology mapped to the body’s meridians, Ayurvedic traditions refined across centuries.

Every therapist at CHI completes a structured programme tied to the global CHI standard. They are not generalists who have memorised a menu. They understand the philosophy of touch — and the difference between a body held in tension and one that is simply waiting to release it.

 

What the price truly holds

When people pause at the cost of a session, we understand. But consider what that figure actually contains.

It begins well before you lie down: a thorough pre-treatment health consultation, the quiet of the third floor above the city, the choice of oriental aromatic oils, and unhurried time in the steam room, sauna and Jacuzzi. The treatment itself is not timed to the minute, not shared with ambient noise from the next room. The hour is yours — genuinely, entirely yours.

And when it is finished, you are not asked to leave. You are invited to rest.
