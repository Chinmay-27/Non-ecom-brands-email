# Brand Intake — CHI, The Spa at Shangri-La Bengaluru

**Completed:** 2026-06-02
**Source:** Web research (official site, LBB editorial, SKChase voucher platform, search results)

---

## 1. The Offer

**What does the brand sell?**
Appointment-based luxury spa treatments at a 5-star hotel spa. Menu includes CHI Signature Massages (a proprietary blend of Swedish, Shiatsu, and Reflexology), Ayurvedic therapies (Abhyanga, Shirodhara, Pinda Sweda, Udwartana), facials, body treatments, head/foot massages, and hydrothermal facility access (steam room, sauna, Jacuzzi). Pre-purchase bundle vouchers are a secondary product. Gift e-vouchers available via the SKChase platform.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:**
Not relaxation as a commodity — *holistic healing*. A genuine mental and physical reset rooted in Asian wellness traditions, in an environment that makes you feel elevated before a single treatment begins.

**Price point & commitment:**
- 60-min treatment: ~INR 7,316
- 90-min Shankara collaboration package: ~INR 9,200 + taxes
- Shirodhara: ~INR 14,333
- Bundle: 7 treatments for the price of 5 (~INR 56,050 total)
- **One-off bookings** (no subscription or membership); bundles are the only pre-commitment product
- Premium tier — comparable to top standalone spas in Bengaluru

**Vertical:** Local & service (luxury hospitality sub-vertical)

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:**
Affluent Bengaluru professional, 28–55, who already consumes premium experiences (fine dining, business class, premium fitness). Chronically stressed, time-poor, values *quality* over novelty. Chooses CHI because the Shangri-La brand signals authenticity and global standard, not a gimmick.

**Secondary segments worth separating:**
1. **In-stay hotel guests** (domestic + international business/leisure) — highest intent, lowest booking friction, influenced by concierge
2. **Local residents / non-guests** — plan in advance, must arrive by 10pm; a more deliberate purchase decision
3. **Couples** — dedicated couples' rooms available; anniversary, birthday, Valentine's occasion buyers
4. **Corporate / gifting buyers** — HR incentive, client gifting, team wellness; buy vouchers in bulk
5. **Shangri-La Circle loyalty members** — returning hotel guests with existing brand affinity

**Problem they're in when they start looking:**
Work-related stress and muscle fatigue; desire to "do something nice for myself" around a life occasion; guests looking for a hotel activity that feels genuinely indulgent rather than generic.

**Where leads come from today:**
Hotel concierge referrals (in-stay), organic search, Tripadvisor discovery, word of mouth, Shangri-La Circle email/app, social media (Instagram aspirational content), LBB / Curly Tales editorial.

**How they describe success:**
"I walked out feeling like a different person." / "Worth every rupee." / "We didn't want to leave."

---

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → booking:**
Same day to 2–3 days for in-stay guests. Local non-guests may plan 1–2 weeks ahead for an occasion.

**Free trial / first-session offer:**
No free trial. Friction is reduced by gift vouchers and bundle deals (buy 5 get 2 free) that lower the per-treatment cost. Seasonal offers (Festive Spa Getaway, Shankara collaboration) act as a conversion hook for fence-sitters.

**Who/what closes the deal:**
Self-serve online booking via SpaTime platform, or phone/email direct to the spa. Hotel concierge is a high-trust referral channel for in-stay guests.

**#1 objection before buying:**
Price justification — spending INR 7K+ on a single session requires a deliberate decision. The secondary objection is trust: *"Is a hotel spa really as good as a specialist?"* The CHI brand heritage and authentic Asian treatment philosophy directly overcome this.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**Activation event:**
Completing their first treatment — specifically that moment of emergence from the treatment room, unhurried, grounded, restored. But the experience begins the moment they walk in: the pre-treatment ritual (arriving 15–30 min early, health consultation, zen 3rd-floor environment, oriental aromatic oils) primes the aha before the hands-on work begins.

**Self-serve or assisted?**
Assisted. A therapist guides the entire session. Health consultation is completed on arrival.

*This is the anchor for Campaign 1: Welcome & Onboarding / Booking Confirmation nurture — the pre-visit email should begin setting the sensory expectation before the guest arrives.*

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?**
Not formally. Revenue is repeat-booking driven. No subscription or membership product exists (Shangri-La Circle is a hotel loyalty program, not a spa membership).

**Repeat cycle:**
Varies — monthly for dedicated local wellness regulars; quarterly or occasion-triggered for most guests.

**What does expansion look like?**
- Upsell from 60 → 90 min sessions
- Add-on services (scrub + massage combos, Shirodhara add-on)
- Bundle voucher purchase (7-for-5 pack)
- Couples' room bookings
- Gift vouchers (advocacy + new customer acquisition simultaneously)
- Exclusive collaboration packages (e.g., Shankara Sandalwood)

**Churn / drop-off problem:**
- No re-booking trigger or lapsed-guest sequence observed
- No-show risk is acute: 100% charge policy can create friction/resentment if guest forgets or misses the 4-hour cancellation window
- Local non-guests have no formal loyalty loop outside Shangri-La Circle
- Competition from premium standalone spas (Kama Ayurveda, Six Senses, Tattva, Forest Essentials spa concepts)

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Reasonable primary goal:** More conversions/bookings (activate the large local non-guest segment) + better retention (re-booking frequency) — essentially Campaigns 4, 6, and 7 are the highest-leverage gaps.

**Target metrics:**
- Booking conversion rate from email CTAs (unknown baseline)
- No-show rate reduction (currently punished at 100% — email reminders should move this)
- Re-booking rate / inter-visit interval (unknown baseline)

**Brand voice / tone:**
Sophisticated, serene, and sensory-rich. Unhurried. Draws on CHI philosophy (universal life force, Asian healing wisdom). Never salesy or urgent — *an invitation to arrive*, not a push notification. Closest references: Aesop, Banyan Tree, ESPA. Uses evocative sensory language (scent, warmth, stillness, breath). Avoids clinical or functional wellness-speak.

**Compliance constraints:**
- CAN-SPAM / GDPR / India DPDPA
- Health consultation data is sensitive — do not reference specific health conditions or personalize on health data in emails
- Cancellation / no-show policy must appear in transactional emails — frame warmly, not as a threat
- Adult-only facility — no child targeting

**Sending volume:** Unknown. Likely conservative (Shangri-La brand protects send frequency to avoid list fatigue).

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

Based on gaps in §7 and the booking-driven nature of this business:

1. **Campaign 4 — Booking Confirmation + Reminders & No-Show Prevention** *(highest urgency: protects revenue, reduces no-shows, primes the aha moment before arrival)*
2. **Campaign 1 — Welcome & Onboarding** *(nurtures new local subscribers / first-time bookers into repeat guests)*
3. **Campaign 6 — Renewal, Anniversary & Expansion** *(re-booking nudge + occasion-triggered upsell — highest ROI for an existing happy guest base)*

Campaign 7 (Win-Back) is the fourth priority once the above three are live.
