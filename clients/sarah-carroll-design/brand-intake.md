## 2. The Audience

**Primary customer / ICP:**
Small business owners, nonprofit directors, and sports club administrators within the greater Boston, MA area who need professional-quality design but lack an in-house designer. Often busy operators who want to delegate creative work entirely.

**Secondary segments:**
- Nonprofits needing event brochures, save-the-dates, and campaign collateral (e.g., Living Art Boston, Mass Health Policy Forum)
- Corporations needing infographics and presentations (e.g., Pearson)
- Sports clubs needing team branding and promotional materials (Youk's Kids)
- Educational publishers needing instructional design
- Small businesses needing a logo or website from scratch (e.g., Bridge Property Services, Boston Eyeworks)

**Problem they're in when they start looking:**
They look unprofessional, inconsistent, or DIY — and it's costing them credibility. Or they have a launch coming up (event, product, rebrand) and no time to figure out design.

**Where leads come from today:**
Primarily inbound via website contact form. Social presence on Instagram, Facebook, Behance, and LinkedIn suggests organic social discovery. Likely significant word-of-mouth/referral given the Boston-area focus and community-oriented client base.

**How they describe success in their own words:**
"It looks exactly like us." "People keep asking who designed our logo." "We finally look legit." The emotional result matters as much as the functional one.

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

Everything is missing, so we apply the default priority order:

1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — set the stage after a contact form inquiry; build trust, explain the process, and warm up the lead before the scoping call
2. **Trial/Consult Conversion** — nurture inquiries that haven't booked yet; address cost and trust objections; move them from "interested" to "signed"
3. **Referral / Advocacy** — immediately after project delivery, while the client is delighted; a structured ask for testimonials and referrals is the highest-leverage growth move for a solo studio with no list

*(Note: Campaign 2 (Lead-Magnet Nurture) is deprioritized because no lead magnet exists yet. A simple design tips PDF or "5 signs your logo needs a refresh" guide would unlock this channel — recommend building the lead magnet alongside Campaign 3.)*
