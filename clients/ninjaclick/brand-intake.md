# Brand Intake — NinjaClick
**URL:** https://www.ninjaclick.com
**Date:** 2026-06-04
**Vertical:** SaaS / Visitor Intelligence

---

## 1. The Offer

**What does the brand actually sell?**
A SaaS subscription platform. One line of JavaScript installed on a website identifies up to 80% of anonymous visitors — revealing company details and decision-maker contact information — without requiring forms or cookies. Customers subscribe for ongoing monthly access to identified visitor data.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:**
Stop watching traffic leave. Know *who* is on your site right now and reach them before they disappear — turning anonymous traffic into a contactable pipeline.

**Price point & commitment:**
- Free tier: 100 identities/month (permanent, no card)
- Growth: $97/month — unlimited identities at entry scale
- Scale: $497/month — up to 10,000 identities
- Enterprise: $997/month — up to 50,000 identities
- Monthly subscription, no long-term contract; 14-day free trial with no credit card required

**Vertical:** ☑ SaaS/tech

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:**
Digital marketers, demand-gen managers, and B2B sales teams at companies with meaningful website traffic (typically 5,000+ monthly visitors) who are investing in ads, SEO, or content but watching most visitors leave without converting.

**Secondary segments worth separating:**
1. **B2B sales / SDR teams** — want real-time intent signals and prospect identification for outbound sequences
2. **E-commerce / DTC brands** — want to recover anonymous shoppers and re-market to them without relying solely on paid retargeting
3. **Demand-gen / ABM marketers** — want to measure which campaigns are driving target-account visits and trigger account-based workflows
4. **SaaS growth teams** — tracking which companies are evaluating their product via content or trial traffic

**Problem they're in when they start looking:**
"We're spending on ads and creating content, but most visitors leave without a trace. We know people are interested but we have no way to reach them." Often triggered by high bounce rates, underperforming lead-gen forms, or pressure to improve pipeline without increasing ad spend.

**Where leads come from:**
Primarily inbound — SEO/content (comparison articles, "visitor identification software" search terms), G2/review sites, and word-of-mouth among sales/marketing communities. Some paid ads likely.

**How customers describe success:**
"I can see exactly which companies are on our site." / "We turned 3x more traffic into actual conversations." / "Our SDRs know which accounts to prioritize every morning."

---

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:** Days to 1–2 weeks (primarily self-serve)

**Free trial / consult / demo:**
Yes — 14-day free trial, no credit card required. Also a permanent free tier (100 identities/month) that acts as a low-friction entry point. Enterprise prospects can contact sales.

**What closes the deal:**
Self-serve signup. The product closes itself: once the JS snippet is installed and the user sees real identified visitors in the dashboard, the value is visceral and immediate. Enterprise deals may require a sales call.

**#1 objection or hesitation before buying:**
*"Is this legal / GDPR-compliant? Am I allowed to identify visitors without their consent?"*
Secondary: skepticism that the advertised 50–80% match rate will hold for their specific traffic.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**The activation event:**
Seeing the first batch of identified visitors — real person names, company, job title, and contact info — appear in the dashboard after installing the snippet. This is visceral proof that value is real.

**How long does it take?**
Self-serve and near-instant. Install snippet → wait for the next batch of site visitors → identified results appear. Can happen within minutes to hours of installation, fully self-serve.

*This is the anchor for Campaign 1: Welcome & Onboarding. Every onboarding email must drive toward snippet installation and the first "identified visitor" moment.*

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?** Yes — monthly subscription (MRR), tiered by identity volume.

**Renewal / repeat cycle:** Monthly billing; no stated annual contract (though annual likely offered at Enterprise tier).

**Expansion:**
- Volume-based upgrade: customers hit their identity cap → prompted to upgrade tier (Growth → Scale → Enterprise)
- Seat/team expansion: additional users added to the account
- Potential for annual-plan conversion (one-time win for ARR)

**Churn / drop-off problem:**
- Users who install the snippet but never activate (don't see identified visitors during trial) churn immediately
- Customers who can't connect identified visitors back to closed revenue (attribution gap) lose perceived ROI
- Privacy/compliance concerns can trigger reactive cancellations
- Match-rate degradation from browser privacy changes (third-party cookie deprecation, iOS privacy updates) is a long-term risk

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal:** Trial-to-paid conversion and activation (getting users to the aha moment within the 14-day window)

**Target metric:** Trial conversion rate; time-to-first-identified-visitor (activation speed); MRR expansion rate

**Brand voice / tone:**
Confident, results-driven, direct. Leads with outcomes ("identify 80% of your traffic") not features. Professional but accessible — speaks to both marketers and sales leaders. Action-oriented verbs. Avoids surveillance connotations; leans into "intelligence" and "opportunity" framing.

**Compliance constraints:**
- GDPR compliant (per site) — email copy must be careful not to imply covert tracking; reinforce privacy credentials
- CAN-SPAM applies for all email
- No HIPAA or financial-services constraints identified

**Sending volume:** Unknown — defer to platform limits once ESP is confirmed.

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — Drive trial users to install the JS snippet and see their first identified visitors; make the aha moment happen before Day 7 of the 14-day trial.
2. **Trial / Consult Conversion** — Convert expiring trial users and free-tier subscribers to paid plans; address the GDPR objection; surface social proof and ROI data.
3. **Value Newsletter** — Ongoing use-case education (ABM playbooks, e-commerce recovery, sales workflows) to deepen engagement, reduce churn, and create natural expansion conversations.
