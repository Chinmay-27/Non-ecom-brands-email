The 3-Step Morning Play
1

Prioritize by intent page, not company size

Every morning, filter your NinjaClick dashboard for accounts that visited high-intent pages — pricing, integrations, case studies, or anything that signals active evaluation. A Fortune 500 company browsing your blog is a cold lead. A 50-person startup on your pricing page is a hot one. Work the latter first.

2

Enrich and find the right contact — fast

NinjaClick surfaces company details and decision-maker contact info alongside each identification. Cross-reference with LinkedIn or your CRM to confirm you're reaching the economic buyer — not just whoever's closest to the job title. Takes about 90 seconds per account when you're in rhythm.

3

Reach out while they still remember you

Intent decays fast. An account on your pricing page at 9am is a warm lead by noon and a cold one by tomorrow. Same-day outreach — referencing what they were exploring, not "I saw you on our website" — converts at 3–4x the rate of a follow-up two days later. The signal is the opener.

A note on the 100-identity limit

Most teams running this play on active traffic hit the free-tier cap by the second or third week of the month. At that point, identification pauses — and any high-intent visitor who lands after that is back to anonymous.

Worth knowing where your cap lands in relation to your traffic peaks. You can check usage anytime in your dashboard.
