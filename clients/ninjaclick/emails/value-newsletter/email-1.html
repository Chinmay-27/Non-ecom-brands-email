Edition #1 — Intent Plays

The intent signal most teams ignore
(it's already in your dashboard)

Every edition, one actionable play for turning identified visitors into pipeline. This week: the page visits that signal buying intent — and exactly how to route them to sales the same day.

 

Hey {{first_name}},

Most teams using visitor intelligence make the same mistake: they treat all identified visitors the same. They export the list, pass it to sales on Friday, and wonder why response rates are flat.

The signal hiding in plain sight isn't who visited — it's which pages they visited. Not all pages are equal. Three page categories reliably predict buying intent. If you're not routing these visitors to sales within the same business day, you're bleeding pipeline.

This week's intent play

Signal #1 — Highest intent

Pricing page visits

Anyone who hits your pricing page is mid-funnel at minimum. They're not browsing — they're evaluating. A company-level visitor here means someone at that org is seriously considering a purchase.

The play: Filter your NinjaClick dashboard for any identified visitor who viewed your pricing page in the last 24 hours. Assign them directly to your closest-fit AE or SDR — not a sequence, a direct reach-out with specific context: "I noticed [Company] was looking at our pricing yesterday…"

Signal #2 — Strong intent

Feature or integration pages

Someone reading your CRM integration docs or your API page isn't a casual browser — they're building a business case internally. This is the champion doing the technical diligence before they bring it to the decision-maker.

The play: Route these to sales with a note on which integration they looked at. A rep who opens with "I saw someone at your company was checking out our HubSpot integration" is having a completely different conversation than one cold-calling off a list.

Signal #3 — Warm intent

Case studies and customer proof pages

A visitor reading your case studies is looking for permission to buy. They want to see themselves in your success stories — same industry, same team size, same problem. They're not far from a "yes."

The play: Send them a relevant case study from the same vertical — even if they already read one on your site. A rep sharing a story that mirrors their exact situation is providing value, not pitching.

 

The same-day routing rule

Intent signals decay fast. A company on your pricing page today may have forgotten your product exists by next Tuesday. The teams getting the most out of visitor intelligence have one rule: if the page visit scores above a threshold, the rep sees it that day.

The simplest version of this lives in NinjaClick's dashboard. Every morning — or better, every evening the day before — filter by high-intent pages from the last 24 hours. Spend 15 minutes assigning to reps with context on what was visited. That's the whole workflow.
