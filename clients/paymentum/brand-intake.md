## 1. The Offer

**What they sell:**
Omni-channel merchant payment processing — POS hardware and software, payment terminals (free program for eligible merchants), compliant surcharging / dual pricing / cash-discount programs, ACH transfers, mobile payments, gateways, virtual terminals, cryptocurrency, buy-now-pay-later, and printing/promotional services.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:**
Stop paying 3–4% of every card transaction to the card networks. Paymentum's surcharging and dual-pricing models let merchants recoup processing costs from cardholders compliantly, effectively getting to 0% net processing expense. The promise is: *keep the money you're currently giving away*.

**Price point & commitment:**
- Custom/variable — no public pricing; merchants get a free audit against their current statement
- No long-term contracts at signing
- Free terminal hardware for eligible merchants
- Net cost to merchant is largely offset or eliminated via surcharging passed to cardholders
- **Vertical:** B2B/Professional (fintech / merchant services)

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:**
Small-to-mid-sized business owner — most commonly a practice owner, restaurant operator, or retail store owner — currently absorbing 3–4%+ in card processing fees and motivated enough to change processors. They run lean margins and see payment fees as a silent, recurring profit leak they've been told is unavoidable.

**Secondary segments worth separating:**

| Segment | Profile | Specific angle |
|---|---|---|
| Healthcare | Dentist, physician, chiropractor, optometrist, psychiatrist | Also needs Practice Management Bridge (EMR/EHR integration, patient payment workflows, automated posting) |
| Restaurant | Fast casual, full service, food truck, bar | Needs integrated POS — online ordering, KDS, delivery, self-service kiosk, tablet ordering |
| Retail | Boutiques, salons, auto repair, gas/convenience | Simpler POS needs; surcharging and dual pricing are the primary hook |
| Nonprofit / Education | Schools, charities | Special pricing available; different compliance/messaging needs |
| Partner / Referral | Accountants, bookkeepers, SaaS vendors, consultants | Refers merchants for revenue share; escalating tiers |

**Problem state when they start looking:**
- Just saw their monthly processing statement and finally did the math on what they're paying
- Lost a key employee and is auditing operating costs
- Tired of being locked in a contract with a processor that doesn't pick up the phone
- Was referred by a colleague who "eliminated their processing fees"

**Lead sources:** Primarily referrals, direct outbound/relationship sales, and inbound from "free payments audit" and "calculate your savings" web CTAs. SEO likely secondary.

**How they describe success:** "I don't pay for card processing anymore" / "We're saving $X,XXX a month" / "The terminal was free and setup was easy"

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact to paying customer:** Days to weeks (varies by merchant size and complexity of current setup)

**Free trial / entry offer:** Yes — multiple:
1. **Free Payments Audit** — merchant submits current statement; Paymentum shows exactly what they'd save
2. **Free Demo** — scheduled call/appointment via web form
3. **Calculate Your Savings** — interactive tool on site; generates a "complimentary value summary report"
4. **Free Terminal Program** — qualifying merchants receive hardware at no upfront cost

**Who/what closes the deal:**
Booked appointment with a sales rep → analysis of current merchant statement → side-by-side savings proposal → merchant agreement signing (no long-term contract removes a major friction point)

**#1 objection before buying:**
*"Will my customers get upset about the surcharge?"* Secondary: *"Is 0% processing actually real, or is there a catch?"* and *"I'm mid-contract with my current processor."*

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**Activation event:** Merchant processes their first real transaction at 0% net cost and sees it on their statement. Pre-signup, the equivalent moment is completing the savings calculator or free audit and seeing a concrete annual dollar figure.

**Time to activation:** Same day if terminal is delivered and activated; hardware setup is promoted as quick remote install with minimal disruption.

**Self-serve or assisted:** Assisted — sales rep handles onboarding, hardware shipping/installation, and initial setup. Not a self-serve SaaS flow.

*This makes Campaign 1 (Welcome & Onboarding) the highest-leverage first build — the goal is to make the merchant feel confident and supported from agreement-signing through first live transaction.*

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?** Yes — Paymentum earns on transaction volume (interchange / residuals). Every active merchant processing transactions is recurring revenue.

**Renewal/repeat cycle:** Month-to-month; no forced renewal event. Retention is passive until a competitor approaches the merchant or the merchant has a service issue.

**What expansion looks like:**
- Additional terminals (second register, second location)
- Multi-location / franchise rollout
- Moving up the Partner Program tiers (affiliate → referral partner → strategic alliance → agent/reseller)
- Add-on services: printing, promotional materials

**Churn / drop-off problem:**
- Merchant gets cold-called by a competing ISO offering a slightly lower rate
- Cardholder complaints about surcharges lead merchant to second-guess the program
- Business closes or is sold
- Hardware or software reliability issue that goes unresolved
- Merchant was never fully onboarded and never activated (early-stage silent churn)

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal:** Full lifecycle build — all backbone campaigns are missing; start with conversion-path campaigns that directly affect signed merchant count.

**Recommended first three campaigns:**
1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — activate new merchants, build confidence, drive first live transaction
2. **Lead-Magnet Nurture** — convert free audit / savings calculator leads who didn't book a call
3. **Trial/Consult Conversion** — follow up demo/appointment attendees who didn't sign

**Brand voice / tone:**
Professional yet approachable. Confident and ROI-focused. Plain-spoken — avoids heavy fintech or industry jargon. Empathetic to small-business-owner pressures. Leads with concrete dollar savings ("you're leaving $X,XXX on the table every month") over abstract feature lists. Founder-backed credibility (Joel Truss's Bank of America / Fiserv / Clover background) can be used selectively in trust-building sequences.

**Compliance constraints:**
- **PCI DSS** — cannot encourage merchants to handle or store card data outside compliant flows
- **Visa/Mastercard surcharging rules** — up to 3% cap on credit (not debit); must be disclosed at point of sale; state restrictions apply (a few states prohibit surcharging — segment or suppress those merchants for surcharge-specific messaging)
- **State surcharging laws** — Connecticut, Massachusetts, and Puerto Rico historically ban surcharging; verify current state list before geographic campaigns
- **CAN-SPAM** — all commercial email must include opt-out and physical address (Nashville, TN)
- **HIPAA note** — Paymentum is the payment processor, not a HIPAA covered entity; avoid reproducing any PHI, and do not make clinical or diagnostic claims in healthcare-segment emails
