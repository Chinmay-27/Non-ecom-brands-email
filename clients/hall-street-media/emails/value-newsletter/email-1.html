Money & Career

Stop letting your savings account lose the race

Here's the plain truth: a standard savings account is earning you around 0.5% a year right now. Inflation is running hotter than that. Which means your money is technically losing value while it sits still.

The move this week is to look at a high-yield savings account (HYSA) through an online bank. Rates have been sitting between 4–5% APY. Same FDIC protection. Same liquidity. Just more money working for you instead of sitting flat.

Take 10 minutes this week to:

  • Google your bank's current savings rate
  • Compare it to one HYSA option (Ally, Marcus, SoFi are a few popular ones)
  • If the gap is more than 2%, it's time to make a move

Not financial advice. Do your own research and consult a licensed financial professional before making changes to your accounts.

This Week's Real-Talk Money Tip

"Earning more won't fix a spending leak. Before you chase the next raise, know exactly where last month's paycheck went."

 

Faith & Inspiration

The grind is real — so is the rest

We talk a lot about building and achieving — and that's good. But this week's faith note is a quiet reminder: even the most driven people were designed with a need for stillness. Pushing without pausing isn't ambition. It's a slow drain.

This week: carve out 15 uninterrupted minutes. No phone, no to-do list. Call it prayer, meditation, or just breathing room — whatever connects you. Give yourself permission to receive, not just produce.

Family & Relationships

Have the money talk before it has you

Financial stress is the #1 source of friction in relationships. Couples who talk about money weekly — not monthly, not when something goes wrong — report less conflict and more shared progress. Put it on the calendar like a standing meeting. Make it a check-in, not a confrontation.

◆

Health & Fitness

Your walk counts. Seriously.

You don't need a gym membership to protect your heart. Research continues to back up what grandma already knew — a 30-minute walk most days lowers blood pressure, lifts mood, and keeps the weight in check. If that sounds too simple, it's because it is. Start there. Stack more later.
