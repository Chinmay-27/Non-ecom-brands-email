# Brand Intake — Clarusway
**URL:** https://www.clarusway.com  
**Vertical:** Education / IT Bootcamp  
**Date:** 2026-06-04

---

## 1. The Offer

**What do they sell?**  
Online IT bootcamp programs — part-time, fully online, live + async. Tracks: Cloud & DevOps Engineering (AWS, 8 months), AWS Solutions Architect (5 months), DevOps Engineering (3 months), Applied AI & Data Science, Software Development (Full Stack), Salesforce Admin/Dev, Cybersecurity, Web3 & Blockchain, SDET. Also a free CW Mini Bootcamp (Python, Linux, Git, SQL — 50+ hours).

**Core outcome the customer is buying:**  
A career change into tech — specifically a job offer in their chosen IT track with an average starting salary of $90K–$102K. Clarusway sells the transformation ("delivery driver → cloud engineer"), not just the curriculum.

**Price point & commitment:**  
- $7,000–$8,000 per program (one-time)  
- Part-time: 16–20 hrs/week over 3–8 months  
- Payment options: Upfront (30% discount), monthly installments (~$250/mo), ISA (12.5% income × 18 months after $40K salary threshold, capped at $20K), 0% education credit, Meritize loans  
- 15-day risk-free trial with full refund  

**Vertical:** ☑ Education/nonprofit/membership (specifically ed-tech / career bootcamp)

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:**  
Career changers aged ~25–45 with zero IT background. Former delivery drivers, marketers, stay-at-home parents, hospitality workers — people stuck in a job they don't love who want a well-paying tech career but feel unqualified and are uncertain about cost and time.

**Secondary segments worth separating:**  
- Working IT professionals wanting to upskill or earn a cloud/DevOps/Salesforce certification  
- Corporate/enterprise clients seeking custom team training (separate B2B arm)

**Problem they're in when they start looking:**  
Career dissatisfaction or job loss → Googling "how to get into IT with no experience" → landing on a blog post or quiz → skeptically exploring whether a bootcamp is legit.

**Where leads come from today:**  
SEO (strong blog/content presence), paid ads, YouTube, word-of-mouth/alumni referrals, events, ranked on Course Report / Career Karma / SwitchUp.

**How they describe success in their own words:**  
"I got a job in tech." "I landed a $95K role six months after graduating." "I went from zero to working as a SOC Analyst." Outcome- and salary-specific.

---

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:** Days to 2–3 weeks  
Typical path: free resource (mini bootcamp / quiz) → info session → free advisor call → application form → payment/ISA.

**Free trial / first-session offer:**  
Yes — multiple:  
1. Free CW Mini Bootcamp (50+ hrs, fully free — Python, Linux, Git, SQL)  
2. 2-minute Career Path Quiz → personalized track recommendation + discount coupon  
3. Free Info Session (recorded webinar)  
4. Free Career Consultation ("Talk to an Advisor") — live call

**Who / what closes the deal:**  
Advisor-assisted. Prospect books a free consultation → discovery call → personalized recommendation → application → payment/ISA setup. Self-serve application available as a fallback CTA.

**#1 objection before buying:**  
"I have no IT background — am I really able to do this?" Second: cost and time commitment. Third: skepticism about job placement guarantees.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**The activation event:**  
The moment a complete beginner successfully completes a hands-on exercise in the free mini bootcamp or early in the paid program — running their first Python script, configuring a cloud service, or completing a project — and realizes this is actually learnable by someone like them.

**Secondary aha:**  
Career Management System orientation (resume review, LinkedIn optimization, mock interview) — the point where graduates believe the job is truly within reach.

**Time to activation:** Self-serve; free mini bootcamp available immediately on signup. Paid program aha happens within first 1–2 weeks of live classes.

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?**  
No — one-time per enrollment. Not a subscription model.

**Renewal / repeat cycle:**  
Alumni can stack certifications (e.g., DevOps 3-month → AWS Solutions Architect 5-month). No formal renewal cycle but natural progression exists.

**What does "expansion" look like?**  
- Upsell to longer/more comprehensive combined tracks  
- Alumni referrals (primary growth lever)  
- Corporate / enterprise custom training (separate B2B product)  
- New certification add-ons as tech landscape shifts

**Churn / drop-off problem:**  
Mid-program dropout is the primary risk — overwhelm, life events, falling behind on live sessions. Pre-enrollment: prospects who book an advisor call but never show up or apply. Post-mini-bootcamp: leads who complete the free program but don't convert to paid.

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal:**  
☑ More conversions/bookings (free consult → paid enrollment) and ☑ full lifecycle build (all backbone campaigns likely missing or thin).

**Target metric & baseline:**  
Conversion from free lead (quiz / mini bootcamp) to advisor call booking; and advisor call → enrollment rate. Baselines unknown.

**Brand voice / tone:**  
Motivational, accessible, transformation-focused. Outcome-specific ("$102K average salary", "92% job placement"). Warm and direct — speaks to the hesitant, non-technical beginner with reassurance. Plain language; avoids jargon outside of teaching contexts. Community and mentor-forward. Not corporate. Closest comp: a supportive career coach who also happens to cite statistics.

**Compliance constraints:**  
CAN-SPAM (US). ISA disclosures required where relevant. Standard ed-tech data handling. No HIPAA. GDPR consideration for EU learners. Avoid making guarantees about salary or placement without proper disclaimers (FTC).

**Sending volume / platform:**  
Unknown. Given multiple lead-capture funnels (quiz, mini bootcamp, advisor, info session), list is likely segmented. Platform can likely support behavioral sends.

---

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

Based on the gaps above and the default priority order:

1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — New paid enrollees need a structured sequence driving to LMS login → first live class attendance → first hands-on project. This is the activation event that predicts completion and referral.
2. **Lead-Magnet Nurture** — Mini bootcamp and quiz completers represent a massive warm-but-unconverted pool. A 5–7 email sequence from free resource → advisor call CTA is the highest-leverage conversion play.
3. **Trial / Consult Conversion** — Post-advisor-call non-converters and 15-day trial enrollees who haven't activated need a targeted objection-handling sequence (cost options, "no background needed" proof, success stories).
