## 1. The Offer

**What they sell:** Full-service commercial printing — both transactional (self-serve online orders) and managed corporate accounts.

Product lines:
- Marketing collateral: business cards, postcards, brochures, flyers
- Large-format: banners, signs, vehicle wraps, window graphics, lawn signs
- Apparel: custom silk-screen t-shirts
- Specialty: NCR forms, notepads, envelopes, presentation folders, mounted canvas
- Corporate web-to-print portals: branded storefronts where employees order brand-consistent materials themselves

**Core outcome the customer is buying:** Looking polished and on-brand — professionally printed materials delivered reliably, without the stress of managing vendors or worrying about brand drift across locations or employees.

**Price point & commitment:**
- Entry-level: postcards from $16, brochures from $36
- Bulk quantity pricing (100 → 25,000 units)
- Corporate portals: custom-quoted (B2B contract/retainer relationship)
- Commitment: primarily one-off transactional; corporate portal clients are ongoing recurring

**Vertical:** Local & service — B2B/professional printing

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:** Small to mid-sized business owners and marketing managers in the NJ/NY tri-state area who need printed marketing materials and want a reliable local vendor they can trust.

**Secondary segments:**
- Contractors & tradespeople (vehicle wraps, lawn signs, business cards — high repeat)
- Corporate marketing teams managing multi-employee print orders (portal clients)
- Real estate agents & estate-sale operators
- Event organizers and local nonprofits

**Problem they're in when they start looking:**
- Need materials fast for an upcoming event, campaign, or new business launch
- Frustrated by inconsistent brand quality from a past vendor or commodity printer
- Managing print orders manually across a team and it's becoming a headache

**Lead sources today:** Local referrals, chamber of commerce (Fort Lee, Hackensack), Google/local SEO, Instagram/Facebook, word-of-mouth from Darin's network.

**How customers describe success:** "It looks exactly how I envisioned it." / "They got it right the first time and it was ready when I needed it."

---

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:**
- Transactional: same day to a few days (online self-serve)
- Corporate portals: 1–4 weeks (needs scoping, design, approval)

**Free trial / consult / first-session offer:** No formal trial. Free online quote request available. Product catalog partially public. Corporate portal prospects would typically get a discovery call with Darin.

**Who/what closes the deal:**
- Standard products: self-serve online order (upload file → checkout)
- Corporate portals & large accounts: Darin directly (phone/email consultation)

**#1 objection before buying:** "Can't I just use Vistaprint/Canva Print/GotPrint for less?" — The commodity online printer price comparison. Counter: local quality control, relationship, custom options, turnaround reliability, and the portal value-add for teams.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**Activation event:** Receiving the first print run and it looks great — color accurate, quality paper, exactly as proofed.

For corporate portal clients: logging into their branded storefront for the first time and seeing their pre-approved templates ready for employees to order from.

**How long / self-serve?** Transactional is self-serve (upload → approve proof → ship). Portal setup is assisted (Darin's team builds it). First order turnaround is typically a few business days.

*This becomes the anchor of Campaign 1: Welcome & Onboarding — guiding the new buyer to upload their file, approve the proof, and confidently complete their first order.*

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?** Yes, in two ways:
1. Repeat transactional orders (seasonal restocks, campaign refreshes)
2. Corporate portal clients — stickier, ongoing relationship with higher LTV

**Repeat cycle length:** Varies by customer — monthly for active marketing businesses; quarterly/seasonal for others; corporate portals are always-on.

**What "expansion" looks like:**
- SMB: ordering more product types (adds apparel to their usual collateral order), larger quantities
- Corporate: adding more employees to portal, expanding template library, adding product categories

**Churn / drop-off problem:** One-time buyers who don't return — they get what they need, never hear from Gaba again, and default back to commodity printers next time. No automated reorder nudge exists. Corporate portal clients are sticky once set up but take effort to close initially.

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal:** Most likely a mix of more conversions (turning quote requesters into buyers) and better retention (getting one-time buyers to reorder). Corporate portal pipeline is likely a key growth lever.

**Target metric:** Repeat order rate; quote-to-order conversion rate.

**Brand voice / tone:** Professional and dependable with family-owned warmth. Direct and practical. "Local expert you trust, not a faceless print factory." Darin's personal involvement and multi-generational background should be woven into email — it's a genuine differentiator vs. commodity printers.

**Compliance constraints:** Standard CAN-SPAM / CASL. No HIPAA, financial, or legal-sector restrictions identified.

**Sending volume:** Unknown — likely a modest list size for a regional service business. Conservative cadence (2–4 emails/month) appropriate until list engagement data is available.

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

Based on the gap analysis above and the default priority order:
