What $195 actually buys

Every frame we make starts as Italian acetate — the same material European houses use, cut and hand-finished in our Williamsburg studio. The hinges are real metal, screwed in, not glued. The color isn't a coating that chips; it runs all the way through the material.

And we name our frames after Brooklyn streets — because they're designed by people who walk them, not by a committee in a corporate office trying to reverse-engineer what sold last season at LensCrafters.
