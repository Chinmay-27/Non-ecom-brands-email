# Brand Intake — Brooklyn Spectacles

---

## 1. The Offer

**What do they sell?**
Handcrafted independent eyeglass frames — optical and sunglasses — plus a children's line ("Little Spectacles"). Made with Italian acetate in a Williamsburg design studio. Sold DTC via Shopify and in person at the flagship at 291 Bedford Ave, Brooklyn. Also stocked through authorized US/Canada retailers.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:**
Distinctive, quality frames that signal creative taste and individuality — a clear contrast to mass-market chain eyewear, without the need to import from Europe.

**Price point & commitment:**
- Standard optical frames: $195–$225 (one-off)
- Sale frames: $95
- Designer collaborations (e.g. Issey Miyake): $495
- Gift cards: from $50
- No subscription, no recurring revenue model. Repeat cycle is 1–3 years.

**Vertical:** Local & service — independent consumer eyewear retail (DTC + physical)

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:**
Style-conscious urban creatives and professionals, roughly 25–45, based in Brooklyn/NYC or other design-forward US/Canada cities. They buy glasses as a fashion accessory as much as a vision tool, and actively seek out independent brands over chains.

**Secondary segments:**
- Parents buying the Little Spectacles kids' line
- Gift buyers (gift cards, seasonal giving)
- Online DTC buyers outside NYC who can't visit in person

**Problem they're in when they start looking:**
- Sick of generic mass-market frames (Warby Parker / LensCrafters feel like everyone's wearing the same thing)
- Prescription changed and it's time for a new pair — wants something better this time
- Looking for a meaningful, considered gift

**Where leads come from:**
Walk-ins (Bedford Ave foot traffic), Instagram/UGC (#LoveMyBKS, #BrooklynSpectacles), word of mouth, authorized retailers, organic search.

**How they describe success:**
"People keep asking me where I got these." / "Finally frames that feel like *me*." / "Worth every penny — they've held up for two years."

---

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:** Same day (in-store) to 3–7 days (online browse-to-checkout).

**Free trial / first offer:** No home trial program found. In-store fitting is the de facto try-before-you-buy. Online buyers rely on product photos, measurements, and the return/exchange policy.

**What closes the deal:** Self-serve Shopify checkout online; in-store walk-in purchase. No sales call required.

**#1 objection before buying:**
Price sensitivity ($195–$225 frame-only feels like a stretch vs. fast-fashion eyewear) combined with fit anxiety for online buyers ("What if they don't suit my face shape / don't work with my prescription?").

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

The activation event is **putting on the finished pair for the first time** — in-store at the fitting or opening the delivery box at home — and experiencing the craftsmanship, fit, and inevitable compliments that confirm the price was worth it.

In-store: assisted, immediate. Online: self-serve, 3–7 days post-order.

*This is the anchor for Campaign 1: Welcome & Onboarding — bridge the gap between order confirmation and that unboxing/wearing moment.*

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?** No. One-off purchase model.

**Repeat/renewal cycle:** Natural repeat is 1–3 years, driven by prescription changes, frame wear, or wanting a new look. No built-in trigger.

**What "expansion" looks like:**
- Second pair upsell: optical + sunglasses combo
- Kids' frames for parents already in the brand
- Limited-edition and collaboration drops (urgency-driven)
- Gift cards driving new-customer referrals

**Churn / drop-off problem:**
Single-purchase mentality. After buying, customers have no ongoing reason to engage with the brand. Without active re-engagement, a satisfied customer simply disappears for 2+ years and may buy from a competitor next time.

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal (assumed, confirm on kickoff):** Full lifecycle — the brand has essentially no automated email infrastructure, so all seven campaigns represent potential uplift.

**Suggested priority order:**
1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — biggest immediate win; captures every new buyer and bridges to the aha moment
2. **Lead-Magnet Nurture** — converts email subscribers who haven't purchased yet
3. **Referral / Advocacy** — leverages the strong UGC culture (#LoveMyBKS) to drive word-of-mouth post-purchase

**Brand voice / tone:** Confident, craft-proud, Brooklyn indie energy. Not corporate. Conversational and grounded in local creative culture. Sample language: *"Made in Williamsburg, worn everywhere."* / *"Frames named after the streets that inspired them."*

**Compliance:** Standard CAN-SPAM + CASL (US/Canada). No healthcare/HIPAA exposure — frames only, no vision care services.

**Sending volume:** Unknown. Confirm list size and Shopify/ESP plan limits on kickoff.
