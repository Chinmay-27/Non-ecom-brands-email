# Brand Intake — Warbay

## 1. The Offer

**What they sell:** Done-for-you full-service email marketing, packaged as an agency retainer. Four named service pillars: Email Data Monetization, Email List Management, Email Marketing Automation, and Email Deliverability. AI is positioned as a core part of execution ("Email + AI is Here...").

**Core outcome the customer is buying:** Revenue growth from their email list — without doing the work themselves. The headline on the homepage is literally "Grow Revenue with Dedicated Email Marketing."

**Price point & commitment:** Not published on the site. Based on the "Qualify for Demo" gating and full-service positioning, this is almost certainly a monthly retainer (estimated $2k–$10k/mo range, typical for agencies at this tier). Commitment length unknown — likely month-to-month or 3–6 month contracts.

**Vertical:** ☑ B2B / Professional services (agency selling to other businesses)

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:** SMB-to-mid-market business owner or marketing lead who has an email list (or wants to build one) but lacks the in-house expertise, time, or technical infrastructure to monetize it. They know email is important but aren't getting results.

**Secondary segments worth separating:**
- E-commerce / DTC brands — large dormant lists, need automation + deliverability
- Publishers / media companies — data monetization angle is the hook
- B2B SaaS / service businesses — nurture sequences and lead-gen automation
- Lead-gen businesses — deliverability and list hygiene are primary pain

**Problems they're in when they start looking:**
- Emails going to spam (deliverability crisis)
- Email list sitting idle, not generating revenue
- No automation — everything is manual
- Marketing team overwhelmed, can't keep up with campaigns
- Vague sense that "email should be doing more for us"

**Where leads come from:** Unknown (site is early-stage with no case studies or channel mentions). Likely inbound SEO + referrals + outbound sales given the "Qualify for Demo" flow.

**How they describe success:** "Our emails are actually landing in the inbox and we're seeing more revenue from our list every month."

---

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:** Weeks (agency sale — requires discovery, proposal, contract).

**Free trial / consult / demo:** Primary entry point is a gated qualifying demo call ("Qualify for Demo" CTA on the homepage). No free trial or self-serve option visible.

**Who/what closes the deal:** Demo/discovery call → custom proposal → retainer agreement. Likely founder-led or small sales team close.

**#1 objection before buying:** "We're already handling email in-house" or "I can't justify the retainer until I see what the ROI looks like." Secondary: "Why are you #1?" — bold claim with no social proof yet on the site.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**Activation event:** The first campaign sent on the client's behalf that (a) hits the inbox cleanly and (b) drives measurable revenue or engagement above their prior baseline. This is the moment the client feels they made the right call.

**How long does it take:** Probably 2–4 weeks post-onboarding (list audit → strategy → first send).

**Assisted or self-serve:** Fully assisted — this is a done-for-you service.

*→ This is the anchor for Campaign 1: Welcome & Onboarding — set expectations, collect assets, and build excitement for the first campaign launch.*

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Revenue recurring?** Yes — monthly retainer assumed.

**Renewal / repeat cycle:** Monthly (rolling) or quarterly review. Unknown exact contract terms.

**What "expansion" looks like:**
- Adding more service pillars (e.g., a client starts with deliverability, then adds full automation)
- Increasing campaign volume or frequency
- Adding data monetization as an upsell for clients with large lists

**Churn / drop-off problem:** Slow early results → client loses confidence and brings email back in-house or cancels. Also at risk: clients who never fully onboard (don't send assets, don't complete setup) and never reach the aha moment.
