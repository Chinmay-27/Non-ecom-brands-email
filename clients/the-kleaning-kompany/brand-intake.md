## 1. The Offer

**What does the brand sell?**
Recurring commercial cleaning contracts — janitorial, office, medical facility, commercial kitchen, HOA/multi-tenant, school & day care, shopping center, specialty floor care, and ad-hoc special services. All engagements are custom-scoped; no self-serve pricing.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:**
A consistently clean, professionally maintained facility — every single time — so the buyer stops worrying about their cleaning vendor and can focus on running their business.

**Price point & commitment:**
- No published pricing; custom quote issued after a free site inspection
- Mid-to-high ticket commercial retainer
- Recurring contract (daily / weekly / monthly service cadences); cleaning operations run 24/7 by contract
- 10% off first service referenced in directory listings (possible existing offer)

**Vertical confirmation:** Local & service (B2B commercial cleaning, South Florida–based with multi-state presence: FL, TX, DC, MD, VA, AR)

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:**
Facilities directors, property managers, and business owners responsible for vendor selection and day-to-day operations of commercial spaces. Based in South Florida (Boca Raton / Miami area); multi-state reach. Typically mid-size operations — yoga studios, wholesale bakeries, medical practices, HOA communities, retail centers.

**Secondary segments worth separating:**
1. Medical facilities & healthcare practices (highest compliance sensitivity — OSHA/CDC)
2. Restaurants & commercial kitchens (grease, food safety focus)
3. HOA & multi-tenant communities (property manager as buyer, residents as end-users)
4. Schools & day cares (safety / chemical sensitivity concerns)
5. Shopping centers & retail (high-traffic, continuous coverage)
6. Office & general commercial buildings (most price-sensitive, most common)

**Problems they're in when they start looking:**
- Fed up with a prior cleaning company that was inconsistent or unreliable
- New facility opening and need a vendor from day one
- Compliance audit upcoming (especially medical/kitchen clients)
- A specific incident (missed clean, inspection failure) triggers the search

**Where leads come from today:**
Search (SEO — multiple service-specific landing pages), Google Maps/reviews, referrals (implied by 500+ satisfied clients and strong testimonials mentioning word of mouth). No blog or content marketing visible.

**How they describe success:**
"Every morning when I walk in my facility looks phenomenal." (direct testimonial)
"Consistency, professionalism, and rapid response to special requests." (facilities director testimonial)

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:**
☑ Days to weeks

**Process:**
1. Prospect contacts via form or phone call
2. The Kleaning Kompany conducts a site inspection
3. Custom cleaning proposal is delivered
4. Contract is signed

**Free trial / consult / first-session offer:**
Yes — free site inspection and custom proposal. No paid trial; the contract itself is the first engagement. A 10% off first service offer appears in external directory listings (may be a live offer).

**Who/what closes the deal:**
A signed custom contract, closed by the owner/sales rep after the proposal is delivered. Self-serve signup does not exist — every deal requires human touchpoints.

**#1 objection before buying:**
Trust and reliability. Decision-makers are nervous about letting a new vendor unsupervised into their facility and are often burned by a prior cleaning company. The risk of repeating that experience is the dominant hesitation.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**Activation event:**
The first morning a new client walks in and finds their facility immaculate — the visceral before/after. Multiple testimonials echo this: *"I wish I would have switched sooner,"* *"every morning when I walk in my facility looks phenomenal."*

**How long does it take?**
Immediately after the first scheduled clean (could be the very first night/morning after contract start). Assisted — the team performs the service; the client just shows up.

**Email implication:**
Campaign 1 (Welcome & Onboarding) should prime the client to notice and celebrate that first clean, and confirm the cadence/contact process so they feel confident from day one.

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?**
Yes — recurring janitorial contracts are the entire business model. 98% retention rate cited on-site. 500+ satisfied clients, 10+ years experience.

**Renewal / repeat cycle:**
Ongoing contract; no fixed renewal date mentioned. Service frequency: daily, weekly, or monthly depending on scope.

**What does "expansion" look like?**
- Upgrading base janitorial to include specialty floor care (stripping, waxing, carpet extraction)
- Adding commercial kitchen cleaning to an office contract
- Adding special/event services (post-construction, event cleanup) on top of routine work
- Growing square footage or service days

**Churn / drop-off problem:**
A quality or consistency slip on any visit erodes the trust that won the contract. A single bad service — especially in high-compliance environments like medical facilities — can trigger cancellation. Secondary risk: a cheaper competitor targeting the same client after the initial novelty fades.

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal:**
☑ More conversions/bookings (first priority — turning quote inquiries into signed contracts)
☑ Better retention (second — protecting the 98% retention rate and expanding accounts)

**Target metrics & baseline:**
- Quote-to-contract conversion rate: unknown baseline
- Retention rate: 98% (already strong — protect and expand)
- CTR on emails: no baseline (no existing program)

**Brand voice / tone:**
Professional, trustworthy, and warm. Confident but not salesy. Plain-spoken — speaks to operations-minded buyers, not marketers. Emphasis on reliability, responsiveness, and tailored service. Uses "we" language throughout; feels like a relationship, not a transaction. Avoid overly clinical or corporate language; avoid excessive informality.

**Compliance constraints:**
- CAN-SPAM / GDPR standard email compliance required
- Medical facility segment: OSHA/CDC-adjacent language; no PHI in emails; care with health-related claims
- No financial/legal sector constraints noted

**Sending volume the platform/list can support:**
Unknown — no existing list or ESP confirmed.
