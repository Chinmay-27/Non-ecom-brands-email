# Brand Intake — Blessing Mouth

**URL:** https://www.blessingmouth.com
**Tagline:** Speak Faith. Renew Your Mind.
**Vertical:** Education / Membership (faith-based app)
**Date researched:** 2026-06-04

---

## 1. The Offer

**What they sell:** A faith-based voice declaration app (web + mobile). Users browse a curated library of scripture-rooted declarations organized by life theme — identity, healing, provision, peace, family, purpose — record themselves speaking those declarations aloud in their own voice, and build a daily practice through customizable reminders.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:** Not a devotional. Not a podcast. The customer buys *consistency* — a daily rhythm of speaking God's Word over their own life in their own voice, so that the promises of the Bible move from head knowledge into lived reality. The brand frames this as the missing piece most Christians were never taught: Joshua 1:8's "hagah" (to murmur, mutter, speak aloud).

**Price point & commitment:**
| Plan | Price | Includes |
|---|---|---|
| Free | $0 — always | Foundational library, voice recording, 4 default declaration lists, custom reminders |
| Silver (most popular) | $2.99/mo or $24/yr | Everything Free + full library, all themes, unlimited recordings, custom lists, custom reminder schedule |
| Bronze | Unknown (see app) | — |
| Gold | Unknown (see app) | — |

Freemium model — free plan is permanent with no credit card required. Monthly or annual billing. Cancel anytime.

**Vertical:** Education / Membership (faith-based SaaS/app)

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:** Christian believers of any denomination who feel spiritually stuck or distant from God despite doing "everything right" — attending church, reading the Bible, serving in ministry. They have theological knowledge but lack a consistent, embodied *practice* of speaking the Word.

**Secondary segments:**
- Believers in active life crisis (struggling marriage, health, finances, identity) seeking a breakthrough mechanism
- Consistent churchgoers who sense something is missing from their private devotional life
- New or returning believers looking to build foundational daily spiritual habits

**The problem they're in when they start looking:**
> "You've done everything right. So why does God still feel far away?"

The founder's own story — pastor's kid, two failed marriages, years of Bible knowledge without transformation — mirrors the ICP's felt pain exactly.

**Where leads come from:** Primarily organic/direct (the landing page is built for conviction-led sharing; strong word-of-mouth potential in faith communities). No ads or referral program surfaced.

**How they describe success:** "The peace the Bible talks about — the kind that passes understanding — moved from doctrine into daily reality." Customers want scripture to feel *alive*, not academic.

---

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:** Same day. The entire conversion cycle is self-serve and frictionless — free email or Google signup, no credit card.

**Free trial / entry offer:** Permanent free plan (not a time-limited trial). The free tier is genuinely usable (recording, 4 default lists, reminders) but limited in library depth and customization. The brand's explicit instruction: "Upgrade only when you feel the difference."

**Who/what closes the deal:** Self-serve entirely. The landing page does the selling — strong long-form copy rooted in Joshua 1:8, founder story, scripture proof, and a freemium CTA. Upgrade happens inside the app.

**#1 objection before buying:** *"I already read my Bible / I already go to church — why do I need this?"*
The brand pre-empts this throughout the homepage by teaching that reading ≠ speaking and that the conditional promise in Joshua 1:8 is attached to *speaking and meditating* (hagah), not reading alone. This is the theological premise the entire offer rests on.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**Activation event:** The user records themselves declaring a scripture-based affirmation and plays it back for the first time.

Hearing their own voice speak God's Word over their own life is the differentiating experience — the brand makes this explicit: "When you hear yourself say what God says about you, your spirit receives it differently than when someone else says it. The fear quiets. The doubt loosens."

**How long does it take:** Minutes. The onboarding flow is: sign up → choose a declaration theme → record → set a reminder. Fully self-serve.

**Campaign anchor:** This is the activation event for Campaign 1 (Welcome & Onboarding). The primary email goal post-signup is to get the user to make their **first recording within 24–48 hours**.

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?** Yes — monthly or annual subscription (Silver/Bronze/Gold).

**Renewal / repeat cycle:** Monthly (Silver at $2.99) or annual ($24/yr). Annual plan is the expansion upsell with 33% savings.

**What "expansion" looks like:**
- Free → Silver → Bronze → Gold tier upgrades
- Monthly → Annual plan conversion (value framing: save 33%)
- Deeper habit depth: more themes, custom lists, unlimited recordings unlocked at higher tiers

**Churn / drop-off problem:** Users who sign up free and never build the daily habit. If they don't record in the first session and enable reminders, they disengage before experiencing the transformative aha moment. The app's value is entirely habit-dependent — no habit = no perceived value = churn.

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal:** Unknown without direct client conversation — but based on the freemium structure, the most pressing lifecycle need is **free → paid conversion** and **onboarding activation** (getting users to their first recording before they forget why they signed up).

**Target metric:** Free-to-paid conversion rate; daily/weekly active recording rate (app engagement proxy).

**Brand voice / tone:**
- Personal and confessional (founder shares his own failed marriages, spiritual struggle)
- Scripture-anchored — every claim ties back to a specific verse; AMPC translation used prominently
- Direct and challenging without being condemning ("Every day you don't declare, that other voice gets another unopposed rep")
- Warm, pastoral, and accessible — not denominationally exclusive, not preachy
- Long-form copy that earns trust before asking for anything
- Use "you" directly; address the reader's exact felt experience before offering the solution

**Compliance constraints:** Standard CAN-SPAM / GDPR. No healthcare, finance, or legal vertical. Faith content should stay grounded in the app's own scriptural framing — avoid guarantee language around outcomes (prosperity, healing, etc.) that goes beyond what the site itself claims.

**Sending volume:** Unknown — defer to ESP limits and list size once confirmed.

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

Based on the freemium model and activation-dependent value delivery:

1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — Drive the first recording within 24–48 hrs of signup; teach the Joshua 1:8 premise; set the daily reminder expectation
2. **Lead-Magnet Nurture (Free → Paid Conversion)** — 5–7 day drip for free users; deepen the scriptural case; address the top objection; introduce Silver plan benefits with the "feel the difference" framing
3. **Win-Back / Re-Engagement** — Reactivate free and paid users who have gone quiet; "that other voice is getting unopposed reps" copy angle maps perfectly onto the brand's own language
