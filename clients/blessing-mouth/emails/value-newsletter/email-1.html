This Week’s Declaration

Speak to the Mountain

“Truly I tell you, whoever says to this mountain, Be lifted up and thrown into the sea! and does not doubt at all in his heart but believes that what he says will take place, it will be done for him.”

Mark 11:23 (AMPC)

Notice what Jesus didn’t say.

He didn’t say whoever prays about the mountain. He didn’t say whoever thinks positively about the mountain, or whoever reads enough scripture about the mountain. He said whoever says to it.

The instruction is to your mouth — not your mind.

Whatever you’re looking at right now — the diagnosis, the marriage that feels like it’s slipping, the number in your account that doesn’t add up, the thing you’ve prayed about so many times you’ve nearly stopped praying — Jesus gave you a specific instruction: say something to it. Not past it. Not about it. To it.

Your voice, carrying God’s Word, is the instrument He chose. That’s not a metaphor. That’s the instruction. This week, give it to this verse — out loud, in your own voice, over your own life.

 

Say This Out Loud

I speak to every mountain standing before me, and I command it to move. No obstacle — in my body, my finances, my relationships, or my mind — is greater than the Word of God at work in my life. I believe with my heart and I declare with my mouth: what God has promised over me will come to pass. I do not waver. I do not go silent. I speak — and I trust the One who backs every word.
