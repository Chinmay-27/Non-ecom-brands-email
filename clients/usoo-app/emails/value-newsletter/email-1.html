💡 One Tip This Week

Skip "hey." Ask one real question.

Five minutes sounds like nothing. It's actually enough — if you don't spend the first 90 seconds on the same four questions everyone asks.

When the call starts, lead with something they haven't heard before. One question that requires a real answer — not "where are you from?" but something like:

→ "What's something you were genuinely proud of this week?"
→ "If you could be really good at one thing you've never tried, what would it be?"
→ "What's a small thing that always makes your day better?"

You don't need to be witty. You don't need a perfect opener. You just need to ask something that makes the other person feel like you're actually paying attention. That's when the conversation gets real — and in five minutes, that's how sparks happen.
