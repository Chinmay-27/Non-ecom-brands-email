# Brand Intake — The Omniverse
**URL:** https://theomniverse.city  
**Date:** 2026-06-04  
**Vertical:** Gaming / Metaverse SaaS  

---

## 1. The Offer

**What does the brand actually sell?**  
A subscription-based 3D pixel-streaming virtual world platform ("Omniverse City") with persistent themed districts, an in-game economy powered by OCX currency, AI digital citizens (DigOzens), creator and builder tools, event hosting infrastructure, and cross-platform game publishing via Epic Games integration. Think of it as a metaverse that actually works — built on Unreal Engine, playable in-browser and standalone, with real commerce and community layered in.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:**  
A persistent digital identity and economic presence. Users aren't just playing a game — they're building a virtual life: owning a residence, running a business, hosting events, and earning OCX through participation.

**Price point & commitment:**  
| Tier | Price | Key unlocks |
|---|---|---|
| Explorer | Free (limited early access) | Platform exploration, events, community |
| OmniFolk | $12.99/mo | 1 free residence, 800 OCX/mo, inventory |
| OmniPreneur | $39.99/mo | 1 free business, retail tools, 2,500 OCX/mo |
| OmniPro | Early access / TBD | Creator SDK, 8,000 OCX/mo, builder consultations |
| Producer | Limited (10 licenses/game) / TBD | Epic Games revenue share, publishing tools |

All subscriptions are monthly. No annual commitment or contract. Free Explorer tier is perpetual.

**Vertical:** SaaS/tech (gaming/metaverse platform with subscription model)

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:**  
Gaming enthusiasts, content creators, and digital entrepreneurs aged roughly 18–35 who want a meaningful virtual presence — not just another game, but a place to build something. Early adopters who followed the original metaverse hype and were disappointed; now looking for a platform that actually delivers community, creativity, and economic opportunity.

**Secondary segments worth separating:**
1. **Explorers/Casuals** — free users attending events, exploring districts, no immediate purchase intent; nurture toward OmniFolk
2. **Residents (OmniFolk)** — community members who want a social home base and in-game economy access
3. **Virtual entrepreneurs (OmniPreneur)** — creators, designers, and small-business owners monetizing shops, venues, or services inside the world
4. **Professional creators / publishers (OmniPro + Producer)** — indie studios, 3D developers, game publishers distributing experiences on the platform

**Problem they're in when they start looking:**  
- Frustrated that existing metaverse platforms (Meta Horizon, Decentraland) never delivered real community or utility
- Want to monetize their creativity but locked out of AAA game development pipelines
- Seeking a persistent digital identity and community that transcends any single game
- Intimidated by 3D creation but hungry for the outcome (Omniverse explicitly lowers the barrier: "no prior experience required")

**Where leads come from today:**  
Social media (LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram, Twitter/X, YouTube), Discord community, gaming partnerships (e.g. Walking Dead / Fortnite crossover mentioned in blog), events via Luma calendar, SEO/blog content, affiliate/partner program.

**How they describe success:**  
"I have a place in this world." / "I'm earning from what I build." / "My community actually shows up here." / "I got in early on something real."

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:**  
Same-day for Explorer signup (self-serve, zero friction). Days-to-weeks for upgrade to OmniFolk or OmniPreneur — requires enough in-world experience to justify committing $12.99–$39.99/mo.

**Free trial / first-session offer:**  
Yes — the free Explorer tier *is* the trial. It's perpetual but limited: no residence, no business, capped OCX access. The platform's events and community drive Explorer-to-paid conversion organically.

**Who/what closes the deal:**  
Fully self-serve upgrade from within the platform dashboard. No sales team or demo call required. Conversion is triggered by hitting the ceiling on free-tier features (wanting a residence, wanting to host events, wanting higher OCX allocation).

**#1 objection before buying:**  
*"Is this platform going to stick around?"* — The brand is in alpha/early access. Users are being asked to subscribe monthly to a world that might not exist in a year. Trust and platform longevity are the core hesitations, especially for the OmniPreneur and Producer tiers where users invest significant time and money.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**The activation event:**  
First meaningful session inside the virtual world — specifically one of:
- Attending a live event and feeling real community presence
- Earning OCX for the first time through participation
- Publishing a first shop, space, or experience and having another user interact with it

**How long does it take?**  
Can happen within the first session (events are accessible to free users), but meaningful activation for paid conversion usually requires 2–3 sessions over days to a week.

**Self-serve or assisted?**  
Self-serve. Platform is navigable without onboarding support, though in-world AI citizens (DigOzens) and community Discord bridge the gap.

*This is the anchor for Campaign 1: Welcome & Onboarding — get new Explorers to their first event or in-world moment within 48 hours of signup.*

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?**  
Yes — monthly subscriptions across OmniFolk, OmniPreneur, OmniPro, and Producer tiers.

**Renewal / repeat cycle:**  
Monthly (no annual). No contract means churn is frictionless — cancellation is one click.

**What does expansion look like?**  
- Tier upgrades: Explorer → OmniFolk → OmniPreneur → OmniPro → Producer
- OCX spending in the marketplace (digital goods, residences, business tools)
- Hosting paid events as an OmniPreneur (drives platform stickiness)
- Producer license + Epic Games revenue share (top-of-funnel for highest-LTV users)
- Affiliate / partner program

**Churn / drop-off problem:**  
Platform is in alpha — the biggest churn driver is disengagement when updates slow or community activity feels thin. Since there's no switching cost (monthly billing), any lull in content, events, or visible progress signals "this platform is dying." Re-engagement and community momentum emails are critical.

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal:**  
Convert free Explorer signups to paid subscribers (OmniFolk → OmniPreneur) and reduce early churn from paid tiers — essentially full lifecycle activation for a pre-revenue-scale platform.

**Target metrics:**  
- Explorer → paid conversion rate (current baseline: unknown)
- Monthly subscriber retention rate (current baseline: unknown)
- Event attendance rate among email subscribers

**Brand voice / tone:**  
Energetic, aspirational, community-first. Uses game-world language (Explorers, OmniFolk, OmniPreneur, districts, eventures, DigOzens). Inclusive framing — "no prior experience required," "We Are All Players." Sense of pioneering: being part of something new and significant. Professional enough for the entrepreneur segment, playful enough for the gaming audience. Email copy should feel like a message from a fellow citizen of the city, not a SaaS marketing team.

**Compliance constraints:**  
Standard CAN-SPAM and GDPR apply (international audience likely). No HIPAA. No financial-services regulation — but in-game currency (OCX) must be described as platform credits, not investment instruments. Avoid any language that implies OCX has real-world monetary value or constitutes a financial product.

**Sending volume:**  
Unknown — likely moderate (list is early-access scale, not millions). Conservative start: 1–2 emails/week per active sequence.

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

Based on the gaps above and the default priority order:

1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — Get new Explorer signups to their first meaningful in-world moment within 48 hours. Address the longevity objection early. 5–7 email sequence triggered by Explorer signup.

2. **Lead-Magnet Nurture → Trial Conversion** — Convert engaged Explorers to OmniFolk or OmniPreneur. Showcase resident stories, OCX earning potential, and what's locked behind the paywall. 7–10 day nurture triggered by activation event (first session) or day-7 inactivity.

3. **Value Newsletter** — Repurpose existing blog content (platform updates, creator spotlights, partnership news) into a weekly or bi-weekly email that keeps the community warm, builds trust in platform momentum, and surfaces upgrade CTAs organically.
