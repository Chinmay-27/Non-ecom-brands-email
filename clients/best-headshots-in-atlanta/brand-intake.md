## 1. The Offer

**What they sell:** One-off professional headshot photography sessions in the Atlanta metro area. Services include corporate/LinkedIn headshots, acting/casting headshots, personal branding portraits, family portraits, and event/wedding coverage.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:** A polished, confidence-inspiring professional image — looking their best on LinkedIn, company websites, resumes, and casting portfolios. Tagline captures it perfectly: *"What we all want is to be photogenic."*

**Price point & commitment:**
- One-off per session (no subscription or retainer)
- Basic headshot package: ~$250 (up to 200 proof images, 5 fully retouched finals)
- Additional retouched images available beyond the base package
- Payment accepted via Cash, Check, PayPal, Venmo, Zelle

**Vertical:** ☑ Local & Service (professional photography)

**Differentiator:** Rich guarantees to be "the best value for your dollar" and brings a marketing/sales background to directing clients — resulting in photos that communicate professional confidence, not just technical quality.

## 2. The Audience

**Primary customer / ICP:** Business professionals, job seekers, and corporate employees in the Atlanta metro seeking updated LinkedIn and corporate headshots. Skews toward professionals in transition (new job, promotion, personal rebrand).

**Secondary segments:**
- Actors, models, and performers needing casting headshots
- Entrepreneurs and small business owners building a personal brand
- Families and individuals wanting professional portraits

**Problem they're in when they start looking:**
- Their LinkedIn photo is old, blurry, or a cropped party/vacation shot
- They just landed a new role or started a job search and need to look the part
- An actor needs updated casting materials
- They feel self-conscious in front of a camera and want a photographer who can coach them

**Where leads come from today:** Thumbtack (primary — dozens of 5-star reviews), Zenfolio portfolio site, word-of-mouth referrals, social media (Facebook/Instagram)

**How customers describe success:** "I finally look professional," "I got compliments on my LinkedIn photo," "I felt comfortable the whole time and the photos turned out amazing"

---

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:** Same day to a few days — inquiry via Thumbtack or contact form, then book and pay per session

**Free trial / free consult / first-session offer:** None identified. Booking is direct with no discovery call or complimentary consultation.

**Who/what closes the deal:** Thumbtack reviews + portfolio gallery do the heavy lifting; inquiries convert via phone or contact form to a booked appointment

**#1 objection before buying:** Price sensitivity — "Is a professional headshot worth $250 when my phone takes decent photos?" Rich counters this proactively with his value guarantee and quality/experience positioning.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**Activation event:** Receiving and reviewing the final retouched images — the moment the client sees how polished and professional they look.

**Timeline:** Typically within days of the session; editing turnaround is not publicly specified but implied to be fast for a one-person operation.

**Self-serve or assisted:** Fully assisted — Rich coaches clients through posing and expression during the session, which is core to the differentiated experience.

*This is the anchor for Campaign 1: Welcome & Onboarding — set expectations before the shoot and maximize the aha moment delivery post-session.*

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?** No — sessions are one-off by nature. Repeat bookings only happen organically (career change, updated look, new project).

**Repeat cycle length:** Typically 1–3 years between headshot updates, triggered by career milestones or major life/appearance changes.

**Expansion opportunities:**
- Additional retouched images beyond the base package
- Add-on portrait services (family, events, weddings)
- Repeat booking for a team/group corporate session

**Churn / drop-off risk:** High — after a one-time transaction there is no structural reason for the client to stay in touch. Without email follow-up, past clients forget the brand entirely when they next need headshots. Rich's strong Thumbtack reviews suggest real advocates exist but are not systematically activated.

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal:** More bookings and better retention/reactivation — the list is almost certainly small, so the priority is converting inquiries to sessions and reactivating past clients.

**Target metrics:** Booking conversion rate from inquiry; repeat/referral booking rate (current baseline unknown)

**Brand voice / tone:** Warm, encouraging, confidence-building. Conversational and professional — approachable to everyday professionals who feel awkward on camera. Rich's marketing background shows in his positioning ("best value guarantee," outcome-oriented language). Not stiff or overly polished; genuine and coach-like.

**Compliance constraints:** Standard CAN-SPAM. No HIPAA, GDPR (US local business), or financial compliance requirements.

**Sending volume:** Unknown list size; assume low-volume, highly personal sends are appropriate.

---

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — triggered on inquiry or booking; sets expectations, builds excitement, reduces no-shows, and maximizes the aha moment at delivery
2. **Booking, Reminders & No-Show** — pre-session prep email (what to wear, what to expect) + day-before reminder; directly reduces no-show cost
3. **Renewal, Anniversary & Expansion / Win-Back** — "It's been a year (or two) — time to update your headshot" sequence for past clients; the clearest recurring revenue lever in a one-off service business
