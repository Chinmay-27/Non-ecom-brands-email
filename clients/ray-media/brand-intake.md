## 1. The Offer

**What does the brand actually sell?**  
Digital marketing services — primarily SEO, video marketing, lead generation, and social media management. Sold as ongoing retainers to small and medium businesses.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:**  
Consistent online visibility and inbound leads that convert to paying customers. The tagline "Get Seen, Get Sold" makes the outcome ladder explicit: attention first, revenue second.

**Price point & commitment:**  
Unknown — no public pricing found. Custom quotes delivered after a discovery call. Industry-typical range for a boutique agency at this scale: $500–$2,000/month on a monthly retainer. *Inferred.*

**Vertical:**  
☑ B2B/professional services — digital marketing agency serving local and online SMBs.

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:**  
Small-to-medium local or online business owners who have a viable product or service but lack the digital presence or in-house expertise to generate consistent leads. Think: a restaurant owner who knows their food is great but can't be found on Google; a contractor with happy customers but zero social presence; an online entrepreneur who has tried posting but seen no traction.

**Secondary segments:**
- Local service businesses (restaurants, home services, healthcare, salons) needing local SEO and Google Maps visibility
- Online businesses / e-commerce brands needing social media content and paid/organic lead gen
- Solo founders and entrepreneurs who are skilled practitioners but not marketers

**Problem they're in when they start looking:**  
Competitors are outranking them, inbound leads have dried up, or a recent attempt at DIY marketing produced no measurable results. They're frustrated, time-poor, and risk-averse about spending more money on marketing.

**Lead sources (inferred):**  
Referrals from existing clients, Facebook organic (Ray Ortiz runs an active Facebook presence under @Raymedia.cc), and SEO content on the site itself (the site hosts blog-style content targeting local/niche business terms).

**How they describe success:**  
"I want the phone to ring." / "I want to show up when someone Googles [my service] near me." / "I want more leads without running ads myself."

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:**  
Days to weeks. The discovery call is the central conversion event.

**Free trial / consult / demo:**  
Yes — free strategy/discovery call. Primary CTA on the homepage: *"Schedule a call with our experts."* No free trial product or lead magnet observed.

**Who/what closes the deal:**  
The discovery call closes to a custom proposal, which closes to a retainer agreement. Likely founder-led sales (Ray Ortiz).

**#1 objection before buying:**  
Skepticism about ROI — *"We've paid for marketing before and it didn't work."* Secondary objection: concern about being locked into a long contract with no results for months (valid for SEO, which has a 3–6 month ramp).

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**Activation event:**  
The first time a client sees a ranking improvement, a new Google Maps impression spike, or a tracked inbound lead that came directly from Ray Media's work. For SEO clients this is often around week 4–8. For video/social clients it can be faster (first viral-ish post, first DM from a prospect).

**Self-serve or assisted:**  
Assisted — Ray Media manages execution. The client's "job" is to approve content and answer the phone when leads come in.

*This is the anchor for Campaign 1: Welcome & Onboarding — set expectations, establish the feedback loop, and celebrate the first win.*

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?**  
Yes — monthly retainer model (inferred). SEO and social management are inherently ongoing services; results degrade if you stop.

**Renewal / repeat cycle:**  
Monthly. No evidence of annual contract options.

**Expansion looks like:**  
- Single service → bundled package (e.g., SEO only → SEO + video marketing + social)
- More content volume (more posts, more videos per month)
- Adding platforms (e.g., start with Google/Facebook, expand to YouTube, TikTok)

**Churn / drop-off risk:**  
The biggest risk is the SEO patience gap — clients who don't see big results in months 1–2 cancel before the compounding effect kicks in. No-show on the initial consult call is a secondary drop-off point.

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal (inferred):**  
More consult bookings → more conversions → retainer growth. The entire funnel bottlenecks at the discovery call, so campaigns that move leads toward booking are highest leverage.

**Target metric:**  
Consult-to-retainer conversion rate; consult booking rate from email.

**Brand voice / tone:**  
Confident, plain-spoken, outcome-first. Speaks like an expert who doesn't need to impress you with jargon — just wants to show you results. Entrepreneurial and direct. Copy uses phrases like "Get Seen, Get Sold" and "elevating your brand's visibility." Not corporate; not overly casual either. Think: knowledgeable friend who happens to run a marketing agency.

**Compliance:**  
Standard CAN-SPAM and GDPR. No special vertical constraints (no healthcare, legal, or finance services offered).

**Sending volume:**  
Unknown — will depend on list size.

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

Based on the lifecycle gaps above and the default priority order (everything is missing, primary motion is consult-booking):

1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — confirm the booking, set expectations, establish trust before the call
2. **Trial/Consult Conversion** — convert booked-but-cold leads and no-shows back into the funnel
3. **Booking Reminders & No-Show Recovery** — reduce no-show rate and recover dropped prospects immediately after a missed call

*(Campaign 2: Lead-Magnet Nurture is skipped until a lead magnet is created. If Ray Media develops a free audit or guide, this becomes #2 and Consult Conversion shifts to #3.)*
