The one thing keeping you off page one of Google

Hey {{first_name}},

Quick question: if someone in your city typed "[your service] near me" into Google right now — would they find you, or would they find your competitor?

For most local business owners, the answer has nothing to do with your website. It comes down to something Google offers completely free — that most businesses either never claimed, or set up once and forgot about.

This Week’s Fix

Finish your Google Business Profile

Your Google Business Profile is the listing that shows up in Maps and the local block at the top of search results. It's how Google decides who to surface when someone nearby searches for what you sell.

A competitor with a complete profile — accurate hours, real photos, recent updates, a handful of reviews — will almost always outrank a business with a half-empty one. Google sees a stale profile and asks: should I really send people here?

Most local businesses fall into one of three camps:

1
 Never claimed it. Google auto-created a profile for their business, but they've never taken ownership.
2
 Set it up once and moved on. Outdated hours, no photos, possibly a wrong address. Google notices the inactivity.
3
 Keeping it current. Regular updates, fresh photos, actively collecting reviews. If this is you, you’re already ahead of most of your market.
 

The 10-minute fix

If you’re in camp 1 or 2, here’s what to do right now:

Step 1 — Go to google.com/business and search for your business name.

Step 2 — If it’s unclaimed, claim it. If you already own it, open the dashboard.

Step 3 — Check for four things: a clear description of what you do, accurate hours, at least 5 real photos, and your website URL. Fill in whatever’s missing.

Businesses that do this consistently see measurable increases in local search impressions — often within 30 days. It’s not flashy, but it’s the fastest lever most local businesses have left untouched.
