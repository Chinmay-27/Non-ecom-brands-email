**Intake date:** 2026-06-04

---

## 1. The Offer

**What they sell:**  
Think+ is the network marketing channel of Think Energy, a licensed retail clean electricity provider. Energy Advisors (EAs) join for $99/year and earn acquisition bonuses + monthly residual commissions by referring residential and small-business customers to Think Energy's electricity plans and community solar programs across 13 deregulated US states + DC (TX, CT, DE, IL, ME, MD, MA, NH, NJ, NY, OH, PA, RI + DC).

**Core outcome the customer is buying:**  
- *Advisors:* A flexible, relationship-based income stream ("turning energy into income") that pays residuals as long as referred customers stay on plan — without inventory, quotas, or monthly fees.  
- *End customers:* 100% clean electricity locked to a competitive fixed rate for 12–36 months, with potential savings vs. default utility supply.

**Price point & commitment:**  
| Buyer | Price | Commitment |
|---|---|---|
| Energy Advisor | $99/year (no monthly fee) | Annual renewal |
| Electricity customer | ~14¢+/kWh (fixed-rate) | 12-, 24-, or 36-month contract |
| Community solar customer | 5%+ annual savings for up to 20 years | Long-term enrollment |

**Vertical:** B2C / MLM network marketing channel within the clean-energy retail sector

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP (Energy Advisors):**  
Entrepreneurial US adults — often existing Think Energy customers — who want supplemental income through relationship-based selling without the overhead of a traditional business. Typically active on social media, trust word-of-mouth, care about sustainability, and are comfortable having peer-to-peer conversations about saving money on utilities.

**Secondary segments:**  
1. *Prospects considering joining* — in discovery phase, evaluating the opportunity
2. *New EAs (0–90 days)* — just enrolled, need hand-holding through first referrals
3. *Active builders* — advancing through the 8-rank comp plan
4. *Dormant EAs* — enrolled but never made a referral or went inactive
5. *End customers* — referred by EAs for electricity or community solar (separate lifecycle)

**Pain points they arrive with:**  
- Variable utility bills with no pricing certainty
- Need for flexible extra income without committing to a franchise or second job
- MLM skepticism — worry about what friends and family will think
- Don't know how to explain energy deregulation compellingly
- Stall out after enrolling their inner circle, unsure how to grow

**Where leads come from:**  
Primarily advisor-driven word-of-mouth and social sharing; company-supported events, webinars, and local community campaigns (Think Local philanthropic platform).

**How they describe success:**  
"My energy bill is covered by what I earn." / "I've built a team that earns while I sleep." / "I switched my friends to clean energy and get paid for it."

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:** Same day to a few days — fully self-serve online signup via a personalized EA referral link.

**Free trial / entry offer:**  
No formal trial. Key risk-reversal hook: *"Enroll your own home and earn back your $99 in your first month."* The Free Energy Club (refer 3 customers → earn up to 100% bill credit) is the customer-side incentive.

**Close mechanism:** Self-serve web signup through advisor referral link, supported by advisor-led 1:1 conversations (phone, text, social DMs, home energy reviews).

**#1 objection:**  
"Is this an MLM / pyramid scheme?" — compounded by founder Michael Fallquist's prior companies (Viridian Energy: $5M FTC settlement; Griddy: bankruptcy). Any email content touching income claims must include FTC-compliant typical-results disclosures.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**For Energy Advisors:**  
Receiving the first acquisition bonus ($20 for an electricity referral, $60 for community solar) or seeing their own home electricity bill offset by their earnings — proof-of-concept that the model works.

**For end customers:**  
First energy bill that shows a lower locked-in rate with a 100% renewable supply label.

**How long / self-serve or assisted?**  
Self-serve; advisor-assisted on the advisory side. First referral can happen within hours of enrollment for motivated advisors.

*→ This is the anchor of Campaign 1: Welcome & Onboarding — the sequence must drive the new EA to (1) enroll their own home and (2) make their first external referral within 7 days.*

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Recurring?** Yes — two layers:  
1. Annual $99 EA membership renewal  
2. Monthly residual commissions as long as referred customers remain active ($1–$3/month per electricity customer; higher for solar)

**Renewal / repeat cycle:** Annual (EA membership); contract term end for customers (12/24/36 months)

**Expansion levers:**  
- Rank advancement (8 tiers from Regional Energy Advisor → Senior Partner) unlocking deeper unilevel commissions (3–10 levels), generation bonuses, Partner Pool distributions, and one-time rank bonuses ($100–$50,000)
- Upsell from electricity to community solar referrals (3x acquisition bonus)
- Adding team members below (downline building)

**Churn / drop-off risks:**  
- EA attrition after exhausting inner circle without rank advancement (~70–80% year-1 MLM drop-off pattern)
- Annual renewal lapse for passive advisors who never gained momentum
- End customers switching providers at contract expiration
- Advisor disengagement during the "messy middle" (after initial enthusiasm, before residuals feel meaningful)

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal:** Likely a combination of more conversions/bookings (getting prospects to enroll as EAs) and better retention (keeping enrolled EAs active through first referral milestone).

**Target metric:** EA activation rate (% who make ≥1 referral within 30 days of enrollment); annual membership renewal rate.

**Brand voice / tone:**  
Optimistic, peer-to-peer, community-first, conversational. Speaks in plain language — avoids MLM jargon ("downline," "recruit"), prefers "build your team," "refer a neighbor," "your community." Sustainability is a genuine value, not a greenwashing badge. Think of a motivated friend who's genuinely excited about a good deal — not a salesperson.

**Compliance constraints:**  
- Energy retail regulations differ by state (13 deregulated markets)  
- FTC income-claim rules: any email referencing EA earnings must include typical-results disclosure  
- CAN-SPAM / CASL compliance required  
- Avoid specific income projections without compliant disclaimers  
- Do not use "unlimited income," "passive income," or earnings guarantees without proper FTC disclosure language

**Sending volume:** Unknown — depends on list size and Web Office ESP capacity.
