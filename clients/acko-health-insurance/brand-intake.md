# Brand Intake — Acko Health Insurance
**Date:** 2026-06-02
**URL:** https://www.acko.com/gi/p/health/buy-base
**Vertical:** Insurance / Fintech

---

## 1. The Offer

**What does the brand sell?**
Direct-to-consumer health insurance — Standard and Platinum annual subscription plans, sold 100% online without agents or brokers. Coverage ranges from ₹10 lakh to unlimited sum insured with cashless treatment at 11,500+ network hospitals across India.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:**
Peace of mind that a medical emergency will not financially devastate them or their family. The promise is 100% of the hospital bill, zero co-pay, zero waiting period — no nasty surprises at discharge.

**Price point & commitment:**
- Entry price: ₹18/day (~₹540/month; ~₹6,500/year for a healthy 25-year-old individual)
- Annual subscription, renewable each year
- No lock-in beyond the policy year; non-renewal lapses the policy

**Vertical:** Insurance / Fintech (direct-to-consumer, digital-native insurer)

**Lead magnets / entry offers:**
- Instant premium calculator (no signup required)
- Free medical check-up for eligible new applicants
- Instant digital policy document (zero paperwork, no waiting)

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:**
Urban Indian professional, age 25–45, smartphone-first, currently either uninsured, underinsured on a group corporate plan that won't follow them between jobs, or fed up with traditional insurer bureaucracy and opaque claim denials.

**Secondary segments worth separating:**
1. **Young professionals (22–35)** — buying individual cover for the first time; price-sensitive; motivated by tax benefit (80D) and parental nudge
2. **Young families (28–42)** — family floater; primary trigger is spouse's pregnancy, first child, or a parent's hospitalisation experience
3. **Mid-career upgraders (35–50)** — own a basic policy; had a bad claim experience elsewhere; actively shopping for better coverage
4. **Senior citizen cover (60+)** — often bought by adult children for parents; trust and hospital network breadth are primary concerns
5. **NRIs** — India-based coverage for self or parents; need remote claims support and digital-first process

**What problem are they in when they start looking?**
- A family member just got hospitalised and they saw the bill
- Their employer's group cover is ending (job change, layoff)
- They missed claim filing or had a claim rejected by their current insurer
- Tax-filing season prompts 80D deduction awareness

**Where do leads come from?**
Primarily paid digital (Google, Meta), SEO/content (blog, comparison sites), embedded insurance via Amazon/Ola/Flipkart partnerships, and some word-of-mouth from satisfied claim customers.

**How they describe success in their own words:**
"They actually paid the full bill." / "It was all cashless, I didn't have to arrange funds." / "No forms, no running around."

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:**
Days to 2–3 weeks. The purchase decision is considered (insurance is high-trust), but Acko's self-serve digital flow can compress the cycle to minutes for a motivated buyer. Most leads browse, compare, and return.

**Free trial / entry offer:**
No free trial. The premium calculator is the main no-commitment entry point — it produces an instant quote with plan comparison. Free medical check-up is offered to eligible applicants after application, acting as a post-consideration sweetener.

**Who/what closes the deal?**
Self-serve digital signup — no agent, no salesperson, no call required. The web/app flow takes the customer from quote to payment to instant policy issuance. This is a key differentiator vs. traditional insurers.

**#1 objection before buying:**
*"Will they actually pay my claim?"* — Trust in a relatively newer insurer (founded 2016, unicorn 2021) vs. LIC or Star Health's decades-long name recognition. The 98% claims settlement rate and ₹400+ crore in claims paid are the primary objection-handlers; the email program should reinforce these repeatedly in the nurture flow.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**Activation event:**
Two moments, in sequence:
1. **Immediate aha:** Receiving the digital policy document within minutes of payment — no waiting, no courier, no agent call. Fast and frictionless.
2. **Definitive aha:** First cashless claim approved within hours at a network hospital, with zero paperwork and 100% of the bill covered.

The email program must bridge the gap between moment 1 (policy in hand) and moment 2 (claim event — which may be months or years away) by making the customer *feel* the value before they ever need to use it.

**Self-serve or assisted?**
Fully self-serve for policy issuance. Claims have a 24/7 support team and a hospital pre-authorisation desk (assisted when it counts).

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?**
Yes — annual subscription. The policy must be renewed each year or it lapses. This is the single most important lifecycle event beyond the initial sale.

**Renewal cycle length:**
Annual (12 months from policy start date).

**What does "expansion" look like?**
- Upgrade from Standard plan → Platinum plan (zero waiting period, higher room cover)
- Add family members to the floater (spouse, children, parents)
- Increase sum insured (especially relevant as inflation erodes ₹10L cover over time)
- Add riders: OPD cover, top-up plans, critical illness add-ons
- Annual 10% inflation protection built into Platinum plan (reduces churn trigger)

**Churn / drop-off problem:**
Annual renewal is the danger window. Customers who have had no claims may question the value ("I paid ₹8,000 and never used it"). Competitors run aggressive renewal-poaching campaigns. The email program must deliver perceived value throughout the year — not just a renewal invoice — so the customer feels the premium is worth it even if they stayed healthy.

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal (inferred):**
Full lifecycle — conversion of quote leads + onboarding + retention at renewal. Given the long consideration cycle and annual renewal cliff, the highest-leverage campaigns are onboarding (trust-building before the first claim) and pre-renewal value narrative.

**Target metrics (inferred):**
- Quote-to-purchase conversion rate
- Year-1 renewal rate (primary retention KPI)
- Cashless claim initiation rate (proxy for activation)
- Referral/NPS-driven new customer acquisition

**Brand voice / tone:**
Direct and empowering, anti-jargon, conversational but confident. The "people before paperwork" philosophy should drive copy. No fear-mongering (don't open with "What if you get cancer tomorrow?"). Lead with simplicity, transparency, and zero — zero waiting, zero co-pay, zero paperwork, zero GST. Short sentences. Active voice. Relatable scenarios over statistics wherever possible.

**Compliance constraints:**
- IRDAI regulated (License #157) — all product descriptions, claim statistics, and comparisons must comply with IRDAI advertising code
- India DPDP Act (Digital Personal Data Protection) — data handling disclosures required
- TRAI DLT registration — required for all commercial SMS and transactional email to Indian mobile numbers
- No absolute claim guarantees — cite "98% claims settlement" rather than "we will always pay your claim"
- No medical advice in email content
- Unsubscribe and sender identification per TRAI regulations

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

Based on the lifecycle gaps and the playbook's default priority:

1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — New policyholders need to feel they made the right decision *before* their first claim. A 4–6 email sequence: confirmation + "your policy explained in plain English" + cashless hospital walkthrough + claim-filing preview + mid-year check-in. Anchor on the 98% settlement stat and real claim stories.

2. **Lead-Magnet Nurture (Quote → Purchase)** — Visitors who used the premium calculator but didn't buy. A 4–5 email sequence over 10–14 days: trust objection (#1 barrier) → plan comparison story → social proof (claim testimonials) → urgency/incentive close (ZERO GST offer, free check-up).

3. **Renewal & Anniversary** — Start 60 days before renewal: value recap ("Here's what your cover protected you against this year") → upgrade story (Standard → Platinum) → renewal CTA with inflation-protection angle. Highest revenue-protection campaign in the portfolio.
