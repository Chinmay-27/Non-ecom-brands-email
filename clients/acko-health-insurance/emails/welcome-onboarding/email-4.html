The waiting-period trap most people find out about too late

Hi {{first_name}}, the fine print that guts most health policies has nothing to do with the premium. It's in three clauses most people never read — until they file a claim.

Every year, thousands of policyholders get a rude surprise at the hospital discharge desk. Not because they weren't covered. Because of clauses buried in page 47 of their policy document — sub-limits, co-pays, and waiting periods.

Here's what each one means, and how your Acko plan handles it.

Trap #1

The waiting period that blocks your claim on day one

Most health policies have a 30-day initial waiting period from the date of purchase — during which no hospitalisation claim (except accidents) will be paid. Then there are disease-specific waiting periods: typically 2–4 years for pre-existing conditions like diabetes, hypertension, or thyroid disorders before those ailments are covered.

You buy a policy, feel protected — and then find out your condition wasn't covered at all when the claim comes in.

How your Acko plan handles this

Platinum plan: Zero waiting period. You're covered from day one — for pre-existing conditions, specific diseases, everything. No 30-day gap, no 2-year wait. Your policy is live the moment you pay.

Standard plan: A 30-day initial wait applies, with standard disease-specific waiting periods. But all accrued waiting-period credit is protected as long as you renew — you never start from zero.

Trap #2

The co-pay that makes you pay even when you're "covered"

A co-pay clause means you pay a fixed percentage of every claim — typically 10%–30% — out of your own pocket, no matter what. On a ₹5 lakh bill, a 20% co-pay leaves you arranging ₹1 lakh at the hospital, even with full insurance.

Co-pays are especially common in policies covering senior citizens, or in "affordable" plans that look cheap until you actually claim.

How your Acko plan handles this

Zero co-pay. On both Standard and Platinum. When Acko covers a hospitalisation, we cover 100% of the eligible bill. You pay nothing at discharge beyond what isn't covered under your policy terms. No percentage, no split, no surprise deduction.

Trap #3

The room-rent cap that triggers a cascade of deductions

Room-rent sub-limits cap the room category you're allowed to stay in. Typically 1%–2% of the sum insured per day. With a ₹5 lakh policy, that's ₹5,000–₹10,000/day — fine in a tier-2 city, but almost no private room in a metro hospital.

The catch: if you book a room above the limit, the insurer proportionally reduces every other charge on the bill — doctor fees, surgery costs, diagnostics — not just the room rent. A ₹3 lakh surgery bill can get cut to ₹1.5 lakh. This is the clause that blindsides people the most.

How your Acko plan handles this

No room-rent sub-limits. Choose any room in any network hospital — single private, deluxe, whatever your doctor recommends. Your sum insured covers the full eligible bill without proportional deductions. The room choice is yours, not a clause in the policy.

For Standard plan members

Covering parents or family? Waiting periods matter more than you think.

If you added parents or family members to your Standard plan, their pre-existing conditions — blood pressure, diabetes, heart issues — sit behind a waiting period. Platinum removes that entirely. Worth considering before the next renewal, especially if your parents are in their 50s or 60s.
