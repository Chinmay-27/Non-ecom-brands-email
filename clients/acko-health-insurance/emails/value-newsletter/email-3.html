How one cashless claim saved this family ₹4.8 lakh

Tuesday evening. Emergency surgery. Total bill: ₹4,80,000. Here’s exactly how it unfolded — and what Priya never had to do.

Most insurance stories start with a crisis. This one does too — but it ends very differently from most.

Priya Mehta (name changed with consent) is 34 and lives in Pune with her husband Vikram and their seven-year-old. In March, Vikram came home from work with stomach pain. By 10 PM it was severe. By midnight, an ultrasound confirmed acute appendicitis. He needed emergency surgery — that night.

“My first thought was: how much is this going to cost us? My second thought was: wait, we have Acko.”

— Priya Mehta, Acko Platinum Member, Pune

They drove to Apollo Hospitals — an Acko network hospital. At admissions, Priya showed Vikram’s digital Acko ID card from the app. The hospital’s desk submitted a pre-authorisation request to Acko directly.

Priya didn’t call her bank. She didn’t transfer emergency funds. She didn’t fill a single claim form that night.

Three hours later, Acko approved the pre-auth. Vikram went into surgery. Three days later, he was discharged — fully recovered. The final hospital bill came to ₹4,80,000. Covered in full, cashless, settled directly between Acko and the hospital.

How it works at the hospital

Know the drill before you need it.

1

Go to an Acko network hospital

Show your digital Acko ID card (it’s in the app). Tell the admissions desk you have cashless cover. That’s it for your part.
2

The hospital sends pre-auth to Acko

The admissions team handles the pre-authorisation request directly with Acko’s desk. No forms for you to sign. No funds to arrange. Acko’s team reviews and responds.
3

Approved. Treated. Walk out.

Acko settles directly with the hospital. At discharge you sign nothing with a rupee sign attached. The bill is between Acko and the hospital.
