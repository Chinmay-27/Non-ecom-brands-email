3 things to check in any health policy
(yours included)

Most people find out about the fine print at the worst possible time — during discharge. Here's how to read your policy before that happens.

Hey {{first_name}},

Here's something that happens every week in India: a family gets a hospital bill. Their insurance should cover it. But it doesn't — not fully. The fine print had a clause nobody read.

Three things cause most of those surprises. Take 2 minutes to check them in your own policy — whether it's with Acko or anyone else.
1

Room-rent sub-limits

Many policies cap the hospital room you can occupy at 1–2% of your sum insured per day. That's ₹1,000–2,000/day for a ₹10 lakh cover — a general ward in many cities. If you pick a room above that cap, the insurer proportionally reduces every other claim component too.

Check your policy for: a "room rent limit" or "room rent sub-limit" clause. If you see one, do the math against local hospital rates.

 

In your Acko plan, there are no room-rent sub-limits. Any room, any hospital in the network.
2

Waiting periods for specific diseases

Most standard policies include a 2–4 year waiting period for certain conditions — joint replacements, hernia, cataracts, kidney stones, and more. Even if you're mid-treatment, a claim for a listed condition filed before the waiting period ends will be rejected.

Check your policy for: an "Exclusion — Waiting Period" section. Count the years from your policy start date.

 

Acko Platinum has zero waiting period for most conditions from day one. On the Standard plan, waiting periods apply — check your policy schedule for specifics.
3

Co-pay clauses

A co-pay means you pay a fixed percentage of every claim bill — typically 10–20% — before the insurer covers the rest. On a ₹5 lakh bill, a 20% co-pay leaves ₹1 lakh on you, regardless of how big your sum insured is. It's often applied to senior cover or non-network hospital claims.

Check your policy for: a "Co-payment" or "Co-pay" clause under the payment terms section.

 

Acko has zero co-pay on all plans. 100% of the eligible hospital bill is covered. Nothing comes out of your pocket at discharge.

The 5-minute checklist

Open your policy document right now and Ctrl+F for these three terms:

✓

"Room rent" — is there a daily cap?
✓

"Waiting period" — which conditions, how long?
✓

"Co-pay" — any percentage you owe per claim?
