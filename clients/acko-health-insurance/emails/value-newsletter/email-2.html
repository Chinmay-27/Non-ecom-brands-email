What is Section 80D, exactly?

Section 80D of the Income Tax Act lets you deduct the premium you pay for health insurance from your taxable income. Less taxable income means a smaller tax bill — straightforward as that.

Who you cover Max deduction
Self, spouse & children ₹25,000
Parents (below 60) +₹25,000
Parents (senior citizens, 60+) +₹50,000

Max combined deduction: ₹75,000 (self + senior parents). Subject to applicable conditions.

Quick example

Say your annual Acko premium is ₹12,000 and you're in the 20% tax bracket. Claim it under 80D and you save ₹2,400 in tax — real money back, every year you stay covered.

In the 30% bracket? That same premium saves you ₹3,600.

How to claim it — three steps

1

Download your premium receipt

Log into your Acko account → Policy details → Download premium certificate. Done in under a minute.
2

Enter it in your ITR under Chapter VIA

Look for Section 80D in the deductions section. Enter the premium amount for self/family, and separately for parents if applicable.
3

Keep the receipt handy

You don't submit it with the return, but keep it on file. Your CA will ask for it.

One more thing worth knowing

The 80D deduction is capped at the premium you actually paid. If you increased your sum insured this year, your deductible amount could be higher — it goes up with the premium.

And if medical costs have risen since you first bought your policy — they have — it might be worth checking whether your current sum insured still covers what a real hospitalisation would cost today. Your cover protects you both ways: at the hospital and at tax time.
