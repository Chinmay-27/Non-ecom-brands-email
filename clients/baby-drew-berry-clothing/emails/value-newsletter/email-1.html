The 3-second softness test every parent should know

Hi {{first_name}},

Last week a mama reached out to ask how she could tell — really tell — if a fabric was safe for her newborn's skin before she bought it. She'd been burned by three "ultra soft" onesies that turned out to be scratchy after two washes.

I've heard this more times than I can count. So I pulled this tip from our blog — the one Jill wrote last winter — because it deserves to land in your inbox, not just in a search result.

The 3-Second Softness Test

Before you add anything to your cart, rub the fabric label against the inside of your wrist — not your palm, not your fingertips. Your inner wrist is the closest your body has to newborn skin in terms of sensitivity.

If it catches or drags in under three seconds, put it back. A fabric that's gentle enough for baby will glide with no friction at all. Bamboo viscose and fine-knit pima cotton will pass every time. Most polyester blends won't.

This works in stores, at baby showers, even when sorting through hand-me-downs. Three seconds. Trust your wrist.

The reason we chose bamboo viscose for Drew Berry's core line isn't a marketing decision — it's a personal one. Our own baby came home with skin so sensitive that even "baby-safe" detergents left a rash. We spent months researching before we found fabrics that actually worked. Bamboo viscose, when woven right, is naturally hypoallergenic, temperature-regulating, and about three times softer than cotton by tensile measure.

That's not a claim. That's what we'd hand to our own child. And it's what we send to yours.
