## 1. The Offer

**What they sell:** Baby and toddler clothing sold direct-to-consumer via e-commerce boutique. Product range covers bodysuits, two-piece sets, dresses, pajama sets, bamboo viscose garments, swimwear, and accessories (hats, headbands, mittens, socks). Collections are organized by gender (boys/girls), material (bamboo), and season.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:** Comfort and safety for their baby, expressed through soft high-quality fabrics — plus the emotional satisfaction of dressing their child in something meaningful and beautiful. Parents aren't just buying clothes; they're buying peace of mind and a feeling of being a good, thoughtful caregiver.

**Price point & commitment:** Budget-friendly to mid-range. Flash sales push individual items as low as ~$6.99; typical items estimated at $15–45. All purchases are one-off transactions — no subscription, membership, or retainer model identified.

**Vertical:** ☑ Other: **E-commerce / Baby & Toddler Apparel**

## 2. The Audience

**Primary customer / ICP:** New and expecting parents — primarily mothers — aged 25–40, shopping for newborns through toddlers (0–3 years). Quality-conscious and research-driven; they read fabric labels, care about bamboo vs. synthetic, and want clothing that is both safe for sensitive skin and aesthetically pleasing for photos and social sharing. Active on Instagram.

**Secondary segments worth separating:**
1. **Gift buyers** — grandparents, aunts, uncles, and friends buying for baby showers or birthdays. Driven primarily by aesthetics and price; less likely to care about the brand story but respond to bundle visuals and gift-ready packaging.
2. **Repeat parents** — already trust quality brands, buy faster, respond well to "new arrivals" and seasonal drops without needing heavy persuasion.

**Problem they're in when they start looking:** Frustration with itchy, rough, or poorly-made baby clothes that irritate sensitive skin and fall apart quickly. Also the overwhelm of choosing from dozens of generic fast-fashion baby brands with no soul or story behind them.

**Where leads come from today:** Instagram (@babydrewberry) is the primary traffic driver; Facebook (BabyDrewBerry); organic search via blog content (SEO-targeted parenting articles); flash sale urgency on-site.

**How they describe success:** "My baby is comfortable and happy," "it washed perfectly and still looks new," "so soft — even softer than I expected," "it's exactly what I wanted for the photos."

---

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:** Same day. This is an impulse-adjacent purchase driven by flash sales, emotional brand appeal, and Instagram discovery. Parents see a cute outfit, land on the site, and buy within a single session — especially when a countdown timer is running.

**Free trial / consult / first-session offer:** None. There is no trial or consultation mechanism. Flash sale pricing and countdown timers serve as the friction-reducer instead.

**Who/what closes the deal:** Self-serve e-commerce checkout. The site closes the sale; Instagram and flash sale urgency drive the visitor in. No human sales touch.

**#1 objection before buying:** "I can't feel the fabric before I buy it." Parents buying for sensitive baby skin are skeptical of softness and quality claims they can't verify tactilely. The bamboo viscose positioning and emotional brand story help overcome this, but it remains the primary hesitation for first-time buyers.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**The activation event:** The moment a parent holds the item in their hands and puts it on the baby for the first time — they feel the fabric quality and see baby's comfortable, happy reaction. This is the proof point that every pre-purchase objection was wrong.

**How long does it take:** 3–7 days from purchase (shipping time). Entirely self-serve once ordered.

**Why this matters for Campaign 1:** The post-purchase window (days 1–7 pre-delivery, day of delivery) is the highest anxiety and highest engagement period. A welcome/onboarding email sequence that bridges the gap between purchase and first wear — with fabric care tips, styling ideas, and a "you made a great choice" reassurance — will reduce buyer's remorse and prime the social share moment.

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?** No formal recurring mechanism. Repeat purchase is organic and lifecycle-driven — baby's rapid growth (sizing up every 2–4 months) is the natural repurchase trigger, but there is no system in place to capture it.

**Repeat cycle length:** 2–4 months for size-based repurchase; seasonal collection drops 4× per year as an additional trigger.

**Expansion opportunities:**
- Upsell to the bamboo viscose premium line (higher margin, premium positioning)
- Seasonal/themed collection drops (holiday, summer, back-to-school)
- Accessories add-ons: hats, headbands, socks, mittens as low-friction add-to-cart items
- Gift buyers can be converted to self-purchasers with a "for your own little one" angle

**Churn / drop-off problem:** Baby ages out of the 0–3 year sizing window, ending the natural purchase cycle. The bigger near-term risk is brand forgetting — the 2–4 month gap between purchases is long enough for parents to forget the brand exists and shop elsewhere for the next size up. No re-engagement system currently identified.

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal:** Full lifecycle build — nothing exists; starting from zero.

**Priority campaigns to build first:**
1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — post-purchase sequence that bridges the gap to first wear, reassures quality, primes the social share, and plants the seed for repeat purchase
2. **Lead-Magnet Nurture** — create an email opt-in incentive (e.g., sizing guide PDF, "bamboo vs. cotton" fabric guide, or first-order discount) and build the nurture sequence for pre-purchase subscribers
3. **Value Newsletter + Size-Up Reminders** — repurpose blog content into a monthly email; layer in milestone-triggered size-up reminders tied to baby's age (the natural repurchase event)

**Target metrics:** Unknown baseline; first targets should be list growth rate, post-purchase email CTR, and repeat purchase rate.

**Brand voice / tone:** Warm, intimate, emotionally resonant. Poetic but never overwrought. Family-centered storytelling — the Drew Berry legacy ("One Love") should underpin every email without being heavy-handed. Avoid corporate marketing speak. Simple, honest, genuine. Think: a letter from one parent to another, not a promotional blast.

**Compliance constraints:** Standard CAN-SPAM. US-based. No healthcare, financial, or HIPAA constraints. GDPR may apply if shipping internationally — unknown.

**Sending volume:** Unknown; assume modest list size for a boutique brand; start conservative (1–2 emails/week maximum).

## Output: First 3 Campaigns

1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — post-purchase sequence (3–5 emails): order confirmation → shipping anticipation → "your package is arriving" → first-wear moment + care guide → social share ask + cross-sell accessories
2. **Lead-Magnet Nurture** — create email opt-in (sizing guide or fabric education PDF), then 4-email pre-purchase nurture sequence converting subscribers to first-time buyers
3. **Value Newsletter + Size-Up Triggers** — monthly parenting/fabric content email repurposing blog; plus triggered size-up reminder at baby age milestones (3m, 6m, 9m, 12m, 18m, 24m)
