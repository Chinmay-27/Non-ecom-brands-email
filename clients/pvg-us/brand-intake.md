# Brand Intake — Paradigm Venture Group (PVG US)
**URL:** https://www.pvgus.com | **Founded:** 2021 | **HQ:** Wolcott, CT | **Team size:** 11–50

---

## 1. The Offer

**What does the brand actually sell?**
Enterprise interoperability consulting and AI-powered workflow automation. Services span:
- **Enterprise System Interoperability** — bridging legacy platforms to modern cloud architectures via a semantic data layer (Single Source of Truth)
- **AI Enterprise Ecosystem** (Friday_OS + Krista AI) — 15+ AI capabilities, 200+ enterprise integrations, 4–6 week deployment
- **AI-Powered Sales Ecosystem / Sales Accelerator** — automated customer acquisition systems, calendar-filling pipelines
- **Conversational AI** — AI phone agents capable of 10–40 minute calls
- **Build a Custom App** — low-code custom application development delivered in weeks
- **Data Cleaning** — supply chain and value analysis platforms
- **Contract Management** — third-party lifecycle management
- **Revenue Cycle Management** — converts bad patient debt into cash flow
- **Rebate & Compliance Management** — aligns surgeons, supply chain, and administration
- **Advisory Services** — strategic consulting

**What's the core outcome the customer is buying?**
Elimination of data silos and administrative burden — unified systems, automated workflows, and a real-time single source of truth so leadership can make better decisions and revenue stops leaking through manual processes.

**Price point & commitment:**
Not publicly listed. Enterprise B2B project-based engagements and/or retainer. The AI Enterprise Ecosystem cites a 4–6 week deployment window, signaling a structured delivery model. Estimated range: $25K–$250K+ per engagement. All deals initiated via discovery call — no self-serve.

**Vertical:** ☑ B2B/professional + healthcare specialization (also serves enterprise operations and sales-led businesses)

## 2. The Audience

**Primary customer / ICP:**
Healthcare operations and technology leaders — COO, CIO, VP of Supply Chain, Revenue Cycle Directors — at hospitals, integrated delivery networks (IDNs), and health systems. They have a stack of disconnected legacy systems (EHR, ERP, billing platform) and a team doing manual work to compensate.

**Secondary segments worth separating:**
1. **Enterprise operations leaders** (supply chain, procurement, contract management) — non-healthcare, but same silo/integration pain
2. **Sales-led businesses** seeking AI-driven customer acquisition (Sales Ecosystem / Conversational AI product line)

**What problem are they in when they start looking?**
Either: (a) a specific pain point has become unignorable — billing errors, audit failures, supply chain cost overruns, or staff turnover from manual-work burnout; or (b) a strategic initiative (AI adoption, EHR migration, M&A integration) has exposed how broken their data infrastructure is.

**Where do leads come from today?**
LinkedIn (active company page), events (they host/attend — "Revolutionize" event mentioned), referrals, and search. GPT-5 Readiness Audit serves as a top-of-funnel content offer.

**How do they describe success in their own words?**
"We finally have one place to see everything." / "Our billing team stopped doing manual reconciliation." / "We can actually trust the numbers now."

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying customer:** Weeks to months (complex enterprise B2B)

**Is there a free trial, free consult, demo, or first-session offer?**
Yes — **GPT-5 Readiness Audit** (free, promoted via homepage banner). This is the primary lead magnet. No product trial. Discovery call is the standard next step from any CTA.

**Who/what closes the deal?**
A human salesperson following a booked discovery call → scoped proposal → signed engagement. Primary CTAs on the site are "Contact Us" and "Get Started" — both lead to contact form or call booking.

**What's the #1 objection or hesitation before buying?**
Integration risk: *"Will this break what we already have?"* / *"Our systems are too complex / too custom for a vendor to integrate."* Secondary objection: budget justification for a high-ticket, intangible-outcome engagement.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**What's the single action a new customer must take to *get* the value?**
See their first automated workflow execute end-to-end with zero manual intervention — e.g., EHR data flowing cleanly into the billing platform and generating a correct claim, or a CRM update triggering an ERP inventory adjustment automatically.

For the AI Enterprise Ecosystem specifically: the moment Friday_OS answers a complex operational query by pulling live data from multiple integrated systems in real-time.

**How long does that take? Is it self-serve or assisted?**
Assisted, 4–6 weeks for AI Enterprise Ecosystem deployment. Custom app builds positioned as "weeks." Fully hands-on engagement — not self-serve.

*This becomes the anchor of Campaign 1: Welcome & Onboarding — set expectations, narrate the journey to first workflow live, reduce anxiety about integration complexity.*
