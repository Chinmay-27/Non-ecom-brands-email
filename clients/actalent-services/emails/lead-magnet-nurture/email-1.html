The gap the report exposes — and why it compounds

Engineering teams in regulated industries face a compounding problem that standard recruiting wasn't built to solve. The issue isn't that good candidates don't exist — they do. The issue is that the conventional hiring path is too slow for the pace of project work:

1

Niche skills don't sit in a general talent pool. A power systems engineer who holds a utility-grade protection relay certification, or a CRA with IND-phase oncology trial experience, isn't discoverable through a 30-day job posting.
2

Compliance exposure grows with every week of vacancy. Regulated work doesn't pause for open reqs. Gaps in quality or safety-critical roles create audit risk that's often worse than the schedule risk.
3

The mis-hire cost in niche disciplines is high. A wrong fit in a specialized role takes months to identify and months more to correct — by which point the project milestone has already moved.
