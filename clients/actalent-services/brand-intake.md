# Brand Intake — Actalent Services
**URL:** https://www.actalentservices.com  
**Date:** 2026-06-04  
**Vertical:** B2B/Professional — Engineering & Sciences Staffing + Technical Services

---

## 1. The Offer

**What they sell:** Two interlocking service lines:
1. **Talent Solutions** — contract, contract-to-hire, and direct-placement staffing for engineering and sciences roles
2. **Technical Services** — project-based engineering services (systems/software, transmission & distribution, grid automation, mechanical/electrical, manufacturing) and sciences services (clinical development, scientific support) plus project/quality management consulting

**Core outcome the customer is buying:**
- *Employer clients:* Get the specialized, job-ready engineers and scientists they need — fast — without the risk and overhead of building internal recruiting capacity for niche roles.
- *Candidates:* Land a meaningful placement in a high-impact engineering or sciences role aligned to their expertise.

**Price point & commitment:** Not publicly disclosed. Staffing operates on a markup model (percentage of contractor bill rate) under master service agreements. Technical services are SOW/project-based. All deals are enterprise B2B; no self-serve or listed pricing. Commitment ranges from short-term contracts (weeks) to multi-year staffing relationships.

**Vertical:** ☑ B2B/Professional (enterprise staffing and technical services)

---

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP (employer/client side):**
Engineering directors, VP-level R&D leaders, project management office heads, and talent acquisition managers at mid-to-large enterprises in:
- Life Sciences (pharma, biotech, CROs, medical devices)
- Aerospace & Defense
- Utilities & Construction (transmission/distribution, grid modernization)
- Transportation
- Consumer & Industrial Products
- Semiconductor

They have active headcount gaps or project resource shortfalls and cannot fill niche roles fast enough through standard recruiting.

**Secondary segments:**
- Engineering job seekers (early-career through senior, seeking contract or perm roles in STEM disciplines)
- Sciences / clinical candidates (CRAs, lab scientists, clinical research coordinators)
- Current contractors on active placements (loyalty, re-engagement, expansion)

**Problem state when they start looking:**
- Project timeline is slipping due to a skills gap they can't fill internally
- A new regulatory or technology requirement demands expertise they don't have in-house
- Headcount freeze prevents direct hiring but project deliverables still require resources

**Where leads come from:** Inbound inquiry form, referrals from existing clients, industry events, SEO/content (Insights blog + market reports), and direct outreach by account managers.

**How they describe success:** "Actalent eliminated schedule risk, ensured quality, and protected margin." Clients want on-time delivery with qualified talent; candidates want career advancement and a trusted placement partner.

---

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact → paying client:** Weeks to months for new enterprise accounts; days-to-weeks for repeat clients with active requisitions.

**Free trial / consult / demo:** Yes — free consultation. The contact form captures inquiry type, primary need, and timeline (from "Immediate Need (<1 month)" to "Seeking Consultation"), routing to a dedicated sales rep. No structured demo product.

**Who/what closes the deal:** Human account manager. Flow: inbound inquiry → qualification call → proposal or SOW → contract signature. No self-serve close path.

**#1 objection before buying:** Staffing markup cost versus direct hire, offshore options, or a lower-margin competitor. Secondary: confidence that Actalent's talent pool depth and quality in a specific niche justifies the engagement.

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**For employer clients:** Receiving the first batch of qualified candidate submittals — ideally within 48–72 hours of requisition — where at least one hire-quality profile matches the role. The activation event is "first successful placement goes to work."

**For candidates:** Being matched and submitted for a relevant role within days of registering, ideally resulting in a client interview. The activation event is "first interview scheduled."

Both are assisted (handled by account managers and recruiters), not self-serve. Time-to-value is days to weeks depending on role complexity.

*This becomes the anchor of Campaign 1: Welcome & Onboarding.*

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?** Yes — structurally recurring. Staffing contracts roll as long as the project or need continues. Technical services SOWs lead to follow-on projects. Revenue is relationship-recurring rather than subscription-automatic.

**Renewal / repeat cycle:** Contractor placements renew at end-of-assignment (weeks to months). Enterprise MSAs are reviewed annually. Repeat requisitions from active clients are ongoing.

**Expansion looks like:**
- Client adds new requisition types (e.g., adds sciences staffing after starting with engineering)
- Client upgrades from talent solutions to bundled technical services
- Contractor headcount scales up on an existing account
- New business unit or geography within the same enterprise client

**Churn / drop-off problems:**
- Client goes dormant (no new requisitions submitted for 90+ days) — often when a project wraps and the next cycle hasn't started
- Contractor is converted to direct hire by client (revenue-ending event, though sometimes a relationship win)
- Candidate talent pool goes cold between placements (no re-engagement = candidate joins a competitor's roster)
- New procurement contact at client doesn't have the prior relationship — account resets to cold

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal (assumed):** Full lifecycle — convert inquiry form leads, retain active client accounts, re-engage dormant clients and candidates.

**Priority given lifecycle gaps:** 
1. Welcome & Onboarding (client-side and candidate-side; no evidence of automation)
2. Trial/Consult Conversion (post-consultation nurture to close the deal)
3. Win-Back / Re-Engagement (dormant clients with lapsed requisitions, dormant candidate pool)

**Target metric:** Conversion rate from consultation inquiry to signed contract (client); placement rate from registration to first interview (candidate). CTR on nurture emails as leading indicator.

**Brand voice / tone:** Professional, forward-looking, people-first. Uses outcome language ("eliminate schedule risk," "protect margin," "connecting passion with purpose"). Moderately formal — B2B but warm. Avoid overly salesy or aggressive copy; authority-through-expertise is the right register.

**Compliance constraints:** CAN-SPAM / CASL required. Staffing context — avoid guarantees on placement timelines, compensation, or job outcome. Life sciences content should avoid clinical efficacy claims. Standard B2B consent and unsubscribe practices.

**Sending volume:** Unknown; assume enterprise-scale list with strong segmentation needs (employer vs. candidate, discipline, geography, urgency).

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

1. **Welcome & Onboarding** (employer-side) — automated sequence triggered by contact form submission, sets expectations, introduces the account manager, and drives toward the first consultation call
2. **Trial/Consult Conversion** — post-consultation nurture series for employer prospects who haven't signed; addresses cost objection, reinforces quality, shares relevant case studies
3. **Win-Back / Re-Engagement** — reactivates dormant client accounts (no requisition in 90+ days) and dormant candidate profiles with market intelligence hooks and a low-friction re-engagement CTA
