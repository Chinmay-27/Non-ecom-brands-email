## 1. The Offer

**What they sell:** Fractional co-ownership stakes in luxury sailing catamarans. The standard unit is a 1/6 share giving the owner up to 5 weeks/year on board; premium vessels offer 1/4 shares with up to 8 weeks. The fleet (8+ yachts, 55–100 ft) migrates between the Mediterranean in summer and the Caribbean in winter. Full yacht management, crew, maintenance, and charter brokerage are bundled — owners have zero operational involvement.

A separate **yacht charter business** runs alongside ownership, letting prospective buyers experience the fleet before committing, and generating revenue that offsets co-owner costs.

**Core outcome the customer is buying:** The private-yacht lifestyle — sailing the Mediterranean and Caribbean with a professional crew, on a boat that feels like yours — without the $3M–$10M sticker price of sole ownership, without the 47 idle weeks per year, and with charter income that can cover the mortgage payment.

## 2. The Audience

**Primary ICP:** Affluent US-based individuals, typically 40–65, with $500K+ net worth. Business owners, executives, and retirees who already travel to or aspire to sail the Mediterranean and Caribbean. They have the means but not the time or appetite for full ownership overhead.

**Secondary segments:**
1. **Lifestyle buyers** — experience and status are primary; financial return is secondary
2. **Investment-minded buyers** — focused on charter income covering carrying costs; want to see the math
3. **Aspiring first-time owners** — always dreamed of yacht ownership; NexGen makes it feel possible for the first time
4. **Existing charter clients** — already spending $20K–$80K/year renting weeks; open to owning equity instead

**Problem they're in when they start looking:** Either (a) they've charted before and resent paying rent with no upside, or (b) they've priced a full yacht purchase and been shocked by the total cost of ownership. They're searching for a smarter middle path.

**Lead sources:** Primarily inbound — paid search, direct web traffic, word of mouth in sailing/yachting communities. Brochure download and inventory email subscription capture top-of-funnel.

**How they describe success:** "I have five weeks a year on a beautiful boat, I don't think about maintenance, and the charter income basically pays for itself."

## 3. The Sales Cycle

**Time from first contact to paying customer:** Weeks to months — typically 4–12 weeks. This is a $250K–$4M+ decision requiring vessel selection, financing arrangement, legal review, and scheduling alignment with co-owners.

**Free trial / entry offer:** No free trial of ownership. However:
- Free glossy brochure (digital) available on request
- Email list for new inventory alerts
- Financing information request
- The separate **charter service functions as an experiential trial** — prospects can book a charter week and experience the fleet before committing to ownership

**Who/what closes the deal:** Fully sales-assisted. Inbound inquiry (web form or phone) → sales call → personalized proposal with vessel options and projected charter income → contract signing. No self-serve checkout exists.

**#1 objection before buying:** Trust and legitimacy — "Is this a real deal or will I get burned?" Mixed online reviews and low category awareness mean skepticism is high. Secondary objection: scheduling — "Will I actually get the weeks I want, or will co-owners block me out?"

---

## 4. The "Aha" / First-Value Moment

**The activation event:** The new co-owner's **first week on board** — stepping onto their yacht in a Mediterranean cove or Caribbean anchorage, greeted by a professional crew, with zero logistics to manage. That's when the purchase becomes real and the value becomes visceral.

**Pre-purchase proxy aha:** Receiving a personalized brochure or proposal that shows them "this specific boat, these specific weeks, at this price, generating this much charter income." The abstract becomes concrete.

**Assisted or self-serve:** Fully assisted. Expect white-glove handoff from sales to onboarding coordinator to vessel crew.

*This anchors Campaign 1 (Welcome & Onboarding) around getting new owners from signed contract → first booking confirmed → first week aboard as quickly and excitedly as possible.*

---

## 5. Retention & Recurring Revenue

**Is revenue recurring?** Yes — annual management fees and seasonal booking cycles (Med summer / Caribbean winter) create natural recurring touchpoints. Charter income distributions keep owners financially engaged year-round.

**Renewal / repeat cycle:** Annual seasonal booking windows. Owners re-engage with NexGen each season to lock in their weeks.

**Expansion looks like:**
- Upgrading from a smaller to a larger / newer vessel
- Purchasing a second share on a different yacht (different size category or destination)
- Referring a friend or family member who becomes a co-owner on the same vessel

**Churn / drop-off risk:**
- Charter income underperforms stated projections
- Scheduling friction with co-owners (can't get desired weeks)
- Yacht condition or crew quality disappointments
- Owner decides to sell share if secondary market is accessible
- Competitor enters with a better-priced or better-managed program

## 8. Goals & Constraints

**Primary goal:** More conversions/bookings — converting brochure downloaders and inquiry calls into signed co-ownership contracts. Secondary: lead generation and awareness in a niche category.

**Target metric:** Inquiry-to-contract conversion rate; number of new co-owners per quarter. Current baseline unknown.

**Brand voice / tone:** Aspirational yet accessible. Luxury but not stuffy — conversational warmth ("Cheers to you"). Speaks to the dream first, finances second. Avoids dense nautical jargon. Think: knowledgeable friend who owns a yacht and wants to share the secret.

**Colors:** Navy (#0D1E3E) · Gold (#C4A052) · White (#FFFFFF) · Deep navy text (#1A2340)

**Compliance constraints:** Standard CAN-SPAM / GDPR. Investment/return claims (charter income, cash-on-cash projections) must use hedged language: "projected," "historically," "up to." No HIPAA or sector-specific licensing constraints identified, but return claim copy requires legal review before sending.

**Sending volume:** Unknown; list likely small. Start conservative (1–2x/week max) and monitor deliverability.

## Output: First 3 Campaigns to Build

Based on the gap analysis above and the default priority order:

1. **Welcome & Onboarding** — New co-owners (and hot prospects post-call) need an immediate, high-touch sequence that reinforces the purchase, explains what happens next, and builds excitement toward the first booking. This is where the brand's aspirational voice shines.

2. **Lead-Magnet Nurture** — Brochure downloaders and inventory subscribers are the largest cold segment. A 5–7 email sequence (over 3–4 weeks) educating leads on the ownership model, addressing the trust objection, showing real charter income math, and prompting a call is the clearest revenue unlock.

3. **Trial/Consult Conversion** — Prospects who book an intro call but don't sign within 2 weeks need a reactivation sequence: social proof (owner testimonials), objection handling (scheduling fears, ROI concerns), and a soft deadline (e.g., limited shares remaining on a specific vessel).
