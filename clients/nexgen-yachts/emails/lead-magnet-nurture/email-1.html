The Question Everyone Has

"What if I can never get the weeks I actually want?"

It's the right thing to ask. Here's how our allocation system actually works:

1

Priority Request Window (60 days out)

Each co-owner submits their top three preferred weeks in advance. Priority rotates each year so no single owner monopolizes peak season.
2

Owner Calendar Visibility

You see the full owner calendar online. Open weeks outside peak periods are often abundant — and can be reserved on a rolling basis any time of year.
3

Unbooked Weeks Go to Charter

Any weeks you don't use aren't wasted — they go into the charter pool and generate revenue that flows back to you. You're incentivized to plan, but you're never penalized for flexibility.
 

The Honest Comparison

Chartering vs. owning a share

If you've been chartering for a few years, you already know the math isn't on your side. A crewed week on a 60-foot catamaran in the Med typically runs $20,000–$35,000 all-in. Five weeks: $100,000–$175,000. Every year. With nothing to show for it.

Chartering

5 weeks/year
Annual spend: $100K–$175K
Equity built: $0
Charter income: None
After 5 years: $500K–$875K spent

Co-Owning a Share

Lagoon 65 · 1/6 share
Net annual cost: ~$14K–$24K*
Equity stake: $575K asset
Charter income: Quarterly distributions*
After 5 years: Equity + 25 weeks sailed

After five years of chartering at those rates, you've spent half a million dollars or more and own nothing. After five years as a co-owner, you hold an equity position in an asset — one that has been generating charter income the entire time.

That's not a pitch. That's arithmetic.
