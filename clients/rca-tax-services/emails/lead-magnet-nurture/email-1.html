The problem isn't your CPA. It's what they're hired to do.

Most CPAs are hired to file a correct, compliant return. They're very good at that. But filing and planning are two completely different jobs — and the strategies that actually move your tax rate require decisions made before December 31, not after.

Retirement-plan design, entity structuring, capital-gains timing, cost segregation on real estate — none of these show up on a tax return after the fact. They have to be engineered ahead of time. Which means if nobody is doing that proactively for you right now, you're likely leaving money on the table this year, the same way you did last year.
