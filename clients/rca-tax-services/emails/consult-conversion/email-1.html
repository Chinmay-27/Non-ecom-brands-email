A Note On Your CPA

Your CPA is doing exactly what they were hired to do: file an accurate return.

That's not a criticism — compliance is important. But accurate filing and proactive tax planning are two completely different jobs. A filing-focused CPA looks backward: they record what happened. RCA looks forward: we restructure what's about to happen so the IRS sees a smaller number.

Most of our clients keep their existing CPA. We coordinate with them directly. Adding RCA doesn't replace your CPA — it gives your CPA a strategy to file.

 

Why This Year Matters

Tax strategy isn't something you implement after December 31. Most of the strategies we discussed have hard calendar deadlines. An S-Corp election requires action before the end of your tax year. A defined benefit plan must be established before year-end to qualify for this year's deductions. Cost segregation studies need to be commissioned before the filing deadline.

Waiting until next year doesn't delay the decision — it costs you a full year of savings. At the ranges we discussed, that's a six-figure decision that resets with the calendar.
