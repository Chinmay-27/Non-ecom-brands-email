The mistake that slows almost every buyer down

Most people start by browsing homes. Zillow, Redfin, long Sunday drives through neighborhoods they love. Makes total sense — that's the fun part.

But in Atlanta's market, that habit bites you. Here's why: the best homes — the ones in the neighborhoods you actually want — are going under contract fast. Sometimes in a weekend. And sellers' agents won't take an offer seriously from a buyer who isn't pre-approved.

Pre-approval isn't the same as pre-qualification. Pre-qualification is a quick estimate based on numbers you gave a lender. Pre-approval means a lender has actually verified your income, credit, and assets — and they've told you in writing what you can borrow. It takes a few days to set up, and it changes everything.

"The first thing I do with any buyer is connect them with a lender I trust before we ever set foot in a house. Not because it's a rule — because it makes their offer competitive the moment something great hits the market."

— Aaron Moen, REALTOR®

The market isn't the problem — the process is

Here's the thing I see over and over: people feel overwhelmed by the Atlanta market, but it's not really the market that's overwhelming. It's the process — because nobody ever laid it out plainly.

Once you know the order of operations — lender first, neighborhoods second, offer strategy third — the whole thing gets a lot quieter in your head. The market isn't waiting to trick you. It just moves fast, and you want a guide who knows the route.
