# Benchmarks & Sources — Non-Ecom Email

Use these for client pitches, goal-setting, and the "why automation" argument. **Every number is cited with source + year.** Read the caveats first — several widely-quoted stats are inflated, stale, or ecommerce-only.

---

## ⚠️ Read this first: open rates are broken

**Apple Mail Privacy Protection (MPP)** auto-loads tracking pixels, so an "open" fires whether or not a human reads the email. Apple Mail is ~58% of global opens, so this skews almost everything.

- A **2024 Validity** study found Apple-Mail-dominant senders saw reported opens **18–32 percentage points above verified engagement**; opens have roughly **doubled** vs. pre-MPP. — [Validity, 2024](https://www.validity.com/blog/case-closed-the-mystery-of-declining-email-open-rates/) · [Paubox](https://www.paubox.com/blog/how-apple-mail-privacy-protection-inflates-email-open-rates) · [Mailchimp MPP FAQ](https://mailchimp.com/help/apple-privacy-faq/)

**→ Optimize for clicks (CTR), click-to-open (CTOR), replies, meetings booked, activations, and conversions.** Clicks are unaffected by MPP. Treat open rate as a rough deliverability canary only.

## The automation argument (triggered flows vs. broadcasts)

This is the core pitch for building always-on lifecycle flows instead of blasts:

- **GetResponse (2023):** Triggered emails **45.38% open / 5.02% CTR** vs. Newsletters 40.08% / 3.84% → **+31% relative click lift**. Autoresponders **51.05% open / 5.59% CTR** → **+46% relative click lift**. — [GetResponse](https://www.getresponse.com/resources/reports/email-marketing-benchmarks)
- **General consensus:** segmented/behavior-triggered emails outperform broadcasts by **~2–3×**; automated campaigns can generate **~320% more revenue** than non-automated.
- **Safest non-ecom assumption:** plan for a **~30–50% click lift** from automation. The 2–3× / 320% figures lean on ecommerce flow data.

**⚠️ Do NOT quote to non-ecom clients:** "flows = ~5% of sends but ~41% of email revenue" and "abandoned cart = highest ROI" are **Klaviyo/ecommerce** stats that depend on cart data non-ecom brands don't have. Use only as directional proof that automation beats broadcast.

**ROI:** email broadly returns **~$36–$42 per $1** spent. — multiple benchmark roundups.

## Case studies

**1. TAB (B2B doc management) — lead nurture.** 5 emails, 10 days apart over 50 days, mapped to buyer-journey stages. Vs. their newsletter: **+133% open, +650% CTR, +32.6% conversion** (content downloads), plus lead scoring to focus sales. — [MarketingSherpa](https://www.marketingsherpa.com/article/case-study/pilot-campaign-increases-conversion-326)

**2. Dropbox (SaaS) — referral/advocacy.** Two-sided referral (500MB free for both sides) embedded in onboarding, the welcome email, the checklist, and storage-limit moments. **3,900% growth in 15 months (100K → 4M users)**; ~2.8M invites in April 2010. *Caveat: ~1/3 of signups were already word-of-mouth — the program amplified existing momentum.* — [Viral Loops](https://viral-loops.com/blog/dropbox-grew-3900-simple-referral-program/) · [GrowSurf](https://growsurf.com/blog/dropbox-referral-program/)

**3. Voyo Croatia (subscription streaming) — win-back.** Personalized reactivation by favorite genre, then a top-10 picks follow-up. **38% open, 2.53% CTR, contributing to 500% ROI in six months.** — [Bloomreach](https://www.bloomreach.com/en/use-cases/win-back-campaign)

## Conflicting data — how to handle it
- **Open-rate disagreement** (e.g., nonprofit 40% Mailchimp vs. 52–54% GetResponse/Sona): different platforms/audiences/MPP handling. Treat all opens as directional.
- **SaaS opens vary by email type:** marketing ~23–30% vs. outbound sequences ~35–45%. Don't compare across types.
- **Automation lift ranges 31% → 2–3× → 320%:** higher figures are ecom; use ~30–50% click lift as the conservative non-ecom planning number.

*Research compiled June 2026. Re-verify benchmark figures before each client pitch — email benchmarks shift annually and MPP continues to distort open data.*
